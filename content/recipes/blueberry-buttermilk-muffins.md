instructions:
- "Warmed oven to 425°F. Lined muffin pan with paper liners sprayed lightly with non-stick for easy release. High heat jumpstarts muffin dome but watch closely."

- "Using stand mixer, creamed softened butter and brown sugar till light and fluffy. Adds air, critical for crumb structure. Added buttermilk (or yogurt), oil, honey, eggs, and vanilla gradually. Batter will be liquidy, not thick. Don’t freak."
- "Reduced speed to medium, incorporated dry mix slowly. Too fast or overmix and gluten kicks in, muffins get tough. Stopped mixing when just combined. Folded blueberries gently with rubber spatula, trying not to bruise berries or turn batter purple."
- "Spooned batter into muffin cups using quarter-cup measure for even sizing. Mixed cinnamon and sugar, sprinkled on top for crisp, fragrant crust."
- "Dropped oven temp from 425°F to 350°F immediately after loading. That blast of heat helps muffin start rising fast but then lower heat ensures even cooking inside without too dark tops."
- "Baked 15 to 20 minutes, checking visually. Tops golden and edges pulling away from liners means done. Inserted toothpick; clean or with a few moist crumbs, perfect. Overbaking dries crumb, underbaking leaves raw pockets."
- "Let muffins cool completely on rack before digging in. Fragile crumb, moist pockets. Scent of cinnamon and maple-honey lingers."
