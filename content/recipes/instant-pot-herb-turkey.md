ingredients:
- "1 turkey breast (remove any gravy packet)"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil, divided"
- "2 tablespoons butter"
- "1 teaspoon dried sage"
- "1 teaspoon dried rosemary"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin (substitute for paprika)"
- "1 teaspoon seasoned salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "2 stalks celery, chopped"
- "1 medium onion, quartered"
- "3 garlic cloves, smashed"
- "2 cups water"
instructions:
- "Strip any gravy packet from turkey breast, discard. Paper towel dry thoroughly. Moist skin ruins crust."
- "Mix sage, rosemary, thyme, cumin, seasoned salt, and black pepper in bowl. Cumin adds earthiness here; paprika was dull in past tries."
- "Drizzle about 1 tablespoon olive oil evenly over turkey. Rub well to coat; no need for exact measurement, just slick skin."
- "Sprinkle herb-spice mix over skin side first, pat to embed. Flip, repeat; multiple sprinkles build layers. Don’t rush rubbing, it sticks better."
- "Heat Instant Pot to sauté. Add 1 tablespoon olive oil plus 2 tablespoons butter. Melt, listen for sizzle popping."
- "Sear turkey on all sides until golden brown, about 5-6 minutes each face. Look for caramelized skin, deep color. Remove and rest on plate."
- "Turn off sauté mode. Dump chopped celery, onion, and smashed garlic into pot bottom. These produce aromatics steam will draw up."
- "Place wire trivet inside pot over veggies; handles must be accessible."
- "Slowly pour 2 cups water around trivet base. Avoid splashing on hot oil – careful, might spit."
- "Set turkey breast on trivet, breast side up. Fits snugly; if too big, trim excess skin or fat carefully."
- "Lock lid, close valve for sealing. Set manual pressure for 30 to 38 minutes depending on weight—listen for pressure rush, steady burn rate."
- "Let pressure release naturally at least 12-18 minutes. No quick release; abrupt steam pull dries meat inside out."
- "Open lid carefully. Lift turkey out by trivet handles to cutting board. Tent loosely with foil; resting seals juices."
- "Slice against grain once rested 10 minutes minimum. Juices redistribute if sliced too soon, dry slices result."
