ingredients:
- "1 ½ cups all-purpose flour"
- "¾ cup unsalted butter softened"
- "⅔ cup light brown sugar packed"
- "⅓ cup granulated sugar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "2 tablespoons heavy cream"
- "¼ teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 310°F not higher or flour scorches fast."
- "Spread flour evenly on parchment-lined baking sheet so it’s a thin, uniform layer. Bake 6 minutes. Smell changes, faint toasted nut aroma signals done. Don’t let turn brown or bitter. Immediate cooling crucial, or heat carries on, ruining texture."
- "Beat butter and sugars with vanilla on medium-high. Thick, fluffy texture formed after 2-3 minutes. Mix until you see pale creaminess. If butter too cold, blend becomes lumpy. Room temperature butter is best; I learned that after several fails."
- "Drop mixer speed to low, drizzle in heavy cream. Cream cushions mixture, making it silky but stable. No rush on this or mix ends up broken or greasy."
- "Add flour half a cup at a time with salt sprinkled in. Stop when flour disappears and dough pulls together without dry streaks. Don’t overmix or dough toughens. Texture feels velvety but stable under wooden spoon pressure."
- "Fold in chocolate chips by hand until they scatter uniformly. Wood spoon works better for control, prevents breaking chips. Leftover clumps mean uneven bites."
- "Chill dough wrapped for 25 to 35 minutes. Firm enough to scoop, soft enough to spread. Over chilling makes it too stiff, lose that edible softness."
- "Keep dough covered or it picks fridge odors and dries out surface."
- "If no heavy cream, milk or half-and-half works but leads to slightly thinner dough."
- "Butter can be replaced with coconut oil, but flavor shifts and dough firm texture changes. Try blending ¾ oil and ¼ butter to balance."
