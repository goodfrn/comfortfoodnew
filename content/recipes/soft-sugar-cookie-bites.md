ingredients:
- "2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour"
- "¾ cup granulated sugar"
- "½ teaspoon fine salt"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "12 tablespoons unsalted butter cold, cut into pieces"
- "1 large egg plus 1 egg yolk"
- "1 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "¼ teaspoon almond extract optional; use lemon extract alternative"
- "1-2 tablespoons whole milk splash if dough too dry"
instructions:
- "=== Food processor method ==="
- "1 Combine flour, sugar, salt, and baking powder in food processor bowl. Pulse a few times just to mix evenly."
- "2 Toss cold butter cubes in. Blitz briefly until mixture looks sandy with pea-size butter bits. That’s key for tender flakes, not paste."
- "3 Add egg, yolk, vanilla, and optional almond or lemon extract. Pulse in short bursts until the dough just starts to clump up but isn’t overworked. Add a splash of milk if it seems crumbly or won’t hold."
- "=== Stand mixer method ==="
- "4 Mix powdered sugar (or granulated if preferred) with salt briefly in bowl."
- "5 Switch to paddle attachment. Add butter pieces cold. Beat on low then medium speed. Watch for butter and sugar turning creamy without melting. Scrape bowl sides often."
- "6 Toss in egg, yolk, vanilla, and optional almond/lemon extract. Mix until combined."
- "7 Fold in flour and baking powder gently. Use slow speed or hand fold. Just until no dry flour patches remain. Add splash milk if dry."
- "=== Common steps ==="
- "8 Divide dough in half. If rough, gentle knead on floured surface just to bring together, no overworking or it toughens."
- "9 Sandwich each half between two parchment sheets. Roll to about ½ inch thickness. Why thick? Cookies puff nicely and stay tender. Thin means hard edges."
- "10 Freeze both packs 15-25 minutes until firm but not frozen rock solid. Helps cleaner cuts."
- "11 Using pizza cutter or sharp knife, slice into squares or rectangles. Don’t stress precise sizes; re-roll scraps and chill again if dough warms."
- "12 Bake on parchment-lined sheet at 345-350°F. Watch edges: golden but centers still pale; poke lightly if unsure. Overbake dries out chewiness."
- "13 Let cool fully before topping."
- "=== Frosting and decorating ==="
- "14 Whip up buttercream. I like keeping one batch white, dye two more holiday red and green using gel colors — less runny, richer hues."
- "15 Spread or pipe a dollop on each. Sprinkle festive decorations while frosting still tacky. Crunch of sprinkles, color pop makes these irresistible."
- "16 Store cooled decorated bites in airtight container. They keep softness for days, fridge not necessary unless hot weather."
