ingredients:
- "1 cup corn syrup"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "0.25 cup water"
- "2 cups raw peanuts (or chopped walnuts for twist)"
- "0.5 cup unsalted butter"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "0.5 tsp baking soda"
instructions:
- "GREASE a large sheet of aluminum foil using unsalted butter or a nonstick spray. Set aside ready for the molten mess."
- "IN a large cast iron skillet over medium heat, combine corn syrup, sugar, and water. Stir to dissolve sugar blurred into syrupy mix. You want no grit."
- "WATCH for bubbles, foam forming soft ball stage around 230-240°F. Don’t rely only on the thermometer; softness test in cold water helps too."
- "ADD peanuts now and stir CONSTANTLY. Seriously. If you stop or slow, peanuts burn or clump like rocks. Hear their tiny pops? That’s roasting."
- "KEEP cooking and stirring. The mix darkens, a light smoke curls gently. Approaching hard crack stage near 295-305°F is key. A moment too late and scorched bitterness taints the batch."
- "REMOVE from heat IMMEDIATELY at 300°F mark. Heat robs sweetness fast after."
- "QUICKLY add butter chunks, vanilla, and baking soda. The soda fizzes and swells the mix, lightens color. Don’t freak. Stir fast to blend air but don’t overwork or it collapses."
- "POUR molten caramel nut mixture onto prepped foil. Spread quickly, thinly using a wet spatula or buttered knife. Thickness controls bite; thinner snaps faster."
- "LET cool at room temperature until fully hardened. Avoid fridge; condensation ruins the brittle crunch."
- "BREAK into uneven, rough pieces with hands or back of a spoon. Enjoy sticky, nutty rewards."
