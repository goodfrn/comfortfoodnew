ingredients:
- "1 loaf sturdy white bread, cut into 1-inch cubes"
- "4 large eggs"
- "3 cups whole milk (or 2 ½ cups milk plus ½ cup cream for extra richness)"
- "½ cup granulated sugar (reduce to ⅓ cup if very sweet bread)"
- "½ cup dark brown sugar (sub maple syrup for unique flavor)"
- "1 ½ teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon"
- "¼ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg"
- "5 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and divided"
- "FOR THE CARAMEL SAUCE"
- "¾ cup packed light brown sugar"
- "¾ cup heavy cream (sub full-fat coconut milk if dairy-free)"
- "5 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "¾ teaspoon vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Coat a 9 x 13 baking dish lightly with nonstick spray or butter; prevents sticking, crucial for cleanup."
- "Toss the bread cubes evenly in the dish; loaf choice matters here — day-old or slightly stale bread absorbs custard better and avoids sogginess."
- "In a mixing bowl, beat eggs vigorously; add milk, sugars, vanilla, cinnamon, and nutmeg next. Whisk to fully combine—don’t under-mix or you’ll have pockets of flavor that aren’t evenly distributed."
- "Pour custard over bread cubes. Press gently with a spatula to push bread down, letting it soak completely—ideal soaking time varies, but I let it rest 12 minutes for maximum pellucid custard absorption."
- "Drizzle 5 tablespoons melted butter over soaked bread surface. This extra fat creates crispy edges and buttery richness; skip or reduce if worried about calories but flavor diminishes."
- "Preheat oven to 325°F instead of higher baking heat; slower bake means custard sets without cracking, edges turn golden but not burnt. Bake uncovered for 50 to 55 minutes, or until crust is deeply golden and center just set but still jiggles slightly if nudged—overbaking dries pudding."
- "While baking, create caramel sauce: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine brown sugar, heavy cream, and butter. Stir intermittently. Listen to bubbling sounds; when sauce thickens and darkens slightly (5 to 7 minutes), remove from heat."
- "Immediately whisk in ¾ teaspoon vanilla. Taste here. Sauce should be luscious, balanced sweet and buttery—not grainy or burnt."
- "Once pudding’s out, pour caramel evenly on top; sauce will glisten and soak in immediately with steamy warmth. Serve warm—cooling dulls textures; if you want leftovers, refrigerate and reheat gently with splash of cream."
- "For variations, swap cinnamon with cardamom or add orange zest for brightness. Toss in chopped nuts or dried fruit. Using brioche or challah bread ups richness but adjust soaking time to prevent mushiness."
- "Common pitfall: pouring custard unevenly leads to dense pockets; patience to soak fully is essential. If crust browns too fast, shield loosely with foil halfway through baking."
- "Storage tip: keeps 3 days refrigerated. Can freeze but tends to get soggy once thawed; better reheated from fridge with caramel on side."
