- "1 ripe kiwi peeled and cut into chunks"
- "1 cup fresh strawberries hulled"
- "½ cup plain Greek yogurt (substitutes milk)"
- "¾ cup coconut water (instead of milk)"
- "1 tablespoon honey or agave syrup optional for sweetness"
- "½ teaspoon fresh lemon juice for brightness"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Cold tools ready? Grab large, high-powered blender. Drop in chopped kiwi first. Chunk size matters here to avoid fibrous clumps."
- "Add hulled strawberries. Ripe fruits yield better aroma and color. Don't skip—the sweetness-tart balance critical here."
- "Spoon in Greek yogurt. Yes, switch it up from milk for creaminess, protein boost, thicker texture. Coconut water next, pour slowly."
- "Drizzle honey or agave if your fruit's too tart or not ripe enough. Zing? Lemon juice sneaks in for just a touch of acid to brighten flavor."
- "Seal lid tight, crank speed to high. Blend. You'll hear the motor whine, catch fragments swirling, smells intensify."
- "Blend roughly 1:15 to 1:30 minutes. Watch for uniform color with speckled seeds dispersed evenly. Stop when texture thick but pourable."
- "Too dense? Add splash more coconut water sparingly—blend another 15 seconds or until consistency fits your mood."
- "Pour into a chilled glass. Garnish with small kiwi and strawberry wedges for a pop, contrast in texture and color."
- "Taste test—if tasting flat, a pinch more lemon juice or a little sweetener fixes it. This smoothness needs balance."
- "Enjoy immediately. Leftover smoothie will separate, shake or stir before next sip."
