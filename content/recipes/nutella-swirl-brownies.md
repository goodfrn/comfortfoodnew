Set the oven to 345. Line a 9x9 pan. Spray it like you mean it. Nutella doesn't stick the way normal chocolate does — it pools at the edges and fuses everything together if you're not careful.

This is the easiest chocolate brownies recipe that actually tastes like something. Takes 52 minutes total. 12 minutes prep, 40 minutes bake. The swirl isn't decoration. It's half the brownie.

## Why You'll Love These Nutella Brownies

No mixer needed. Dump Nutella, oil, sugar, eggs in a bowl and whisk until it's shiny. Takes maybe two minutes.
The texture stays fudgy. Not cakey. Not dense. That's the hazelnut spread working — it keeps things soft even after cooling.
Swirling Nutella on top creates these gooey pockets that taste way better than mixed-through. Trust it.
Cleanup is almost nothing. One bowl. One pan. Done.
Homemade but tastes like you bought it. Honestly better. Way less waxy.

## What You Need for Hazelnut Brownies

Two cups of Nutella. That's the whole point. Nutella, not generic hazelnut spread.

A third cup of neutral oil. Vegetable works. Light olive oil works. Melted coconut oil works too — adds a whisper of coconut but doesn't hurt anything.

Half cup sugar. Less than a typical brownie. The Nutella is already sweet. Trust it.

Three eggs at room temperature. Cold eggs don't mix in as smoothly. Leave them out for ten minutes if you remember.

One cup flour. All-purpose. Nothing fancy.

One and a half teaspoons baking powder. That extra half teaspoon keeps these from being totally dense.

Fine sea salt. Just a pinch. Punches the chocolate forward.

## How to Make Nutella Brownies

Preheat to 345. Not 350. Five degrees lower saves the edges from setting while the center's still wet. Line the 9x9 with parchment or foil. Spray the whole thing generously. Edges stick otherwise.

Combine Nutella, oil, sugar, eggs in a bowl. Whisk until glossy and shiny. No streaks. This takes about two minutes of actual whisking. Use a rubber spatula to scrape the sides halfway through so everything gets incorporated.

Fold in the flour, baking powder, salt. Gently. The moment you can't see flour patches, stop. Overmixing makes them tough and cakey. That's the mistake.

Pour the batter into the pan. It's thick. Spreads slowly. Smooth the top with a spatula but don't fuss with it. Keep the touch light.

Warm a quarter cup of extra Nutella in the microwave for about 15 seconds. Watch it. It should shimmer and loosen up, not boil or get hot. Drop teaspoons of it across the batter. Then drag a knife through in swirls. Back and forth. Don't overdo it. The less you swirl the more distinct the pockets stay.

## How to Get Nutella Brownies Fudgy and Perfect

Bake 32 to 40 minutes. Check at 32. The edges should pull away from the sides slightly and feel set when you touch them gently. The center should jiggle when you nudge the pan — like, electrically jiggle — but not slosh. Press your finger near the center. It should feel fudgy. Barely set. Not runny but definitely not firm.

Too brown at 35 minutes? Your oven runs hot. Lower the rack or reduce the temp to 340 next time.

The brownies firm up significantly as they cool. Room temperature is better than fridge. Cold brownies set too tight and cutting becomes a whole thing. Wait at least 15 minutes before cutting. The crust holds together better then.

Use a sharp knife. Wipe the blade after each cut. The swirl starts to look pale if you're not done baking — it should look dark and glossy. If the top still looks tan at 35 minutes, add another few minutes.

## Nutella Brownies Tips and Common Mistakes

Edges crumbling when you cut? Lower your oven temp next time or reduce the bake time by a minute or two. It's usually the edges setting too fast and getting brittle.

Pale swirl on top? Bake a little longer. The Nutella should turn dark chocolate brown, almost black in spots.

Brownies taste dry? You hit the center too long. They only need to be barely set in the middle. The jiggle matters.

Room temperature eggs matter less than you'd think but cold eggs take longer to whisk in. Do it if you think about it. Skip it if you don't.

The optional handful of toasted chopped hazelnuts adds crunch. Swirl in salted caramel instead if you want something different. Neither changes the bake time.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Easy Nutella Brownies

**Can you use a different oil?**
Vegetable, light olive, melted coconut — any of them work. Coconut oil adds a tiny hint of coconut flavor but it's barely noticeable. Avoid olive oil that tastes strongly. It fights the chocolate.

**What if your oven temperature is off?**
Brownies are forgiving about oven temp. If yours runs hot, start checking at 30 minutes instead of 32. If it runs cold, you might need 42 or 43. The jiggle test matters more than the clock.

**Can you make these in a bigger pan?**
You could but they'd be thinner and bake faster. Add maybe 5 minutes check time. Smaller pan makes them thicker and fudgier — which is better.

**Do these need to chill?**
No. Room temperature is actually the move. They slice cleaner, taste richer, stay fudgy. Refrigerator makes them too tight and the texture changes.

**Why use baking powder instead of baking soda?**
Baking powder gives a tiny bit of lift without the bitter aftertaste. Baking soda would work but it's sharper. These are sweet already.

**How long do homemade chocolate brownies keep?**
Three days in a container at room temperature. After that they start drying out. Freezer works fine for two weeks but the texture gets a little waxy when you thaw them.
