ingredients:
- "4 salmon fillets skin on"
- "kosher salt adjusted to taste"
- "freshly cracked black pepper to taste"
- "2 tbsp olive oil"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "8 oz cremini mushrooms sliced"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "2 shallots finely chopped"
- "1 tsp dried rosemary crushed"
- "1/4 cup dry vermouth"
- "3/4 cup heavy cream"
- "1 cup shredded aged parmesan cheese"
instructions:
- "Season salmon fillets liberally with kosher salt and cracked black pepper. Set aside while heating pan."
- "Heat large skillet with olive oil on medium-low, allowing oil to shimmer but not smoke."
- "Turn heat up to medium-high just before adding salmon, place skin-side down. Listen for sizzle; that’s the sound of crisping skin."
- "Cook until salmon fibers start to separate along thickest edge, about 3-5 minutes depending on thickness. Don’t juke the time—judge by texture and color change from translucent pink to opaque."
- "Flip gently with spatula, cook other side similarly until just shy of fully cooked—medium is the sweet spot. Avoid poking or pressing fish, it escapes juices fast."
- "Remove salmon from pan, transfer to plate. Skin can be peeled with gentle slide of a spoon or knife; some prefer to keep for crunch."
- "Add butter to hot skillet, reduce to low heat. Melt slowly. Watch it foam then settle, that’s your cue."
- "Toss in sliced cremini mushrooms, garlic and shallots. Stir occasionally; mushrooms will buckle and shed moisture. Wait until shallots soften, translucent—not browned yet."
- "Sprinkle crushed rosemary over mixture, swish in dry vermouth. Flames risk here, so keep pan low and simmer off alcohol."
- "Add heavy cream, stir to mix. Cream builds body; let it bubble gently. Avoid rapid boil or cream will separate."
- "Sprinkle shredded aged parmesan into the sauce. Stir continuously until melted and sauce thickens. Shift consistency by adding a splash more cream or letting simmer longer."
- "Nestle salmon fillets back in skillet, spoon sauce over. Low heat, 1-2 minutes—just to warm through and marry flavors without drying fish."
- "Serve, skin on or off. Garnish optional but fresh herbs or lemon zest brighten richness."
