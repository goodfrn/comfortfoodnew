ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (thyme, rosemary, or chives work great)"
- "1 teaspoon granulated garlic powder (substitute: minced fresh garlic for more punch)"
- "1 cup whole milk (can swap for buttermilk or plant milk like oat for twist)"
- "1 ½ cups shredded sharp white cheddar cheese (swap half for asiago or aged gouda)"
- "¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese"
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil (light olive oil or melted butter if richer)"
- "melted butter for brushing"
instructions:
- "Whisk dry ingredients together: flour, baking powder, salt, herbs, garlic powder. Avoid lumps. Herb distribution critical—don’t clump or the flavor falters."
- "Add milk, cheeses, and oil. Stir gently just until combined. Don’t overwork the dough or biscuits get tough. Lumps okay, better than glue-like uniformity."
- "Use two spoons to dollop dough onto a lined baking sheet (parchment or silicone mat). Aim for 12 even mounds spaced with room to puff."
- "Bake at 395°F (lowered slightly for gentler rise) for about 18 minutes. Look for tops turning golden with some cracks appearing, biscuit edges firm but springy. Smell deepening cheesy-herbal."
- "Right out of oven, brush each biscuit’s top with melted butter to add gloss and soften crust. Serve warm, flaking apart reveals tender, cheesy pockets within."
- "If sideways and underbaked, pop back in for a few minutes watching closely; overbaking dries them out."
- "Store leftovers wrapped airtight. Toast to refresh crust and warm inside."
