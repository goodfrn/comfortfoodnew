ingredients:
- "1 pork tenderloin (about 1 to 1.2 lbs)"
- "1 tbsp olive oil"
- "3 tsp garlic powder"
- "2 tsp kosher salt"
- "1 tsp black pepper"
- "2 tsp dried Italian seasoning"
- "1 tsp onion powder"
- "3 tbsp butter, cut into 3 pieces"
- "Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 410F and prepare a baking sheet with foil; set aside."
- "In medium bowl, blend garlic powder, salt, pepper, dried Italian seasoning, onion powder until combined."
- "Dry pork tenderloin thoroughly with paper towels; moisture ruins crust."
- "Coat tenderloin all around with seasoning blend, rub in deeply with fingertips."
- "Heat olive oil in heavy skillet over medium heat until it shimmers and lightly smokes."
- "Add seasoned pork; sear each side and ends until dark golden crust forms (about 3-4 min per side). Resist moving too much; you want that Maillard reaction."
- "Transfer pork directly to foil-lined sheet. Place three butter chunks spaced evenly on top."
- "Wrap pork loosely but sealed in foil, tenting over the top to trap butter melt and steam."
- "Roast in oven about 18-25 minutes or until internal temp hits 143-158F. Check at 18 min to avoid overcooking. Use instant read thermometer into thickest part. Timing varies by tenderloin thickness."
- "Remove from oven, sprinkle fresh parsley over top for brightness."
- "Tent again loosely with foil; rest 5 min to let juices redistribute. Don’t skip resting or pork dries out."
- "Slice against grain for tender bites."
