ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 medium yellow onion finely chopped"
- "4 cups fresh corn kernels or well-drained frozen"
- "2 teaspoons honey"
- "½ teaspoon kosher salt"
- "⅓ cup toasted chopped pecans (optional twist)"
instructions:
- "Heat butter in a heavy skillet on medium-low; swirl to coat, letting butter melt fully without browning right away"
- "Toss in chopped onion; sweat gently, stirring often, until translucent and scents rise—about 5 minutes. No color yet, just softening"
- "Add corn kernels; maintain medium-low heat. Let corn sizzle quietly, stirring every 4-6 minutes. Watch for kernels to blister, edges brown slightly, and a faint sweet caramel aroma. This will take roughly 30–35 minutes. Slow and steady wins here"
- "When onions and corn show caramel spots and kernel skins wrinkle slightly but still snap, drizzle honey and sprinkle salt; stir well to coat evenly"
- "Toss in toasted pecans right before serving for a crunchy counterpoint; if nuts unavailable, no sweat—corn holds well solo"
- "Serve hot straight from skillet. Leftovers keep covered, reheat gently on stove, adding splash water if dry"

faq:

- "q: Can I use frozen corn? a: Yes but thaw fully, dry well or it steams. Wet kernels won’t caramelize right. Frozen taste is a bit different but good. Adjust cook time—sometimes shorter since kernels softer."
- "q: What if onions brown too fast? a: Drop heat. Splash small water to cool pan. Keep stirring so no bits burn. Onions want translucent softness, not full crisp. Adjust onion cut size for quicker softening or gentler cook."
- "q: How to store leftovers? a: Store covered fridge, use within 3 days best. Reheat on low stove with tiny water splash to avoid drying. Microwave too fast, kernels toughen. Leftovers lose some crisp but keep flavor."
