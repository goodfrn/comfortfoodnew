ingredients:
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "3/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon Herbes de Provence (swap for poultry seasoning)"
- "1 teaspoon chicken bouillon powder"
- "1 1/2 pounds chicken tenders"
- "1/2 cup all-purpose flour"
- "2 large eggs beaten"
- "12 saltine crackers finely crushed"
- "Vegetable oil for frying (about 2 to 3 inches deep)"
instructions:
- "Crush saltines finely. Food processor works best but rolling pin in bag is fine too."
- "Mix salt, pepper, garlic powder, Herbes de Provence, and bouillon in a small bowl. Toss chicken tenders in this mix in a large bowl or zip-top bag. Let rest 20 minutes—don’t rush this or flavor stays shallow."
- "Set up dredging: flour in one dish, eggs beaten in second, crushed crackers in third. Keep close."
- "Dredge each tender in flour first, coating lightly but enough to help egg stick. Dip in eggs, let excess drip off—too wet makes coating soggy. Press into cracker crumbs firmly; crumbs must grip well or they fall off during frying."
- "Heat oil in deep Dutch oven. Use a thermometer: target 365°F but can range +/- 5 °F. Test oil temp with a small scrap of cracker—should bubble vigorously but not smoke."
- "Cook tenders in batches, 3 or 4 at once to keep oil from cooling too much. Fry about 4 minutes per batch, flip once if needed. Watch for golden-brown crust and internal temp hitting 165°F. Thickness affects timing."
- "Drain cooked tenders on cooling rack over pan to avoid soggy bottoms. Keep warm in low oven around 200°F if serving later."
- "Adjust oil temp as needed between batches. Too hot—burn crust, undercooked inside. Too cool—greasy, limp crust."
- "Serve immediately with favorite dipping sauce or slaw."
