ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 cup whole wheat flour"
- "1 tablespoon sugar"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon salt"
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "1/2 cup buttermilk"
- "1/2 cup whole milk"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "1/2 cup shredded pepper jack cheese"
- "1/2 cup cooked bacon diced"
- "1 small jalapeno finely diced optional"
- "Topping mix: 4 oz cream cheese softened"
- "1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley"
- "1 teaspoon honey"
- "Pinch of salt"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 420F. Lower than usual to avoid burnt bottoms which happened before."
- "Grab sheet, line with parchment or a silicone mat so edges don’t burn."
- "Whisk all flours, sugar, baking powder, garlic powder, salt in big bowl. Important to mix dry first for even rising."
- "Melt butter but hold off adding all at once. Save a tablespoon for topping."
- "Stir melted butter, buttermilk, and milk into dry mix until just combined. Slightly sticky dough—don’t overwork or biscuits get tough."
- "Fold in chives, shredded cheeses, bacon, jalapeno (or skip) carefully. Distribution matters for every bite."
- "Scoop dough with 1/4 cup scoop—makes uniform biscuits—space about 1.25 inches apart; they expand visually, no crowding."
- "Bake 16-18 minutes watching for golden tops and puffed-up cracks if edges start too brown, drop heat by 10F."
- "While baking, mix cream cheese, parsley, honey, salt until spreadable. Not runny; texture should be creamy yet thick."
- "Pull biscuits from oven, dollop topping on warm biscuits immediately—cream cheese melts into nooks, gives creamy contrast."
- "True cooling—let rest 10 minutes on warm tray. Internal steam finishes cooking, biscuit texture firms up with a slight crust on bottom."
- "Eat warm or store airtight. Reheat uncovered briefly to bring crispness back. Leftover dough freezes well, freeze on tray then bag."
- "Sticky dough fixes: add flour tablespoon at a time. Too dry? Splash more buttermilk, mix gently."
- "Swapping cheeses? Use any sharp melting cheese but avoid watery fresh cheeses or flavor dilutes."
- "If no buttermilk, milk + 1 tbsp vinegar or lemon juice for 5 minutes works fine, acid reacts with baking powder for lift."
