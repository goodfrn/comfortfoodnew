instructions:
- "=== Melt Mix ==="
- "In a microwavable container, combine almond bark and hazelnut spread. Heat for 50 seconds, checking every 20 seconds to stir and break lumps. Stop heating when mixture is smooth, shiny, and just fluid enough to spread — no graininess or too hot spots. You want melting sounds and soft bubbling but no scorching or separation. Use gentle stirs to keep heat distributed evenly."
- "=== Assemble Faces ==="
- "Spread about a tablespoon of the melted mixture on each rice cake, thick enough to hold decorations but not dripping off edges. Work swift; when coating gets tacky, it won’t hold things well."
- "Place two mini pretzels near the top of each cake for ears. Press lightly but don’t puncture the rice cake surface."
- "Position candy eyes below pretzels, spacing wide for expression."
- "Add one dried cranberry at lower center for a bright red nose. Alternate: use a small berry or even candy-coated chocolate for different punch."
- "Let sit at room temperature to firm up. If impatient, pop in fridge for 3-5 minutes; check often to avoid cold sweat forming. Avoid acid-based nitpicking on rice cakes which may get soggy if left too long."
- "Snick crunch meets creamy hazelnut, sweet almond bark in every bite. Watch out for melty hands if too warm."
- "Imperfect shapes add charm. Pretzels crack or break? Use small breadsticks or cereal sticks easily swapped. No candy eyes? Use small dots of darker chocolate or tiny icing dots piped."
- "Coating can be thickened by chilling for a minute before spreading to avoid sliding or falling apart. Keep an eye on mixing temperature to avoid butterfat separation in hazelnut spread."
