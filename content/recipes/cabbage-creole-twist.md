ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons butter"
- "1 medium green bell pepper diced"
- "1 small zucchini diced"
- "1 medium cabbage head shredded"
- "2 medium fresh tomatoes chopped"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons salt"
- "3/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
instructions:
- "====Step 1===="
- "Melt butter over medium heat in a wide skillet or Dutch oven. Should foam lightly but not brown. The butter is your flavor base here; don’t rush the melting stage or burn it, or bitterness creeps in."
- "====Step 2===="
- "Add bell pepper and zucchini next. Zucchini replaces the usual onion for a milder, earthier note. Let them sweat, stirring often, about 4 to 5 minutes until soft but still with some bite. Listen for gentle sizzling. If it’s roaring, the heat’s too high."
- "====Step 3===="
- "Toss in the shredded cabbage in handfuls. Stir to coat with butter and catch those lovely little crackles as cabbage hits the heat. Add the chopped tomatoes after you’ve mixed cabbage thoroughly. Cook until cabbage is tender but still slightly crisp and juicy; usually around 15 minutes. No mush here. Look for a slight translucence and gentle collapse but not total breakdown."
- "====Step 4===="
- "Season gently with salt and pepper near the end. Salt too early can draw too much water out–watch texture carefully. Stir well to distribute seasoning evenly. Let flavors marry a few minutes off the direct heat."
- "====Step 5===="
- "Taste. Adjust salt, pepper. Maybe a pinch of cayenne if you’re feeling bold. Serve immediately with something robust enough to stand up to its boldness — maybe grits or crusty bread."
- "Common signals to watch: the smell transforming from sharp raw veggies to sweeter sautéed notes, the slight wilt of cabbage leaves, and no excessive liquid pooling. If it looks soggy, turn up heat briefly or drain excess moisture next time."

faq:
- "q: Can I use onion instead of zucchini? a: Yes, but onion sharpens flavor and changes cooking time. Zucchini softer, slower moisture release. Swap means watch for sogginess and reduce cooking time a little. Onion chars faster, cook carefully."
- "q: What if my cabbage gets soggy? a: Turn heat up fast, stir out excess water, drain if needed. Soggy means too low heat or overcrowding. Use shredded cabbage finer for speed or bigger chunks for crunch. Experiment with batch cooking, keep moving pan."
- "q: How to store leftovers? a: Refrigerate sealed container. Reheat gently in skillet, add fresh butter or a dash of water. Avoid microwave if possible—it dumps texture fast. Good for 2-3 days, best fresh though. Can freeze but lose some crunch, thaw slowly."
- "q: Substitute for bell pepper? a: Mild poblano or green chili works if no bell pepper. Watch spice levels. Also try sweet green chili or even chopped celery for crunch. Flavor shifts with each, so season accordingly. Keep an eye on moisture differences too."
