ingredients:
- "1 cup mashed cooked sweet potato"
- "1 cup packed brown sugar"
- "½ cup white sugar"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour"
- "2 large eggs"
- "¾ cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup toasted walnuts chopped"
- "turbinado sugar for sprinkling (optional)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 340°F. Prep a baking sheet with parchment or a silicone mat. Do not skip lining; cookies stick otherwise."
- "In a big mixing bowl, dump brown sugar, white sugar, baking soda, and sifted flour. Mix well with a whisk or fingers to avoid lumps."
- "Add mashed sweet potato, eggs, butter, vanilla, and nuts. Stir until just combined; dough will be sticky but firm enough to hold shape."
- "Scoop small portions with a cookie scoop. I use about one tablespoon size. Drop on prepared sheet spaced well, they spread a bit."
- "Optional: sprinkle extra chopped walnuts and a pinch of turbinado sugar on each ball. Adds crunch and shimmer; tactile contrast."
- "Bake 13-17 minutes. Watch for edges to firm and turn golden brown. Centers will look soft but not wet. Gives chewiness."
- "Cool on wire rack. Avoid stacking warm cookies or they steam and soften too much."
- "Store in airtight container at room temp for up to 3 days. Beyond, cookies lose chewy texture and become brittle."
