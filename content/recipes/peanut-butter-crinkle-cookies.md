ingredients:
- "1¾ cups plus 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "½ teaspoon baking soda"
- "½ teaspoon kosher salt"
- "½ cup creamy peanut butter (can swap with almond butter but expect flavor shift)"
- "½ cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "1 cup dark brown sugar, packed"
- "1 large egg"
- "1½ teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "½ cup granulated sugar"
- "½ cup powdered sugar, sifted"
- "15 miniature peanut butter cups, halved"
instructions:
- "Start by sifting all dry ingredients — flour, cocoa, baking soda, salt — into a medium bowl. Break clumps; no lumps allowed here. Cocoa can be dusty; a fine sieve a must."
- "Using a stand or handheld mixer at medium-high speed, beat peanut butter and butter together for a minute or so until creamy but not fluffy. Scrape sides once or twice. Overbeating starts incorporating air, not wanted."
- "Add brown sugar next, keep mixing at same speed, about a minute until fully integrated and the mixture thickens. Graininess from sugar should disappear."
- "Slow mixer to medium-low. Crack egg in. Beat just until fully combined — no visible yolk streaks. Overmixing here risks tough dough later."
- "Pour vanilla in, brief mix to blend. Done when no brownish streaks remain. Aromas merge here; smell test."
- "Add dry mix in three additions, gently folding after each addition until barely incorporated. Overworking develops gluten, makes hard cookies — no good. Dough's sticky but manageable."
- "Cover bowl with plastic wrap, chill in fridge for 25–35 minutes. Chilling firms dough, eases handling, prevents spreading in oven, giving that crinkle texture."
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment or lightly greased foil. Greasing foil is a must — no sticky disasters."
- "Put granulated sugar in shallow bowl, powdered sugar in another or a quart-sized sealable bag. Prepping sugar coatings in advance speeds rolling."
- "Scoop dough with tablespoon measure, roll into a ball. Slightly flatten. Press half a mini peanut butter cup in center, wrap dough edges over, sealing candy inside. Roll into smooth ball again. Arranging completed cookie balls on first baking sheet, spaced about 2 inches apart."
- "Roll each ball first in granulated sugar, then in powdered sugar until fully coated. The double coating creates crackled tops and subtle texture contrast."
- "Arrange coated balls on second prepared sheet, spaced well. Give them breathing room."
- "Bake 7–12 minutes — ovens vary. Watch crackling on surface and edges setting but not browning too fast. The tell: cracks open and smell rich peanut butter and chocolate mingling. Remove before edges darken heavily. Let rest on sheet 2–4 minutes before transferring to cooling rack. Hot cookies fragile, so gentle."
- "Serve when fully cooled; centers chewy with molten peanut butter cups inside — that contrast is everything."

tips:
- "Sift dry ingredients well to avoid lumps. Cocoa powder can cling, so push through a fine sieve. Incorporate baking soda and salt evenly for uniform rise. Clumps lead to uneven texture — cracks or dense spots show this quickly."
- "Mix peanut butter and butter until creamy but not fluffy. Overbeating traps air, changes crumb. Keep mixer speed moderate to avoid air pockets that can cause cracks or holes."
- "Brown sugar adds moisture and chewiness. Do not skip or replace with white sugar directly. Mix well till fully incorporated — graininess disappears, visibly smoother batter."
- "Add the egg slowly at medium-low mixer speed. Quick additions or high speed can cause emulsification breaking — grainy or curdled dough. Stop as soon as no yolk streaks remain."
- "Fold dry mix gently in three batches. Overworking dough develops gluten, makes tough cookies. Dough should stay sticky but manageable, not tough or rubbery after folding."
- "Chill dough 25 to 35 minutes before shaping. Firm dough resists spreading, holds peanut butter cups inside instead of leaking. Warmer dough leads to flat, oily cookies. Don’t skip chilling unless prepared for mess."
- "Roll cookie balls with half a peanut butter cup inside. Wrap edges carefully so candy fully enclosed; exposed pieces melt out and create oily spots on pan."
- "Roll balls twice in sugar — granulated first, then powdered. This double coating locks moisture during baking and creates crackly texture on surfaces. Skipping this changes look and chew."
- "Bake time varies 7 to 12 minutes. Look for cracks forming, edges setting but not browning too fast. Smell rich peanut butter and chocolate mingling as a key sign. Pull early rather than late to avoid dryness."
