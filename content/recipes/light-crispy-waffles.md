ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 3/4 cups milk (or buttermilk substitute: milk plus 1 teaspoon vinegar)"
- "1/2 cup melted butter (or neutral oil for lighter taste)"
- "2 large eggs separated"
instructions:
- "Separate eggs. Beat whites until foamy and soft peaks form. Not just frothy; air locked in but not stiff. In separate bowl whisk yolks briefly; set aside."
- "In a large bowl whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, salt. Add milk and melted fat. Stir till combined but still slightly lumpy. Overmix kills rise and texture."
- "Fold yolks into batter gently. Then fold egg whites in thirds — don’t rush. Air bubbles are fragile but crucial for crisp exterior."
- "Heat waffle iron thoroughly. Use butter or whatever fat you like to grease. Nonstick sprays can sometimes leave residue; butter browns better."
- "Spoon batter into hot iron but don’t overload. Close lid gently. Listen for that subtle hissing and steam tapering. Usually 3-4 minutes for my iron but depends."
- "When waffle surface turns golden brown and edges crisp with slight crackle, done. Use tactile check: waffles should release easily."
- "Remove carefully to wire rack not plate—avoids sogginess from steam. Eat hot. Flavor hits peak just out of iron."
- "If waffles stick, maybe iron’s temp is low or batter too wet. Try adjusting butter or drying batter a bit. If too dry, add splash milk."
- "Play with milk substitutes—almond milk thins batter and softens crispness, so watch cook time and batter thickness."
- "Crispier edges achieved by more butter in batter or slightly longer cook time, but watch burn risk."
- "Got no waffle iron? Use heavy skillet for quasi-waffles or griddled pancakes. Texture differs but tasty all the same."
