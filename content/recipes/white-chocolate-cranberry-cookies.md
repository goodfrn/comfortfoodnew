ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 1/4 cups packed light brown sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "1 1/2 cups old-fashioned rolled oats"
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 cup dried tart cherries chopped"
- "3/4 cup toasted pecans chopped"
- "1 1/2 cups white chocolate chunks"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 345°F. Line cookie sheet with parchment or silicone mat; no sticking, easy cleanup."
- "Cream butter and brown sugar until pale and fluffy. I do this low speed first, then medium to avoid dusty sugar clouds. Important: don’t rush; fluffy texture traps air for tender crumb."
- "Add eggs one by one, beating well after each. Follow with vanilla. The batter gets shiny, smells rich—sign you’re on track."
- "In separate bowl, whisk oats, flour, baking soda, and salt thoroughly. Uniform mix prevents hot spots or collapse in oven."
- "Add dry ingredients in two batches to wet, gently folding/mixing after each. Overmix and your cookies toughen; just combine for soft bite."
- "Fold in dried cherries, toasted pecans, and white chocolate. Don't skimp on nuts or fruit—they balance sweetness and add chew and crunch contrast."
- "Drop heaping tablespoonfuls spaced 3-4 inches apart on lined sheets. They spread. If dough sticky, chill 10 mins—but I find fresh softer cookies more rewarding."
- "Bake 10-11 minutes until edges turn golden amber and centers look set but soft, not gooey. Listen for slight crackling on hot tray; aroma sharpens here."
- "Remove, let cool on wire rack to finish firming without steaming. Warm cookies fall apart. Wire racks prevent soggy bottoms."
- "Store airtight once cooled for best texture; fridge dries them. Room temp preferred unless summer heat ruins the white chocolate chunks."
