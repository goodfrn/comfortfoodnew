ingredients:
- "2 cups mixed berries (blueberries, blackberries, quartered large strawberries)"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "2 tbsp cornstarch"
- "1 tbsp fresh lemon juice"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1 tbsp freeze-dried raspberries (crushed) - twist"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "2 tbsp granulated sugar"
- "1 1/2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/4 tsp salt"
- "5 tbsp cold unsalted butter (cut into pea sized pieces)"
- "3/4 cup buttermilk (or 3/4 cup milk plus 1 tsp vinegar or lemon juice for substitute)"
- "2 tbsp heavy cream (for brushing)"
- "1 tbsp coarse sugar (turbinado or demerara)"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 380°F. Slight bump for more caramelization yet careful not to scorch edges."
- "Spray a 2.5 to 3 quart ovenproof dish liberally with non-stick spray. Important for easy cleanup, cobbler juice sticks stubbornly."
- "Dice large strawberries into quarters. Toss all berries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and crushed freeze-dried raspberries in large bowl. Stir gently to avoid turning berries mushy. Pour mixture evenly into prepared dish—don’t overcrowd berries, so juices bubble freely."
- "Whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in another bowl. Add cold butter pieces. Use your fingers to press butter into flour until pea-sized lumps remain. Pastry cutter or forks work but fingers provide tactile control. Avoid overworking or butter will melt prematurely causing tough dough."
- "Slowly pour in buttermilk while stirring just until dry ingredients moistened. Batter should be sticky but scoopable. If too wet, flour sprinkle. If too dry, splash more buttermilk."
- "Scoop dough over berry layer in uneven dollops using large cookie scoop or spoon. Rustic look preferred—don’t flatten or smooth biscuit tops; thick edges brown better. Brush biscuit tops with heavy cream, then sprinkle coarse sugar generously for crunch and sparkle."
- "Bake for 38 to 43 minutes. Visual cues key: biscuit topping golden amber with crisp edges; thick bubbling juices pooling and crackling along pan edges. Listen for gentle crackle as fruit cooks down."
- "Cool 8 to 12 minutes. This rest lets juices thicken, topping firm but tender. Serve with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, melting into warm cobbler like a velvety sigh."
