ingredients:
- "3⁄4 cup yellow cornmeal (try coarse grind or polenta style)"
- "1⁄4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1⁄4 cup honey (instead of sugar; adds depth and a hint floral)"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "6 tablespoons melted coconut oil (swap for butter, melts with a subtle coconut hint)"
- "1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries (if frozen, no need to thaw)"
- "Extra few blueberries for topping"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 350°F. Spray an 8x8-inch pan with nonstick spray or grease well. I like coconut oil spray here, adds aroma."
- "Whisk cornmeal, flour, honey in medium bowl. Honey sticks sometimes so mix thoroughly to even distribution."
- "In separate, beat eggs till light. Whisk in milk and vanilla. Combine wet with dry — don’t overmix or cornmeal toughens. Just until combined, lumpy is fine."
- "Melt coconut oil until just liquid. Pour in and stir gently. This keeps crumb moist and lends subtle richness; butter works but coconut is a twist I picked up in last batch."
- "Fold blueberries in softly—vigorous folding bursts berries turning batter purple. Leaving spots of fresh juicy pockets. Tossing them lightly in flour beforehand helps prevent sinking but I skip that step."
- "Pour batter into pan. Scatter a few berries on top. Gives a pop of color and extra bursts as they caramelize on the surface."
- "Bake 30 to 38 minutes—check visual cues, not clock. Cornbread’s done when top is dry and golden with cracks that give under slight press but spring back. Insert toothpick or skewer; it should come out mostly clean, a few moist crumbs okay."
- "Oven sound changes too—listen for gentle sizzling receding after 25 minutes. Smell earthy sweetness and faintly toasty corn signals nearing end. Don’t wait till fully browned or it dries out."
- "Cool 10 minutes minimum. Cornbread tightens up smelling sweet, rich nutty with fleeting blueberry aroma. Moves best to wire rack for even cooling if you want crisp edges."
- "If pan is nonstick-challenged, line bottom with parchment for rescue. No fan or convection oven, rotate mid-bake for even color."
- "Crumb texture varies with cornmeal coarseness; finely ground tends dry quick, coarser adds rustic chew and moisture retention."
- "Got frozen berries? Add directly—batter thickens slightly; bake time may stretch by 3-5 minutes."
- "Sticky honey makes batter heavier; add with wet ingredients for smoother incorporation."
- "If no coconut oil, melted unsalted butter is welcome replacement; don’t burn it, just melted warm."
- "Hover not necessary. Trust eye and touch more than set timers—cornbread tells when it’s ready if you listen."
