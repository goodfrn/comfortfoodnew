ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1/2 cup natural peanut butter"
- "4 cups mini marshmallows"
- "5 cups corn flakes"
- "1/3 cup dried tart cherries"
- "1/3 cup sliced almonds"
- "1/4 cup mini dark chocolate chips"
instructions:
- "Spray a 9x13 or 5x7 pan with nonstick spray; choose smaller for thicker bars or larger for thinner ones."
- "Low-medium heat. Melt butter, peanut butter, and marshmallows in a saucepan. Stir consistently with wooden spoon. Watch marshmallows melt, become glossy and creamy, about 4-7 minutes. Do not rush or burn—slick texture and gentle bubbling indicate ready stage."
- "Remove from heat. Fold in corn flakes, dried cherries, and almonds. Mix just enough to coat but keep some crunch intact."
- "Press mixture firmly into pan. Use back of spoon or place parchment on top and press with your hands. Should have even surface but not overly compacted or it gets tough."
- "Sprinkle mini chocolate chips immediately on top so they stick but don’t melt completely. Let cool at room temperature until firm. Patience is key here."
- "Slice once cold completely. Bars hold better when fully set; warm will crumble or stick."
- "Store airtight; keeps texture longer in cool spots."

ingredientsNote: "Butter can be salted if that's all you have; reduce added salt elsewhere. Natural peanut butter holds flavor and prevents greasiness more than processed versions. Marshmallows — mini works best for quick melting; avoid jumbo chunks that take forever and cause uneven texture. Corn flakes are standard, but bran flakes or rice crisps swap nicely for deeper or lighter crunches. Tart cherries provide a tart pop; raisins or chopped apricots work well. Almonds can be sliced, slivered or chopped roughly — toasted for more aroma but not necessary. Chocolate chips on top are optional — even chopped dark chocolate shards or white chips add variety. Use nonstick spray that won’t leave residue — butter or canola oil sprays are my favorites. If allergies strike, swap almond to sunflower seeds and peanut butter to almond or soy butter."
instructionsNote: "Start by preparing your pan; choosing pan size affects bar thickness—thicker bars take longer to set, thinner bars set quicker but can dry out fast. Melt butter, peanut butter, and marshmallows at medium-low heat—no hot flames; too hot burns sugars in marshmallows, ruining gloss and flavor. After removing from heat, fold dry ingredients in gently to maintain crispness.
