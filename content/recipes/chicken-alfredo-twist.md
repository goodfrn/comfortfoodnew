ingredients:
- "12 ounces fettuccine noodles"
- "1 tablespoon dried oregano"
- "3/4 teaspoon salt, divided"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "3 boneless skinless chicken breasts, pounded to even thickness"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "4 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "1 1/2 cups freshly grated Parmesan cheese"
- "2 tablespoons fresh chopped parsley"
- "Optional twist: 1 teaspoon smoked paprika in chicken seasoning"
instructions:
- "Boil salted water vigorously for fettuccine until just tender but still a little bite. Drain thoroughly. Set aside, toss lightly with olive oil to keep strands separate."
- "In small bowl mix oregano, smoked paprika, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and black pepper. Press the seasoning on both sides of each chicken breast. Oregano swaps Italian seasoning for a rustic punch; paprika adds depth."
- "Heat olive oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high. Oil should shimmer but not smoke. Sear chicken breasts until golden with crisp edges, about 5-7 minutes per side depending on thickness. Internal temp 165°F. Rest on plate; juices redistribute so it’s juicy, not dry."
- "Turn heat to medium. Add butter—listen for gentle sizzle before garlic. Toss garlic, garlic powder, and remaining salt in and stir. Aroma should fill your kitchen within 45 seconds. Don’t brown garlic or bitter notes sneak in."
- "Sprinkle flour evenly, whisk rapidly to avoid lumps. Cook the roux for about 1 minute; no raw flour flavor here. It should smell slightly nutty, not floury."
- "Slowly drizzle milk and cream in while whisking nonstop to keep sauce velvety. No lumps. If lumps form, temper liquid slower or whisk aggressively."
- "Raise heat slightly to bring sauce to low boil—watch carefully. Slowly bubbles appear around edges and sauce thickens. Lower heat and keep whisking every 20 seconds. Takes about 3-4 minutes. If sauce sticks or burns, lower heat and add splash extra milk."
- "Remove pan from heat and fold in Parmesan cheese. Stir gently until melted. Sauce should shimmer, coat the back of a spoon thickly but pour slowly."
- "Add fettuccine to sauce, toss to coat noodles thoroughly. If sauce seems too thick, a splash of pasta water loosens it without watering down flavor."
- "Slice rested chicken thin against the grain—shows pink juices trapped inside. Lay strips atop pasta. Scatter parsley for fresh, bright contrast. Serve immediately before sauce thickens too much or noodles soak it up."
