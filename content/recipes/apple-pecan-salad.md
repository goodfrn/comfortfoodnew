ingredients:
- "1 stick unsalted butter"
- "3/8 cup packed dark brown sugar"
- "1/8 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 tablespoon water"
- "1 1/2 cups pecan halves"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/3 cup olive oil"
- "1/4 cup apple cider vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon white vinegar"
- "3/4 tablespoon fresh lime juice"
- "1/8 cup pineapple juice"
- "2 teaspoons honey"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "1/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "6 cups spring mix greens"
- "2 medium honeycrisp apples, sliced"
- "3/4 cup blue cheese crumbles"
- "3/4 cup dried cranberries"
instructions:
- "=== Candied Pecans ==="
- "Preheat oven 350F. Sheet lined with parchment—no sticking, easy cleanup. Melt butter, brown sugar, salt, water in medium skillet over medium. Stir till sugar glossy, melted; watch closely or it scorches fast. Toss in pecans. Stir nonstop. The sugar coats and crackles; a hint of butter fat in air. After ~3 minutes, flames might rise in pan if you aren’t cautious. Pull from heat; immediately stir in cinnamon. Spread pecans single layer on pan. Bake 4-6 minutes tops until smell deepens and nuts dry. Let cool fully—crisp is key. Warm nuts stick; cooled nuts snap."
- "=== Vinaigrette ==="
- "Whisk together olive oil, apple cider vinegar, white vinegar, lime juice, pineapple juice. The mix should shimmer. Add honey, salt, pepper, garlic powder. Whisk till smooth. Taste! Adjust salt or honey depending on juice sweetness. If too sharp, add extra honey sparingly. If too thick, a splash water or pineapple juice thinned mine out in past tries."
- "=== Salad Assembly ==="
- "Toss spring mix in large bowl. Add apples, blue cheese, cooled candied pecans, dried cranberries. Drizzle vinaigrette slowly, toss gently. Too much dressing? Scooped some off with leaves before serving. Want more texture? Use Granny Smith instead of Honeycrisp for tart snap. For nuts, walnuts swap well but butter-sugar mix maybe less needed. Serve immediately—lettuce wilts fast under wet dressing."
