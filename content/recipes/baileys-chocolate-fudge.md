ingredients:
- "2 cups semisweet chocolate chips"
- "1 can 14 ounces sweetened condensed milk"
- "1/3 cup Kahlua Irish coffee liqueur"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "Parchment paper for lining"
instructions:
- "Line a loaf pan tightly with parchment paper, letting excess hang over sides for easy lift-out later. No shortcuts here."
- "Microwave chocolate chips and condensed milk in a large microwave-safe bowl. Heat in 25-second bursts stirring vigorously after each. This stops scorching, ensures even melt."
- "Look for glossy, thick mixture. It may seem too stiff at first, keep stirring. Should be lump-free before liquor."
- "Quickly fold in the Kahlua and vanilla extract. At first mix will seem chunky, but liquor softens it to a creamy, pourable texture. Only use fresh liquor, rancid taste kills fudge."
- "Pour mixture evenly into the prepared pan, scrape to level surface with a spatula. Tap pan lightly on counter to release air pockets."
- "Chill in fridge for about 2 hours or until firmly set. Longer chill equals better sliceable fudge. Resist the urge to cut early, fudge will crumble."
- "When set, lift fudge by parchment overhang and transfer to cutting board. Use a sharp knife warmed under hot water for clean slices."
- "Store fudge in airtight container in fridge to maintain texture and flavor. Let sit at room temp 10 minutes before serving for best bite."

Sweetened condensed milk adds creaminess and sweetness, don’t substitute with evaporated milk or regular milk unless you adjust sugar dramatically. Kahlua gives a coffee kick that Baileys doesn't; feel free to experiment with other Irish cream substitutes but watch for consistency changes. Vanilla extract should be pure for authentic depth—not imitation. Parchment paper is a lifesaver for removal and cleanup; foil can sometimes stick. Keep the liquor measured just under a third of a cup; more means fudge won’t set properly and gets runny."
instructionsNote: "Microwave heating’s tricky area. Don’t just set a timer and walk off. Chocolate can go from perfect melting smooth to grainy burnt swirls if you overheat. Stirring between short bursts is vital to distribute heat. The thick lumpiness before adding liquor is normal—liquor liquefies it. Don’t add liquor directly to hot chocolate mix or it may seize or separate. Spreading fudge into the pan evenly traps no air bubbles, prevents crumbly texture. Two hours chill is bare minimum; fudge continues firming after that. Cutting cold fudge cleanly means warming the blade first between cuts—cuts get ragged if knife’s cold or dry. tips:
- "Stir chocolate and condensed milk after every short burst in microwave. Hot spots burn easily; glossy thick mix signals near melt. Don’t skip stirring or burn ruins batch fast."
- "Pour liquor and vanilla only when mix is smooth but still warm—not hot. Too hot vaporizes flavor, too cold means clumpy fudge. Fold gently; chunks smooth out after waiting short time."
- "Use double boiler if impatient or wary of microwave nukes. Water steam temp won’t scorch, but slow stirring needed. Watch sheen closely; fudge thickens quickly. Take off heat just before full melt."
- "Parchment paper lined pan critical—fudge sticks to foil, tears on removal. Excess paper sides help lift entire slab in one go. Skip this and slicing turns sticky nightmare. No parchment, use silicone molds."
- "Cut fudge with knife warmed in hot water. Cold blade drags chunks; warm one cleans through easily. Wipe blade between cuts or residue builds, fudge pulls uneven. Chill minimum 2 hours; less equals crumbly slices."
