ingredients:
- "2.5 cups peeled cubed sweet potato"
- "12 ounces elbow macaroni"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "1 small onion chopped"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "2 cups whole milk"
- "1 cup vegetable broth"
- "1 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1/4 teaspoon red pepper chili flakes"
- "1 1/2 cups sharp cheddar cheese shredded"
- "1 cup smoked gouda shredded"
- "1 1/2 cups fresh spinach chopped"
- "1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs"
- "1/3 cup Parmesan cheese grated"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 375 F. You want that radiant heat ready for last step crisp."
- "Boil water in a medium pot. Toss in peeled diced sweet potato. Cook 12-17 minutes; poke with fork - should be fork tender but not falling apart. Drain off water fully. Slip potatoes back and mash well until no lumps, about 2.5 cups."
- "Meanwhile, salt boiling water and cook elbow noodles just shy of al dente - check package but shave off 1-2 minutes for bake. Drain. Toss with small drizzle of oil to avoid sticking."
- "Warm olive oil in large skillet. Toss in chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook on medium heat 5-7 minutes till onions turn translucent and smell fragrant – don’t brown or burn garlic or it turns bitter."
- "Sprinkle flour over onion-garlic mix. Whisk aggressively for 30 seconds to cook raw flour taste out. Slowly pour in milk and veggie broth bit by bit while whisking nonstop. The sauce will hiss as it thickens. After 4-6 minutes low boil, reduce heat; sauce should be thick coating the spoon but not gluey."
- "Off the heat, stir in Dijon mustard, salt, pepper, chili flakes. Fold in shredded cheddar and smoked gouda right away to melt evenly. Then blend in mashed sweet potato and chopped spinach. Spinach wilts slightly from residual heat; don’t cook separately or it gets rubbery."
- "Combine sauce with noodles in pot. Stir good so cheese coats every noodle. Taste. Adjust salt or spice if dull. You want layers here - savory, little zip, creamy, sweet mild from potatoes."
- "Transfer everything to oven-safe casserole dish. Mix panko with Parmesan cheese in small bowl. Sprinkle evenly on top. This topping crisps and adds crunch when baked."
- "Bake uncovered 12-18 minutes; watch for bubbling edges and golden brown crust forming. You’ll hear crackles and see steam rise when it’s just right. Don’t overbake or drying out begins."
- "Let cool 5 minutes post oven to set up. Serve warm."

tips:
- "Boil sweet potatoes fully until just fork tender. Test starting at 12 minutes - too soft turns mushy texture downstream. Drain very well or watery mash ruins sauce thickness. Mash smooth with no lumps. Lukewarm mash blends better into cheese folding step."
- "Pasta undercook by 1-2 minutes from package directions. Oven finish carries over more cooking without sogginess. Toss drained noodles in a little olive oil right after, keeps noodles separate. Prevents gluey egg whites vibe you might get with sticky pasta."
- "Onion and garlic cook low-med heat until translucent; don’t brown garlic or astringent bitterness appears fast. Watch, stir often, smell deep aroma developing before pouring flour. Flour jumpstart thickening but whisk fast to avoid lumpy clumps."
- "Pour milk and broth slowly while whisking or sauce can seize up with lumps. Hiss sound shifts from steam to bubbling low boil around 5 mins; sauce shows thick ribbon on spoon - no gluey texture. If gluey, add splash broth or milk off-heat."
- "Add mustard, salt, and spices off-heat lets flavors meld without burn or harshness. Cheese folded in warm sauce melts truer, no uneven clumps. Add mashed sweet potato and spinach last; residual heat gently wilts spinach. Otherwise, spinach gets rubbery and turns swampy."
- "Mix sauce and pasta thoroughly - every noodle needs coating or dry bits will kill mouthfeel. Taste test crucial here. Adjust salt and spice, little extra zip with pepper flakes brightens flavors. Balance creamy and sweet root notes with cheddar sharp bite."
- "Panko-Parmesan mix sprinkled thin and even over casserole top crisps into crunchy crust. Watch oven carefully 12-18 minutes. Hear crackles and steam means almost done. Too long dries out casserole and dulls cheesy melt. Rest 5 mins post bake to set cheese slab."
