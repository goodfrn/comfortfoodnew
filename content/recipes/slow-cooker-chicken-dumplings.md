ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "2 cans condensed cream of mushroom soup"
- "3 cups chicken broth"
- "1 can water"
- "1 medium yellow onion chopped fine"
- "3 boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "Half cup all purpose flour"
- "5 tablespoons ice cold water"
instructions:
- "Everything goes in the slow cooker. Butter, mushroom soup, chicken broth, water, onion, chicken breasts. Push the chicken down so it's covered by liquid. If it's sticking out, add a little more broth. Uncovered chicken dries out and gets tough."
- "Low for 7 hours. Don't touch it. The broth thickens on its own and the chicken gets so tender it barely holds together. You'll smell it through the whole house by hour five."
- "About an hour before you want to eat, switch to high. Take a fork or potato masher and break the chicken up inside the pot. Chunky pieces. Not shredded fine. You want bites not strings."
- "Make your dumplings. Half cup flour, 5 tablespoons ice cold water. Mix until it holds together. Firm but you can still work it. Cold water matters here. Keeps the dough tender."
- "Flour your counter. Roll the dough thin. About an eighth of an inch. Cut it into rough squares with a pizza cutter. Doesn't have to be pretty."
- "Drop the dumplings around the edges of the slow cooker. That's where the heat is. Stir them in gently so they get coated in broth but don't shove them all to the bottom. They cook better when they're partly in the liquid partly out."
- "Lid on. High for 40 to 50 minutes. Dumplings are done when they feel springy and the centers aren't raw anymore. You can tell by pulling one out and cutting it open. No flour taste means they're ready."
- "Taste the broth. Salt and pepper. Maybe hot sauce if you're into that. Let it sit covered for 10 minutes before serving. The broth thickens up more as the dumplings absorb."

tips:
- "Low for 7 hours. Don't open the lid to check. The smell tells you what's happening. When the broth thickens and you can see the chicken starting to fall apart at the edges, it's close. Break the meat at the end. Chunky pieces with forks or a masher. Not paste."
- "Dumplings thin. Eighth of an inch. Thicker and the middle stays raw and doughy even when the outside looks done. Thinner tears sometimes but cooks through clean. Pizza cutter for cutting. Fast and even. Flour your hands and the board or it sticks to everything."
- "Drop dumplings at the edges. Slow cookers heat from the sides. That's where dumplings cook best. Partly in the broth partly above it. Don't bury them. Stir once gently then leave them alone. This is what makes crock pot chicken and dumplings work properly."
- "Ice cold water for the dough. Not room temp. Cold slows gluten development and you get tender dumplings instead of chewy ones. No baking powder needed. The trapped steam in the slow cooker makes them puff up on their own."
- "Leftovers keep 3 days in the fridge. Dumplings soak up liquid overnight so add a splash of broth when you reheat. Gentle heat on the stove. Microwave works but dumplings can go rubbery. Freezes ok for 2 months but the dumplings soften up after thawing. Still tastes good though."
