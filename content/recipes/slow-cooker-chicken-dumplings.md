ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "2 cans condensed cream of mushroom soup"
- "3 cups chicken broth"
- "1 can water"
- "1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped"
- "3 skinless boneless chicken breasts"
- "1/2 cup all-purpose flour"
- "5 tablespoons ice cold water"
instructions:
- "Set slow cooker on low. Add butter, cream of mushroom soup, chicken broth plus one can of water, chopped onion, chicken breasts. Flip breasts to ensure full submersion in liquid. If not covered, add splash more broth or water; uncovered meat toughens."
- "Cook low for 7 to 7 ½ hours. Smells deepen, broth thickens slightly, chicken fibers start separating without shredding."
- "Increase heat to high about 1 hour before eating. Take potato masher or forks, break chicken into chunky pieces inside cooker. Should mash easily into tender morsels — avoid grinding fine."
- "Mix ½ cup flour with 5 tablespoons icy water. Add more water or flour until dough feels firm but pliable, able to roll thin without sticking. Dumplings flatter means faster cooking, less doughy bite."
- "Dump dough onto floured board, roll to 1/8 inch thickness. Slice into roughly 1-inch squares with pizza cutter or sharp knife. Throw flour behind your back if too sticky."
- "Drop dumplings around edge inside cooker where it’s hottest. Stir gently but thoroughly to partially coat dumplings in broth, prevent sticking but don’t bury completely. Dumplings cook best with partial immersion."
- "Cover. Cook at high for 40 to 50 minutes or until dumplings fluffed, centers opaque. Pull dumpling once it feels springy yet firm to bite — a touch underdone flour bite ruins texture."
- "Taste broth. Add salt, pepper, or a splash of hot sauce if necessary. Stir, then rest covered for 10 minutes. Broth thickens more as dumplings soak flavors."
