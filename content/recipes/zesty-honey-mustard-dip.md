- "Chill bowl beforehand if you want colder dressing fast; helps keep mayo firm."
- "Use a medium bowl, add honey and stone-ground mustard first. Whisk briskly until swirls show and honey breaks up—tiny sticky spots vanish with firm strokes."
- "Add mayo next, fold in gently so you keep texture pockets—no blender here; rustic wins."
- "Pour in apple cider vinegar; notice the sharp aroma release. A quick stir blends the zing without flattening."
- "Sprinkle salt evenly over surface. Give a last vigorous whisk till you see tiny bubbles congregate at edges, not center."
- "Taste now. Mustard can dominate—counter with pepper, maybe a pinch of cayenne if you want heat."
- "Scrape sides with a rubber spatula, mix down fully—no chunks of honey hiding."
- "Transfer to airtight container. Seal tight to avoid fridge odors."
- "Let it sit at least 15 minutes chilled, deeper flavor develops, sharpness softens but hangs on."
- "Shakes well before serving; if it separates, no big deal. Texture becomes smoother after stirring."
- "Store in fridge for up to twelve days; watch for sour off smells or mold—common fail points."
- "If too thick after fridge time, thin with a splash of water or extra vinegar but sparingly."
