ingredients:
- "1 lb strawberries washed, hulled, quartered"
- "1/4 cup granulated sugar plus 3 tbsp for lemon sugar"
- "1 tsp lemon juice fresh"
- "1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/4 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1 1/2 tbsp lemon zest freshly grated"
- "6 tbsp cold unsalted butter cubed"
- "3/4 cup buttermilk cold"
- "1/4 cup heavy cream plus extra for brushing"
- "1 tbsp powdered sugar"
- "1/2 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2 tbsp sour cream"
- "thin dusting powdered sugar for garnish"
instructions:
- "Quarter strawberries, toss with sugar and lemon juice in a bowl. Chill for at least 25 minutes. Look for juices pooling but berries still whole. If no juice, add a pinch more sugar."
- "Heat oven to 430°F. Line sheet with parchment or silicone mat. Prep for quick transfer."
- "Rub sugar and lemon zest between fingers until fragrant, oils release. Combine with flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt in a big bowl. Whisk well till mixed evenly."
- "Cut cold butter into small cubes. Press into dry mix with fingertips till crumbly dough appears. Keep fingers cool or chill dough if too soft."
- "Make a well, pour buttermilk in one go. Stir gently with spatula or fork, just enough to bind dough loosely. Don’t overwork. Ragged edges signal just right."
- "Dump dough on floured surface. Knead, fold over itself 7 times. Dough should stick slightly but hold shape. Add flour sparingly to avoid tough biscuits."
- "Pat into 3/4 to 1 inch rectangle. Use 3 inch biscuit cutter, straight down, no twist. Gather scraps quickly, press again. Aim for 7 or 8 cakes. Less handling prevents toughness."
- "Place close together on sheet; touching sides bake taller, golden all around, avoid tilt. For uneven edges, trim cutter slightly or use new cutter."
- "Brush tops with cream, sprinkle with sugar if you want sparkle and crunch. Bake 14–19 minutes. Edges deep golden, top cracks lightly. Smell butter roasting."
- "Beat cream medium high. When soft peaks form, add powdered sugar, vanilla, sour cream. Beat till stiffer peaks hold shape but still creamy. Sour cream adds tang and stability."
- "Slice cakes horizontally. Layer whipped cream, strawberries with syrup. Top with second cake. Crown with more cream, berries, dust with powdered sugar. Eat immediately or shortcakes soften."

- "q: How to prevent dense biscuits? a: Cut cold butter, minimal mixing. Dough handled gently, folds only few times. Overkneading develops gluten, chewiness creeps in. Chill dough if too warm before bake."
