ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "½ cup light brown sugar packed"
- "½ cup granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 teaspoons maple syrup instead of vanilla extract for twist"
- "1 ½ cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 ¼ cups old-fashioned rolled oats"
- "½ teaspoon table salt"
- "½ teaspoon cinnamon (replacing baking powder)"
- "½ teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 ½ cups semi-sweet chocolate chunks (sub chocolate chips if preferred)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 345°F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats."
- "In large bowl, beat butter and both sugars until blend creamy but don’t overbeat or add air—just combined."
- "Mix in eggs one at a time, then pour in maple syrup; beating till batter thickens slightly and becomes fluffy-ish."
- "Whisk together flour, oats, salt, cinnamon, and baking soda separately to distribute evenly."
- "Gradually fold dry into wet mixture. Use mixer low or wooden spoon: get all dry stubborn bits incorporated but not overmixing."
- "Fold in chocolate chunks last—don’t pulverize them; chunky bits add surprise pockets."
- "Cover bowl with plastic wrap tight, refrigerate 35 minutes minimum. Dough firms up—makes scooping manageable and prevents spreading."
- "Scoop rounded dough balls onto trays with about 1 ½ inches gap. Press each gently just to flatten tops, helps bake evenly."
- "Bake about 12 minutes. Watch edges golden and centers set but springy when touched lightly—cookies continue setting out of oven."
- "Cool 3-4 minutes on sheet before transferring to rack. Avoid immediate removal, they firm up while cooling."
- "Serve warm or cooled. Store airtight but use within 3 days or freeze for longer."
- "Note: If dough too soft after chilling, add 1 tablespoon flour before scooping. Butter too warm? Chill 10-15 minutes more. Sugar types interchangeable but brown sugar adds chew."
