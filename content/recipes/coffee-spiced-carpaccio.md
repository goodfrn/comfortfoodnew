ingredients:
- "1 pound beef tenderloin center cut"
- "2 tablespoons finely ground coffee beans"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt"
- "2 teaspoons brown sugar (or coconut sugar)"
- "1 tablespoon canola oil (sub grapeseed oil)"
- "Dijon mustard sauce for drizzling (recipe below)"
- "Freshly ground black pepper"
- "Coarse sea salt"
instructions:
- "Mix ground coffee, garlic powder, smoked paprika, kosher salt, and brown sugar in small bowl. Aim for balance so coffee doesn't dominate but still stands out."
- "Pat dry beef tenderloin with paper towels. Rub spice mix all over thoroughly, pressing in firmly so it forms an adhered crust. Skip wet hands; dry hands get the spice to cling better."
- "Heat skillet on medium-high until oil shimmers just before smoke point. Sear tenderloin edges only. Turn frequently for even caramelization. Look for coffee aroma rising and sugar changes to a light caramel shade—not burnt black spots. Should take about 4-5 minutes. Resist temptation to overcook meat here."
- "Remove beef from pan. Let sit til cool enough to handle but still warm to the touch. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap, twisting ends like candy, 3 times in alternating directions. This tight wrap is critical to avoid freezer burn and lock in shape."
- "Freeze wrapped tenderloin for at least 3 hours but no more than 25. Freezing firms beef for razor-thin slices. Don’t skip this or slices turn mushy or tear."
- "When ready, take out from freezer, unwrap carefully to avoid moisture buildup. Let sit 25 minutes max at room temp. Too warm and slices won’t hold shape; too cold and knife drags."
- "Use a very sharp long slicer or mandoline held steady. Slice as paper-thin as possible. Err on thinner than you think. Layer slices on plate lined with plastic. Wrap plate tightly and freeze again until serving time."
- "At serving, remove slices about 10 minutes before plating. Drizzle Dijon mustard sauce liberally. Grind fresh black pepper and sprinkle coarse sea salt on top."
- "Serve immediately. Carpaccio texture should be silky with a slight crisp from spice crust. Any longer thaw means mushiness and loss of that contrast between crust and raw center."

Layering spice in stages helps build up a thicker crust to caramelize nicely."
- "Searing edges on medium-high heat until oil shimmers, not smokes. Flick oil drops to test—too hot means burnt sugar instantly. Flip every 20 seconds or so for even caramelization.
