ingredients:
- "1 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs chunked"
- "½ teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 tablespoon cornstarch plus 1 teaspoon"
- "1 tablespoon shaoxing wine"
- "2 teaspoons tamari soy sauce substitute"
- "2 tablespoons soy sauce"
- "1 tablespoon rice vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon shaoxing wine"
- "1½ teaspoons brown sugar"
- "1 tablespoon hoisin sauce"
- "1 teaspoon sesame oil"
- "3 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "1 cup snap peas swapped for peppers"
- "½ cup celery sliced"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 teaspoon fresh ginger minced"
- "3 green onions chopped, white and light green parts"
- "⅓ cup roasted peanuts"
- "6-8 dried red chiles"
instructions:
- "=== Marinate the chicken ==="
- "Toss chicken chunks with ½ teaspoon baking soda and 1 tablespoon cornstarch — baking soda’s a gamble for tenderness but don’t overdo; past mistakes left slimy edges. Splash in 1 tablespoon shaoxing wine and 2 teaspoons tamari, toss until slick. Rest 8-12 minutes — don’t let cornstarch soak too long or coating falls off. Meanwhile prep veggies and sauce."
- "=== Mix the sauce ==="
- "Whisk 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 1 tablespoon rice vinegar, 1 tablespoon shaoxing wine, 1½ teaspoons brown sugar, 1 tablespoon hoisin, 1 teaspoon sesame oil plus 1 teaspoon cornstarch. Cornstarch amount cut slightly to avoid gluey sauce. Set aside. Sauce should shimmer, hold loosely, not glue the bowl."
- "=== Stir Fry ==="
- "Heat wok or large skillet over medium-high, vegetable oil until shimmering, almost smoking — pan must be hot or chicken steams, not crisp. Drop chicken pieces in, spread out immediately, don’t crowd. Rest undisturbed 2½ minutes till base crisps, hear popping, edges turning gold. Flip, cook other side another 2-3 minutes. Chicken will puff up, feel firm but juicy. Remove, tent loosely with foil — don’t trap steam or chicken sogs."
- "Add snap peas and celery to same pan; sizzle should still sing. Stir briskly for 5-6 minutes till veggies soften yet snap under teeth, vibrant green intact. Now toss in garlic, ginger, green onions, chiles, peanuts along pan edge so peanuts toast from radiant heat without burning. Saute 20-30 seconds — fragrance hits, garlic gold but not bitter. Control heat here; garlic burns quick, bitterness kills dish."
- "Pour in sauce and chicken. Toss carefully to coat every piece; sauce should thicken and bubble within 4-5 minutes, watch for glossy sheen coating back of spoon — signals correct texture, not gloopy or watery. Adjust heat low-medium if sauce reduces too fast or thickens prematurely."
- "Serve immediately with steamed rice — essential to balance spice and umami punch."
- "Cleanup tip: If peanuts stick or burn, pre-toast on separate dry pan. If chicken overcooks lest tough; use thigh meat always for juiciness. Substitute walnuts or cashews for peanuts if allergy kicks in. Dried chiles vary by heat; adjust to taste. Alternate shaoxing with dry sherry or omit if none available, but flavor dims slightly."
- "If tried, come back and rant or rave, stars appreciated."
