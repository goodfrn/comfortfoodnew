ingredients:
- "2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour"
- "¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder (use Dutch-processed for richer flavor)"
- "2 teaspoons cornstarch"
- "½ teaspoon baking powder"
- "½ teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened"
- "¾ cup granulated sugar"
- "½ cup packed light brown sugar"
- "3 tablespoons heavy cream (can swap for whole milk but alters texture)"
- "2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "¾ cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "¾ cup milk chocolate chips"
- "¾ cup white chocolate chips"
instructions:
- "Start by whisking flour, cocoa powder, cornstarch, baking powder, and baking soda in a medium bowl. No lumps; cornstarch gives a tender crumb. Set aside."
- "Beat softened butter on medium-high for 1 to 1½ minutes. Aim for creamy, no greasy bits. Key for texture. Not too soft or it’ll ruin the structure."
- "Add both sugars with heavy cream and vanilla. Then mix for 1½ to 2 minutes until that pale, fluffiness shows. You want it aerated but silkier, not grainy."
- "Lower mixer speed. Crack in eggs one at a time, mixing fully after each. Slow speed avoids curdling, egg by egg builds body."
- "Gradually add the flour mixture. Mix on low just until no visible dry patches -- don’t overdo it or they get tough."
- "Fold in all three types of chocolate chips. Semi-sweet, milk, white – harmony in chaos. Use rubber spatula to prevent overmixing."
- "Cover dough tightly, chill in fridge for about 65 minutes. Chilling firms up fat, stops spreading, and develops flavor. If short on time, 45 minutes works but cookie texture changes."
- "Preheat oven to 355°F. Yes, a slight bump from norm. Line sheet with parchment paper to avoid stuck bottoms and ease cleanup."
- "Use 1½ tablespoon scoop for uniform cookies. Space 2 inches apart; dough spreads despite chilling. Smaller scoops = crispier edges, larger = chewy centers."
- "Bake 9 to 12 minutes. I opt closer to 11. Edges firm but centers soft, look slightly underbaked in middle, still shiny. Let sit on sheet 5-7 minutes. They finish cooking off heat – crucial. Transfer to wire rack to cool fully, or they get mushy bottoms."
