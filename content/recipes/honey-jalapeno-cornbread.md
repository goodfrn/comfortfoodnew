ingredients:
- "1⁄2 cup unsalted butter divided"
- "1⁄3 cup honey divided"
- "2 medium jalapeños sliced thin, seeds optional"
- "1 cup cornmeal"
- "3⁄4 cup all-purpose flour substituted with whole wheat flour occasionally"
- "3 tablespoons sugar"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1 cup buttermilk or yogurt thinned with milk"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup canned or fresh corn kernels drained well"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380 degrees F. Grease a 9-inch springform or cake pan thoroughly with cooking spray. Butter burns fast; oil works too."
- "Melt 1⁄4 cup butter and 1⁄6 cup honey in a microwave-safe dish, stirring every 20 seconds. You want the butter liquid but not browned."
- "Pour that sticky honey butter into the bottom of your pan. Slosh it around to coat thoroughly, shiny layer is classic."
- "With gloves on (don’t skip if you like fingers intact), arrange jalapeño slices flat in the honey butter, single layer only. Press gently so they make contact."
- "Whisk cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder and salt in a large bowl until the white powder spreads evenly. Dry mix matters for rise and crumb."
- "Melt remaining 1⁄4 cup butter, but keep this separate."
- "In a separate bowl, whisk buttermilk, eggs, remaining 1⁄6 cup honey and melted butter until silky but not frothy. Too much air messes texture."
- "Add wet mix to dry ingredients. Stir just till no dry specks. Overmixing tightens crumb, no one wants rubbery cornbread."
- "Fold in corn kernels delicately, don’t break them up. Corn bursts unexpectedly; these bulbs of sweetness contrast the peppers."
- "Pour batter over jalapeños in pan. Spread evenly with spatula. Tap pan on counter a few times to pop large air bubbles—avoid sinkholes later."
- "Bake 23 to 28 minutes. Look for edges pulling back from pan, golden brown top with slight cracks, and toothpick inserted with moist crumbs attached."
- "Let cool five minutes in pan. Place plate or platter on top. Flip quickly and confidently—the jalapeños will glisten on top, sticky and hot."
- "Slice into 8 generous wedges, watch for steam and sticky honey glitter on knife. Best warm, hold back if reheating or it toughens."
- "If fresh jalapeños too hot, remove membranes and seeds or use milder peppers like Fresno for subtler heat."
