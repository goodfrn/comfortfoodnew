ingredients:
- "2 cups cake flour sifted"
- "2 1/4 teaspoons baking powder"
- "1/2 cup sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 1/2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries"
- "2 tablespoons brown sugar"
- "2 tablespoons cake flour"
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter melted"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "1 large egg"
- "2 tablespoons white sugar"
- "2 tablespoons coarse sugar"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355 degrees Fahrenheit. Line muffin tins with liners that can handle serious baking — sturdy ones hold shape better."
- "Sift together cake flour, baking powder, sugar and salt into a large bowl. Cake flour prevents toughness; sift well to aerate."
- "In a small bowl toss blueberries with 2 tablespoons cake flour and brown sugar. Brown sugar adds a caramel note, coats berries to keep them suspended, not sinking."
- "In a stand mixer with paddle or large bowl, whisk melted butter, milk and egg gently until just combined. No rushing here; a smooth blend but not whipped."
- "Add dry ingredients gradually to wet mix. Stir slowly and barely. Monitor for lumps, but no dry flour patches—overmixing ruins crumb. It should be patchy, lumpy but uniform."
- "Fold in blueberry mixture cautiously. You’ll get blue swirls, especially if frozen berries. That marbling is normal. Resist urge to over fold; berries bruise easily."
- "If you want a sugar topping, toss white sugar and coarse sugar together in a small bowl. This gives crunchy crust, contrast to soft insides."
- "Fill muffin cups about two-thirds full. No top-offs; batter rises well. Sprinkle sugar blend atop each. Sugar melts, crisps under heat."
- "Bake 28 minutes for large muffins; 22 minutes if smaller. Oven quirks vary, so watch tops for golden color and spring-back offers best doneness clue. Little cracks? Good sign. Smell buttery, sweet with berry hints."
- "Remove from oven and cool on wire rack. Muffins firm up as they cool; tops should feel springy, not wet or sticky. Cooling period lets crumb settle—resist eating too soon."
