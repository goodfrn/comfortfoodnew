ingredients:
- "1 package corn muffin mix (around 8 ounces; replace Jiffy with authentic or homemade cornmeal blend)"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup buttermilk (or for richer, use a combo of 1/2 cup heavy cream plus 1/2 cup sour cream; add 2 tablespoons jalapeño pickle juice instead of brine)"
- "1/3 cup vegetable shortening (greasing muffin tin)"
- "12 fresh jalapeño slices (plus a few extras if you like it hotter)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345° to 355°F range. Too hot dries edges. Grease a standard 12-cup muffin tin with shortening. For 16 muffins, prep two tins or batch bake."
- "In a large bowl, break up corn muffin mix lumps with a fork or whisk. Don’t pulverize—just knock down biggest chunks. Helps even hydration later."
- "Crack eggs in. Pour in buttermilk combo with pickle brine—watch thickness. Stir gently using spatula or wooden spoon. Avoid overmixing or batter turns gluey and tough after baking."
- "Fold until batter mostly smooth but still slightly lumpy. Batter consistency should be thick yet pourable. Think dense pancake batter, not spoon-stiff dough."
- "Spoon batter into tins, fill each cavity about two-thirds full. Tops will puff, so leave room. Press a jalapeño slice directly atop each muffin for flavor burst and visual appeal."
- "Slide muffin tray in center rack of oven. Bake for roughly 18 to 22 minutes. Look for puffy golden tops that spring back lightly to touch and edges pulling slightly from pan walls."
- "Cool 5 minutes before running a knife around edges; muffins will be fragile hot. Remove carefully and cool on rack for better texture and easier handling."
- "If muffins dome too much or top burns, reduce oven heat by a few degrees next batch or try a lower rack position."
- "Optional twist: add a handful of sharp cheddar or cotija cheese to batter for savory dimension. Or fold in corn kernels for crunch and added texture."
- "Common pitfalls: overmixing batter = rubbery crumb. Too hot oven = dry, cracked tops. Underbake = dense and gummy. Visual bounce test beats stopwatch."
- "Leftovers toast beautifully next day, crisp outside creamy inside. Refrigerate tightly or freeze wrapped individually."
