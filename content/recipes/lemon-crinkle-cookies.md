- "1/2 cup unsalted butter browned, cooled"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 large egg yolk"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon almond extract switched in for lemon extract"
instructions:
- "Whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt in medium bowl. Set aside."
- "In a large bowl, rub lemon zest and granulated sugar between your fingertips until fragrant oils release—granules will feel coarse and moist. This awakens lemon flavor more than zest alone."
- "Pour in browned butter; whisk with sugar until combined and slightly thickened. Brown butter adds toasted nuttiness missing in regular melted butter—trust me, worth the extra step."
- "Add eggs and egg yolk. Whisk well until the mixture turns smooth and pale yellow. Then stir in vanilla and almond extracts. Almond extract gives a deeper, less sharp flavor contrast to the lemon."
- "Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture gently, stirring just until no flour streaks remain. Overmixing develops gluten—tough cookies, no thanks."
- "Cover bowl and chill dough minimum 1.5 hours or overnight. If overnight, dough firms up a lot; let sit at room temp 10 minutes before scooping. Chilled dough rolls easier, less sticky."
- "Preheat oven to 345°F (adjusted down slightly). Line baking sheets with parchment paper."
- "Set out small bowls: one with granulated sugar, another with powdered sugar. Use a cookie scoop or spoon to form 1.5 to 2 tablespoon dough balls. Roll balls between palms for smoothness."
- "Roll each ball first in granulated sugar, then coat liberally in powdered sugar. Double powder coat helps cracked surface pattern develop during baking. Place cookies spaced 2 inches apart on sheets. If dough warms up, pop back in fridge between batches."
- "Bake 9-12 minutes. Look for edges turning opaque and firm, centers jiggle slightly. Bottoms should be golden but not browned. Underbaking is your friend here; centers stay gooey after cooling."
- "If cookies spread unevenly, swirl pan gently or nudge dough with spoon while warm to shape circles. Press dough balls flat very slightly before baking if they seem stubborn to spread."
- "Let cookies cool 1-2 minutes on pan, then transfer with spatula to wire rack lined with parchment. Cooling firms cookies, sets texture. Store airtight after fully cooled."
