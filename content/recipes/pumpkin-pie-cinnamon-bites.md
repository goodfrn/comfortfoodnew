ingredients:
- "1 can 12-count cinnamon rolls with icing"
- "1 cup pumpkin puree"
- "1 large egg or 3 tablespoons heavy cream"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar or maple syrup substitute"
- "1½ teaspoons pumpkin pie spice blended with ½ teaspoon ground ginger (swap cinnamon for nutmeg twist)"
- "½ teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "¼ cup crushed pecans (optional, adds crunch)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 350°F. Spray 12-cup muffin pan liberally with nonstick spray. This step critical to avoid sticking—muffin tin can be finicky."
- "Split each cinnamon roll horizontally into two thinner discs. Pat one half gently into bottom and about one-third up sides of each muffin well. Press firmly but no tearing. Uniform thickness ensures even baking."
- "In medium bowl, whisk pumpkin, egg (or cream), sugar (or syrup), pumpkin pie spice mix, and vanilla until just combined. Avoid overmixing or filling gets too loose, see slight thickness when scooped."
- "Spoon a heaping tablespoon pumpkin filling into center of each dough-lined cup. Sprinkle pecans over top if using. They toast beautifully in oven adding texture contrast."
- "Bake 20 to 22 minutes; watch color closely. Crust turns deep golden with crisp edges; filling sets but jiggles slightly when shaken. I rely on aroma too—the pumpkin spice should be fragrant and baking scent deepens near done."
- "Cool on wire rack 10 minutes minimum. Filling firms up more but stays creamy. If you rush cooling the filling can weep or crack. Repeat with remaining rolls and filling."
- "Serve warm or cooled. Optional drizzle of cinnamon roll icing or fresh whipped cream amps sweetness. Store leftovers wrapped, reheat gently to revive crispness but no microwave steam-over—oven or skillet works better."
