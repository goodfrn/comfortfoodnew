ingredients:
- "1 package refrigerated pizza dough (about 12 ounces)"
- "12 cooked meatballs (can be homemade or store-bought)"
- "1 cup marinara sauce, regular or spicy"
- "1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper"
- "cooking spray or vegetable shortening"
- "optional: grated Parmesan cheese"
- "optional: minced fresh parsley"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 410°F; I bumped it slightly to get a crisper crust quicker."
- "Spray or grease a 12-cup muffin pan to prevent sticking; use shortening if you want extra browning on edges."
- "Roll dough flat; slice into 12 squares roughly equal. Press squares into muffin wells, covering bottom and sides. No gaps or dough too thin—watch the edges."
- "Bake dough cups 7-8 minutes until dough is just set, edges look matte to slightly puffed, but not browned yet."
- "Mix garlic powder, onion powder, ground pepper in a small bowl. Brush a thin coat over the warm dough cups immediately after par-bake. Why? Adds flavor and keeps dough from sogging out from sauce later. Skipping this step tends to yield gummy bottoms."
- "Place one cooked meatball into each dough cup. Don’t overcrowd; one per cup works best so heat circulates and melts cheese evenly."
- "Ladle roughly 1 tablespoon marinara over each meatball; should coat but not drown it."
- "Top with about 1 tablespoon shredded mozzarella each, enough to cover meatball but not heap or spill over. Cheese melts best on moderate layer."
- "Return to oven 6-7 minutes, watch for bubbling, edges turning golden brown—avoid overcooking or cheese burning. If cheese still pale after, another minute or two."
- "Pull from oven; immediately sprinkle with grated Parmesan and fresh parsley for brightness and salty kick."
- "Cool a few minutes; dough will crisp as it cools for that satisfying crunch under cheesy topping."
- "Serve warm. Use leftovers wrapped tight, reheat covered to avoid drying. Dough may soften if refrigerated too long."
