ingredients:
- "3 large ripe bananas mashed (about 1½ cups)"
- "¾ cup granulated sugar (can reduce to ⅔ cup for less sweet)"
- "½ cup creamy natural peanut butter (swap for almond butter for twist)"
- "⅓ cup vegetable oil or melted coconut oil"
- "½ cup buttermilk (or plain yogurt thinned with a splash of milk)"
- "2 large eggs at room temperature"
- "1½ tsp vanilla extract"
- "1¾ cups all-purpose flour (can substitute ½ cup with whole wheat flour)"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "½ tsp baking powder"
- "¼ tsp fine sea salt"
- "¾ cup semi-sweet chocolate chips (dark for less sweet or chopped nuts for crunch)"
- "For glaze:"
- "4 tbsp smooth peanut butter"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "2 tbsp whole milk (or milk of choice)"
- "1 cup powdered sugar sifted"
- "½ tsp vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 350°F Adjust rack one notch below center to avoid overbrowning top but still good rise"
- "Grease or parchment-line a 9×5 loaf pan Light-colored pans show browning better—dark pans absorb more heat risking burnt crust"
- "Mix wet: mash bananas with fork in a large bowl Stir in sugar, peanut butter, and oil until streaks disappear Then whisk in buttermilk, eggs, and vanilla till combined Be gentle with eggs, avoid foaming—tough crumb risk"
- "Separate dry bowl: whisk flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt Sifting optional here—just break clumps well Avoid overmixing gluten development—dense bread"
- "Fold dry gradually into wet with rubber spatula Stop mixing when just incorporated Batter should look mottled but not dry Still bits of flour okay; stir too much and bread turns chewy"
- "Fold in chocolate chips gently Reserve few chips for sprinkling on surface if desired"
- "Pour batter into prepared pan Smooth the top with spatula or back of spoon"
- "Bake 60–65 minutes or until toothpick comes out mostly clean Tiny moist crumbs fine but no wet batter Center may jiggle less after 1 hr 5 min but check visually crust golden with cracked top"
- "Cool in pan on wire rack about 30 minutes Bread pulls slightly from edges, smells nutty and sweet Almost too hot to touch but not burning"
- "Remove from pan Cool completely This is key—warm bread with glaze melts glaze and makes it slide off"
- "Glaze: Microwave peanut butter, butter, and milk in 30-second bursts Stir between until smooth and melted No overheating or separating"
- "Whisk in powdered sugar and vanilla If glaze too thick add splash milk if too thin add sifted powdered sugar Adjust till spreadable but not runny"
- "Spread glaze evenly over cooled bread Let set 10–15 minutes before slicing Use serrated knife for clean cuts"
