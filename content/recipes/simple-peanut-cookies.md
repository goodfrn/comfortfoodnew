- "1 large egg"
instructions:
- "===PREP==="
- "Heat oven 350 F give it time to steady not just blasting hot. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper nice and smooth. Reduce potential sticking, clean up easier."
- "===MIX==="
- "In a large bowl, spoon peanut butter and honey. Use a hand mixer or sturdy spoon. Beat medium-high speed—2 1/2 minutes this time to ensure honey fully incorporates. Creamy, shiny surface signals ready. Egg next; break it whole and pour in, then stir or beat on low briefly. Just until no more streaks or clumps. Overmixing ruins texture, leads to dense cookies."
- "===SHAPE==="
- "Scoop dough: Tablespoon-sized balls, but I prefer slightly rounded for uniform baking. Dough’s sticky but firm. Grease your fork (butter or cooking spray) before pressing. Do it twice in crisscross pattern, birds-eye on shape and even spread out. Press too hard—cookies turn tough. Too loose—lose shape. Precision counts."
- "===BAKE==="
- "Slide cookie sheet into oven center rack. Check after 8 minutes—edges start browning, look slightly dry or matte, middle soft but not runny. That subtle pull back from parchment, visual shrinking cues. Oven temps vary; trust eyes more than clock. Undercooked? Leave 1 to 2 minutes more but watch closely or risk burning."
- "===COOL==="
- "Let cookies rest on sheet for 2 to 3 minutes. Still fragile warm but beginning to set. Removing too soon breaks them. Then transfer to cooling rack to avoid sogginess. Cookies firm up more selectively. Store airtight, room temp, lasts days without losing that chewiness or flavor. Freezing works but slightly changes texture over time."
