ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 cup Dutch-processed cocoa powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 tablespoon tapioca starch"
- "10 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened but cool"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "2/3 cup light brown sugar, packed"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "1 large whole egg"
- "1 large egg white, lightly beaten"
- "1/3 cup half-and-half cream"
- "1 1/2 cups dark chocolate chips, divided"
instructions:
- "Whisk dry ingredients: flour, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, tapioca starch till even color, lumps gone. Set aside."
- "Cream butter and both sugars using paddle attachment or by hand. Should turn pale, airy - about 2 1/2 minutes at medium speed or slightly longer mixing by hand. Scrape sides well."
- "Add vanilla, whole egg; beat to incorporate fully. Fold in beaten egg white gently to keep air bubbles intact - this yields softer texture. Avoid overbeating now."
- "Lower mixer speed. Add half the dry mix gradually. Add half half-and-half. Mix just till combined, no more."
- "Repeat the dry mix and cream addition. Stop mixing as soon as dough holds together. Overworking scrambles butter and tightens gluten - tough cookies."
- "Stir chocolate chips in by hand. No mixer for this step; aim is even distribution without crushing chips."
- "Wrap dough airtight. Chill minimum 1 hour, up to 3 hours if possible. Chilling slows butter, reduces spread and intensifies flavors. Rest time vital."
- "Preheat oven 350°F (175° C). Line sheets with parchment or silicone liners. Avoid greasing, cookies hold better that way."
- "Portion dough with mid-sized scoop or heaping tablespoon. Roll between palms for smooth balls – helps even baking spread."
- "Place 2 inches apart. Bake about 10 minutes. Watch edges. When firm but center still a bit soft, pull out. Cookies firm up off heat but keep moist interior."
- "While hot, press a few chips on top for show and melty contrast. Let sit on sheet few minutes then transfer to rack for full cooling. Avoid stacking warm cookies."
- "Use visual cues not just timer. Smell deep chocolate and sugar caramelizing. Texture touches warm dough corners for perfect finish."
