ingredients:
- "1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice blend"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 cup canned pure pumpkin puree"
- "1/2 cup vegetable oil"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons almond extract"
- "1/3 cup light brown sugar"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "8 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "1/4 cup granulated sugar, for filling"
- "1 large egg yolk"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Line two 12-cup muffin tins with 20 muffin liners, set aside."
- "In a medium bowl, beat softened cream cheese with 1/4 cup sugar using handheld mixer on low for 1 minute until creamy but not overwhipped."
- "Add egg yolk and almond extract; mix for 30 seconds until fully combined and glossy. Transfer to piping bag or zip-top with tiny corner cut; set aside in fridge."
- "Mix flour, pumpkin pie spice, cinnamon, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in separate bowl. Whisk until even—no lumps."
- "In large bowl, whisk together pumpkin puree, vegetable oil, eggs, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and 1 teaspoon almond extract until homogenous. You want no streaks of egg whites or clumps of sugar."
- "Fold dry flour mixture into pumpkin wet mix carefully, stirring just till combined—avoid overmixing or tough muffins. Batter should be thick but still slightly loose, a few bubbles visible."
- "Fill each muffin cup about ¾ full with pumpkin batter. Don’t tap too hard or air bubbles disappear."
- "Pipe about 1.5 teaspoons of cream cheese mixture into center of each muffin, swirl lightly with toothpick if desired, but don’t blend completely; leave cream cheese pockets intact."
- "Bake for 22 to 27 minutes. Listen for soft thuds when pans are gently tapped, muffins spring back when pressed lightly. Toothpick inserted just outside cream cheese area should come out clean-ish, a few moist crumbs fine. Avoid baking until dry or cracking sharply on top."
- "Cool muffins in pan for 8 minutes, then transfer to wire rack completely. Warm cream cheese filling is gooey but cool it firm up for best texture."
- "Serve warm or room temp; leftovers keep 2 days tightly wrapped or freeze once cooled."

Light brown sugar adds moisture and a touch of molasses flavor; replacing with dark brown sugar gives deeper notes but risks sogginess. Vegetable oil can swap with melted butter, but expect a slight change in tenderness and flavor. Cream cheese must be softened for easy mixing; cold cheese ruins texture and makes piping difficult. Egg yolk adds richness and binds cream cheese filling; no substitution recommended here or filling will break down. Piping bag isn’t mandatory; a small spoon works in a jam but neatness suffers."
