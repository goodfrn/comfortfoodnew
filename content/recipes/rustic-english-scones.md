ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "⅓ cup sugar"
- "½ teaspoon salt"
- "½ cup unsalted butter very cold or frozen"
- "⅔ cup sour cream"
- "1 large egg"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven near 405 degrees. Line sheet with parchment; no sticking, even heat."
- "Whisk flour, baking powder, sugar, salt in medium bowl. Uniform blend; big lumps ruin rise."
- "Grate frozen butter with cheese grater directly into flour. Works best cold so it stays crumbly, not greasy."
- "Pinch butter and flour together—no machines. Fingers, pastry cutter, whatever but keep crumbs visible. Overwork melts butter, tough scones."
- "In small bowl, whisk sour cream and egg. Pour into dry mix and gently fold with hands only until it just sticks. Sticky dough means moisture held right—don’t knead."
- "Form round ball, plop onto parchment. Press down with palm to roughly 9 inches across, slightly uneven ok."
- "Slice into 8 wedges gently—don’t press or compress dough too much. Separate wedges a bit for air space, easier browning."
- "Bake 15-20 minutes; watch edges color first. Tops should show faint golden hints but not brown fully. Touch feels firm but quick spring back hint."
- "Remove from oven. Let cool 5 minutes or serve warm right away. Crisp edge, soft crumb, with butter melting inside."
- "If butter missing, use cold coconut oil for subtle twist. Sour cream swap: thick Greek yogurt works but add splash milk to loosen dough."
