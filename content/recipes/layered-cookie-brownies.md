ingredients:
- "1 1/3 cups sugar"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 teaspoons vanilla extract"
- "3 tablespoons water"
- "1 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 1/4 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips divided"
- "10 Oreo cookies or sandwich cookies (for crumbs)"
- "4 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/3 cup heavy cream"
instructions:
- "=== Brownie Layer ==="
- "Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Spray or lightly butter a 9×13 inch pan. The pan surface must be greased well or you’ll risk sticking, especially at corners."
- "Cream sugar and softened butter with paddle attachment or hand mixer on medium speed until mixture lightens and looks fluffy—about 3 to 4 minutes. This aeration sets up a tender crumb later."
- "Add eggs one at a time, fully blending after each addition. Then stir in vanilla and water until combined but don’t overmix or the batter will become tough."
- "Sift flour, baking powder, cocoa, and salt together to avoid clumps. Add dry ingredients gradually to wet, folding gently to keep it light but uniform. Expect a thick, slightly glossy batter."
- "Fold in 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips by hand. Distribute evenly but don’t overdo it; chips help melt in little pockets creating textural contrast."
- "Pour batter into pan and smooth with offset spatula. Bake about 18 to 22 minutes then check with toothpick in center. Look for moist crumbs adhering but no raw batter. Brownie may seem soft but will firm as it cools."
- "Cool completely on wire rack about 30 minutes minimum to avoid melting next layer."
- "=== Cookie Layer ==="
- "Pulse Oreo or sandwich cookies in food processor until fine crumbs form but avoid powdery dust; texture matters for binding. No processor? Place them in a sealed bag, crush with rolling pin until sandy."
- "Mix cream cheese softened with cookie crumbs using paddle attachment or hand mixer. Beat until even but not overly whipped—too much air makes spreading messy."
- "Spread the cream cheese cookie crumb mix evenly over cooled brownie layer. Use a spatula or back of spoon; thickness about 1/4 inch. Press gently to avoid mixing layers."
- "=== Chocolate Ganache ==="
- "Combine remaining 1/4 cup semi-sweet chips with heavy cream in microwave-safe bowl or double boiler. Heat gently in short bursts (15 seconds), stirring often to prevent scorching."
- "When ganache reaches thick, pourable consistency—not runny—immediately spread over cream cheese layer with offset spatula. Cool slightly to thicken if too loose."
- "Chill entire pan in fridge for at least 20 minutes until ganache sets hard enough to slice."
- "Use sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped dry before cutting to get clean edges without cracking ganache. Wipe knife between cuts."
- "Store airtight in fridge up to one week; allow to sit at room temperature 10 minutes before serving for best mouthfeel."
