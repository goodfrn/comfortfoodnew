ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 large onion diced"
- "1 bell pepper diced (any color)"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1 (4 ounce) can diced green chiles"
- "12 large eggs"
- "1 cup whole milk"
- "1 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "1 (30 ounce) bag frozen hash browns, thawed and drained"
- "1 pound breakfast sausage cooked crumbled *substitute ham or turkey sausage*"
- "2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese (divided)"
- "non-stick cooking spray"
- "Optional garnish chopped green onions, fresh chives, hot sauce"
instructions:
- "Start heating olive oil in a decent-size skillet over medium-high heat. Toss in onions and bell peppers. Listen for that sizzle and watch when the peppers start to soften but still hold shape around 5-7 minutes. Stir often but don’t mash. Sprinkle garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Near the end, fold in diced green chiles. Smells intoxicating here; slight tang, savory lift. Remove from heat once veggies are vibrant with slight translucence."
- "Crack all eggs into a large mixing bowl. Whisk briskly while adding milk and Dijon mustard. The mustard cuts the richness, keeps things from getting flat. Whisk until mixture is uniform, a few bubbles on top. No lumps. Set aside."
- "Prep slow cooker with a spray or a light butter rub—must prevent sticking, especially bottom layer."
- "Layer time. Start with half the frozen hash browns spread evenly. Next, crumble in half the cooked sausage or ham. Evenly distribute half the onion–pepper–chile mixture. Top this layer with one cup of shredded cheddar."
- "Repeat layers exactly: remaining hash browns, meat, veggies, and finish with last cup of cheese on top. The cheese seals moisture, forms a browned, slightly crispy crust during cook."
- "Pour the egg mixture slowly, aiming to cover all layers. Some soak in, some rest on top—it’s supposed to be loose. Avoid pouring too fast or unevenly; might create dry spots or soggy pools."
- "Toss lid on. Cook on HIGH for about 3 to 4 hours; edges should be set and bubbling slightly. Or low for about 7 to 8 hours, better if overnight. No peeking unless necessary, lets the steam do its magic."
- "Test doneness by poking the center with a fork or skewer; eggs firm, not runny. The hash browns should be tender but with slight bite, no ice crystals leftover. Remove lid carefully, sprinkle freshly chopped green onions or chives. Hot sauce if you want a little extra punch."

tips:
- "Start with hot oil in skillet. Toss diced onions and peppers in once oil shimmers. Listen for sizzle; peppers soften, don’t mush, about 5-7 mins. Garlic powder, salt, pepper added near the end keeps flavors bright. Fold green chiles in last, preserves their zing without burning. Cook just till translucent. No limp mush allowed."
- "Whisk eggs with whole milk and Dijon mustard thoroughly. Mustard cuts egg richness, stops rubbery texture. Air bubbles in mix help fluffiness later. No lumps. Slow pour this gently to avoid dry spots or soggy puddles. Eggs should coat layers, not drown them. Layering evenly key to moist, tender center."
- "Grease slow cooker thoroughly. Spray or butter. Non-stick crucial, especially on older pots. Bottom layer needs protection from sticking and burning. Use liner for extra security but spread grease if no liner. Stuck edges ruin casserole texture and cleanup."
- "Layer hash browns first, half the bag, spread evenly. Follow with half cooked sausage or ham crumbles. Add onion-pepper-chile mix evenly next layer. One cup shredded cheddar seals moisture on each repeat. Cheese crust forms browned edge when done. Finish top with cheese. Avoid overcrowding layers, balance moisture and texture."
- "Cook low overnight for 7-8 hours or high 3-4 hours. No peeking during cook to keep steam intact. Edges should bubble and brown lightly. Test doneness with fork poke; eggs firm, center not runny. Hash browns soft but still hold some bite. Let rest ten mins after cooking for binding. Garnish green onions or chives, optional hot sauce for punch."

- "q: How to avoid soggy casserole bottom? a: More hash browns bottom layer. Spread thin and evenly. Frozen potatoes need drying; water pools cause gluey texture. Grease slow cooker bottom well. - "q: What if eggs are runny in center? a: Cook longer low or high. Fork poke test best. Runny means not firmed yet. Pour egg mix slowly next time; even coverage helps. Could be slow cooker temp variations. Let sit ten mins after cooking, carryover heat finishes eggs."
