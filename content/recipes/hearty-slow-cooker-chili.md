ingredients:
- "1 lb ground turkey (lean, good swap for ground beef)"
- "1 medium onion, diced"
- "3 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, rinsed roughly"
- "1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed roughly"
- "1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes with juice"
- "1 cup chicken broth (low sodium)"
- "2 tbsp tomato paste (adds depth, avoid direct ketchup substitute)"
- "2 tbsp chili powder (fresh batch if possible)"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika (for subtle smoky twist)"
- "1 tsp ground cumin"
- "1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to heat preference)"
- "1/2 tsp dried oregano"
- "1/4 tsp ground black pepper"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Optional toppings: shredded cheddar, sour cream, chopped green onions"
instructions:
- "Brown the ground turkey in a skillet over medium heat until mostly cooked through with pink just disappearing; avoids grainy texture in slow cooker. Drain excess fat if needed but keep some for flavor."
- "Transfer browned turkey to slow cooker; add diced onion, minced garlic, drained beans, canned tomatoes with juice, chicken broth, and tomato paste. Stir together gently but thoroughly, making sure tomato paste is well incorporated to avoid clumps."
- "Sprinkle chili powder, smoked paprika, cumin, cayenne, oregano, black pepper directly over mixture. Season with moderate salt but wait until near end to adjust final saltiness."
- "Cover slow cooker and set to low. Cook approximately 7.5 hours—not rigid. Watch edges: gentle bubbling from sides with savory small steam puffs escaping; that’s slow cooking talking. Too watery? Remove lid last 30 minutes to thicken or cook uncovered for short bursts."
- "Stir once or twice during cooking, enough to prevent sticking but not overly disturb fragile beans breaking down slowly. Resist poking too often—each stir dilutes heat distribution."
- "Test doneness by spooning thick sauce over beans and turkey, texture should be creamy around beans but not mushy. Aroma should be deep, earthy with faint smoky notes. If tomato brightness too harsh, add a pinch sugar to mellow out acidity."
- "Before serving taste and add salt as needed. Chili thickens further once off heat. If too thick, stir in small amounts of broth; too thin, cook uncovered briefly or add thickening slurry."
- "Serve in bowls topped with a handful of shredded cheddar for melting richness, a dollop of sour cream for cool tang, and chopped green onions for bite and color contrast."
- "Leftovers benefit from resting overnight in refrigerator—flavors meld, texture firms up. Reheat gently on stovetop uncovering to reawaken aromas and resettle thickness."
