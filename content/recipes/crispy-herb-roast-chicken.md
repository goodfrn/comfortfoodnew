ingredients:
- "1 whole chicken, about 4-5 pounds"
- "1/2 cup softened unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon dried poultry seasoning"
- "1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped"
- "1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves"
- "Kosher salt"
- "Freshly ground black pepper"
- "Assorted vegetables (carrots, onions, potatoes), cut into chunks"
- "Cooking spray"
- "Cooking twine"
instructions:
- "Start by preheating oven to 475 degrees F. High heat key here."
- "Prepare a large roasting pan, coat with cooking spray so vegetables don’t stick."
- "Mix softened butter with poultry seasoning, rosemary, thyme; fork works best to combine evenly."
- "Rinse chicken if not prepped for crispy skin; dry thoroughly with paper towels. Moisture kills crispness."
- "Loosen skin carefully with fingers, especially around breast and legs. Use spoon back for hard spots."
- "Rub half the butter mixture under skin, spread evenly. Put any extra butter into cavity; flavor boost."
- "Season outside of chicken liberally with kosher salt and cracked black pepper. Salt is flavor and drying agent."
- "Set chicken breast side down in pan. Truss legs loosely—don’t tie tight, gives even roast without crowding."
- "Scatter chopped vegetables around bird; use leftover herbs if you have them, aromatics pump flavor."
- "Roast for 35 minutes. Listen for sizzling, aromatics coming alive. Skin starts turning amber."
- "Using tongs, flip chicken breast side up carefully. Don’t lose those precious juices in the pan."
- "Toss veggies gently to coat them in drippings and butter. They brown faster this way, caramelization is key."
- "Continue roasting at 450 degrees for about 35-45 minutes more. Internal temp should reach 165°F in thigh."
- "Look for deeply golden skin, juices running clear when pierced between leg and body. Wiggle test—legs should move freely."
- "Remove chicken; very important step—allow resting for 12 minutes. Juices redistribute, meat firms but stays juicy."
- "Grab a baster if making gravy—collect pan drippings now, rich fat, herbal goodness. Otherwise, start carving right away."
- "Carve with confidence—breast slices juicy, skin crisp with spice. Veggies tender, absorb savory fat."
- "Serve hot, maybe a squeeze of lemon if you like punch. Leftovers reheat well; skin loses some crisp but meat stays moist."
