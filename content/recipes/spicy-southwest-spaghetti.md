ingredients:
- "12 oz spaghetti or substitute linguine for silkier bite"
- "1 lb ground beef or ground turkey for leaner version"
- "1 cup diced yellow onion"
- "3 cloves garlic minced fresh, or 1 ½ tsp garlic powder if pressed"
- "1 cup fire-roasted diced tomatoes canned, drained of excess juice"
- "½ cup pinto beans rinsed and drained canned; black beans work too"
- "1 cup frozen corn thawed; can replace with fresh kernels"
- "1 can Campbell's Mexican Style Tomato Soup; alternatively use 1 cup salsa verde"
- "½ cup medium-spicy salsa; swap for pico de gallo to reduce heat"
- "1 medium avocado peeled, diced"
- "3 green onions sliced thin"
- "1 cup sharp cheddar shredded; Monterey Jack for milder melt"
- "1 tsp olive oil"
- "¼ tsp ground cumin optional, layered flavor"
- "Salt and pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Bring large pot of salted water to rolling boil. Add pasta, stir occasionally to avoid sticking. Cook until tender with bite, around 9–11 minutes depending on brand. Drain, toss with tiny drizzle olive oil to prevent clumping. Set aside."
- "Heat skillet over medium heat; add ground beef (or turkey). Break up meat with wooden spoon, stir constantly for even browning. Watch color carefully—should be deep brown, not gray with pink spots. Drain excess fat to keep dish from greasiness."
- "Return pan to stove; add olive oil, onions, garlic cloves, plus garlic powder. Stir well so flavors bloom together. Sauté until onions soften fully, translucent, and just starting to caramelize, 6–8 minutes. Garlic aroma emerges strongly before turning bitter—avoid burning here."
- "Add cumin now if using, toast the spice with meat mixture for 30 seconds. Pour fire-roasted tomatoes, pinto beans, and corn into the pan. Stir in canned tomato soup or salsa verde as substitute, mixing until fully combined. Splash in half-cup salsa; thickens sauce and balances acidity."
- "Bring all contents gently to simmer—tiny bubbles forming at edges—reduce heat slightly. Let flavors meld for about 10 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking or burning on bottom. Taste and season with salt, pepper. If sauce is too thick, add splash of water or broth."
- "Combine cooked pasta with beef sauce directly in skillet or large bowl. Toss thoroughly so each strand carries flavor. Transfer to broiler-safe dishes or plates."
- "Top pasta with diced avocado chunks—adds cool creaminess against warm, spicy sauce—and sprinkle sliced green onions evenly for mild sharpness."
- "Scatter shredded cheddar cheese atop entire surface. Place under hot broiler for 45 seconds to 1 minute, watching carefully. Cheese should bubble and melt, edges just starting to brown—not burnt. This quick finish creates irresistible gooey topping."
- "Serve immediately. Crunchy green onions, creamy avocado, melted sharp cheddar—each bite a balanced texture combo. Leftovers reheat well on stovetop, adding splash water to refresh sauce if dry."

tips:
- "Brown meat thoroughly. Deep color signals Maillard reaction done; gray patches mean undercooked. Drain fat well or dish slides greasy, kills layers. Wooden spoon breaks clumps better than spatula. Watch close, stirring nonstop feels overkill but saves texture."
- "Onions soften on medium low heat. Rushed? Garlic bitterness hits fast — smell changes first, sharp, almost acrid. Pull pan back if needed. Cumin toast right after onion softening, under 30 seconds. Burn it and all goes bitter. Broken spice granules release aroma when heat’s just right."
- "Pasta timing spells difference between sticky glue mess or strands that bite. 9 to 11 mins varies by brand and altitude. Salt scares starch sticking. Stir often first 3 minutes till tender edges appear. Add small olive oil drizzle after draining to keep separation; slips better in sauce."
- "Salsa choices shift heat spectrum—mild blends smoother but here medium spice works well. Tomato soup adds thickness without chopping but shifts sweetness slightly heavier. Salsa verde less sweet, more herbal, swap based on mood. Pinto beans || black beans swap equals texture change—creamy vs earthy."
- "Broiler cheese step demands vigilance. Cheese melts fast; edges brown first and bitter scorched bits ruin finish. Keep eye, 45 seconds tops depending on broiler strength. Let cheese bubble and stretch before cooling. Cool avocado diced last, lime juice helps keep color if prepping ahead."
faq:
- "q: How to avoid greasy meat? a: Drain fat fully, splash olive oil later if needed. Fat left in pan drowns flavors, makes sauce heavy. No drain means oily mess. Alternatives like turkey leaner but watch dryness."
- "q: Can I skip cumin? a: Yes, but missing that warm earthiness. Toast spice in pan for depth, quick 30 seconds max. No cumin means milder flavor, swap with smoked paprika for smoky note if wanted."
- "q: Sauce too thick or thin? a: Thin by adding water or broth in small amounts. Too thick? Salsa splash helps, or tomato soup thickness spot adjustment. Watch simmer bubbles, slow them down to balance texture with flavor punch."
- "q: Best way to store leftovers? a: Cool first, airtight container in fridge. Reheat on stovetop low adding splash water for smooth sauce. Microwave works but risk drying; covered container holds moisture better. Can freeze but texture changes on thaw."
