ingredients:
- "2 1/8 cups shredded sweetened coconut flakes, divided"
- "1 cup sweetened condensed milk"
- "3/4 cup powdered sugar"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "25 pecan halves"
instructions:
- "Set aside about half a cup of the coconut flakes for rolling later; use a coarse, medium texture for better cling rather than finely shredded or shredded too long."
- "In a medium bowl, mix sweetened condensed milk, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and salt until just combined. No beating; aim for gentle folding for even distribution without fluffing air in. Pour in remaining coconut flakes."
- "Fold and press coconut into milk mixture with spatula or spoon. This isn’t stirring to fluff but more compacting to pack coconut firmly. Texture should be sticky and hold shape when pressed. If too wet, add a couple tablespoons more coconut flakes."
- "Using a 1 1/2 tablespoon scoop or balanced heaping spoon, scoop out mixture. Press one pecan half in center with fingers. Wrap the coconut mixture around nut, pressing and rolling in palms. Should be tight. It’ll feel dense when done."
- "Roll formed ball in the reserved coconut flakes. This coats sticky ball and prevents clumping together later. Glittery, slightly dry outer layer."
- "Repeat for remaining mixture; should make about 25-30. Balls unlike snowballs. Firm, sweet, aromatic with subtle nut oils."
- "Store in airtight container. Keeps fridge about a week. They dry out if left out too long. For softer, swap pecan for almond or peanut halves. For twist, toss a pinch of cayenne pepper into mix—heat punches through sweet."

faq:
- "q: Why do my balls fall apart? a: Usually moisture imbalance. Too wet and balls won’t hold, too dry and crumbly. Add coconut flakes bit by bit to fix wet mix. Press nuts tightly into centers, keep mixture dense. Avoid over folding, that traps air and weakens structure."
- "q: What if I don’t have pecans? a: Use almonds or walnuts or macadamias—texture and flavor shift but works. Nut choice changes crunch feel, oil content varies. Toast nuts lightly if raw to deepen aroma. No nuts—try dried fruit chunks but results not same firmness or crunch."
- "q: How to store these snacks? a: Airtight container biggest. Fridge works best, a week max. Room temperature dries balls to chalky texture in days.
