ingredients:
- "12 oz elbow macaroni or similar pasta"
- "3 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "2 1/2 tbsp all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/2 cups whole milk, plus 1 1/4 cups milk or reserved pasta water (total 2 3/4 cups liquid)"
- "2 cups cubed roasted butternut squash"
- "2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "Salt about 1 1/2 tsp"
- "Black pepper 3/4 tsp"
- "1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese"
- "1/2 tsp smoked paprika (substitutes for nutmeg twist)"
instructions:
- "Start pasta in boiling salted water; cook until al dente — usually 9-11 minutes depending on brand and altitude. Drain completely but save 2 1/2 cups pasta water in a large measuring cup before draining the rest. If pasta water not reserved, swap with whole milk for creamier texture."
- "In a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat, melt butter carefully — bubbles rising but not browned. Quickly sprinkle and whisk in flour to form a paste. Whisk continuously; about 20-25 seconds max, no browning — this keeps roux from tasting nutty or burnt."
- "Gradually pour 1 1/2 cups milk or reserved pasta water into roux, whisking vigorously to avoid lumps. Mixture thins out noticeably, thinner than Alfredo sauce but thicker than plain milk."
- "Add roasted cubed butternut squash next, then stir in remaining 1 1/4 cups milk or pasta water. Heat gently to warm all through, small bubbles on edges but not boiling."
- "Use immersion blender or transfer to blender carefully to puree fully; texture has to be silky smooth, no chunks. This step is key – no shortcuts. If too thick, add splash more milk. Sauce should coat spoon lightly and flow."
- "Return blended sauce to pot on low heat. Fold in shredded sharp cheddar cheese by handfuls, stirring till each batch melts entirely before next. Once fully glossy and stretchy, season with at least 1 1/2 teaspoons salt and 3/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper. Smoked paprika adds subtle smoky depth as a twist here, balancing sweetness of squash."
- "Toss drained pasta into sauce off heat; stir gently to coat. Sauce will thicken as it cools. If it looks stiff, add small splash of warm milk or pasta water to loosen."
- "Serve topped with freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Can broil for minute or two for crusty top but optional."
- "Leftovers reheat well on stovetop adding splash milk or water to restore creamy texture if dried out."

faq:
- "q: Can I skip reserving pasta water? a: You can swap with whole milk but texture changes. Pasta water gives starch; keeps sauce lighter, silkier. Milk adds richness but heavier. If none saved, add milk gradually watching thickness. Always test sauce viscosity with spoon; sauce will cling rather than slide off."
- "q: What if sauce gets lumpy? a: Usually roux cooked unevenly or liquid added too fast. Whisk constantly during flour step; add milk/pasta water slowly, mix vigorously to avoid clumps. Blend to smooth if chunks persist. Strain sauce if needed. Also, some lumps vanish on reheating with extra liquid added."
- "q: How to fix sauce that’s too thick? a: Add warm milk or reserved pasta water in small splashes. Stir well, reheat gently. Thick sauce won't coat pasta right, feels pasty. Patience key here; liquid adjustments done gradually. Avoid cold dairy right in; temp shock causes graininess in cheese. Recheck texture tipping spoon."
- "q: How to store leftovers? a: Store in airtight container fridge up to 3 days. Sauce thickens; reheat on stovetop low heat, add splash milk or water to loosen. Microwave works but stir often to prevent drying. Can freeze, but sauce thinness may alter. Consider freezing pasta and sauce separate for better thawing."
