ingredients:
- "3 cups Rice Chex cereal"
- "3 cups Crispix cereal"
- "1 cup pretzel twists"
- "1 cup bagel chips, broken into pieces"
- "0.75 cup dry roasted peanuts"
- "0.75 cup unsalted butter"
- "0.5 cup hot honey (see substitutions)"
- "0.75 teaspoon fine sea salt"
instructions:
- "Set oven rack middle height and preheat to 280°F not 275 to gently crisp without burning edges. Line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment - no foil, it sticks. Better cleanup."
- "Combine Chex, Crispix, pretzels, broken bagel chips, and peanuts in a large bowl, something big enough to toss without crushing cereal."
- "Melt the butter slowly—microwave or stovetop—avoid browning but hot enough to blend nicely. Whisk in hot honey and salt immediately after melting."
- "Instead of tossing cereal in butter mixture directly, pour dry from one bowl into second bowl while drizzling the honey butter slowly between them. This minimizes crushed bits. No one likes dust in the mix."
- "Spread mixture evenly on baking sheet in a single layer. Overlapping causes uneven baking and soggy patches. Thin layer is key here for crunch."
- "Bake 22 minutes watching edges for light golden shimmer and to hear crisp crackle when you shake sheet gently. Remove, stir gently with spatula or wooden spoon, scraping bottom to flip bits."
- "Back in oven 12 more minutes until toasted fully, nuts smell roasted, some pieces slightly golden but not burnt. If you see too dark spots, reduce heat next time or pull earlier."
- "Cool completely on sheet for maximum crunch. Warm mix is soft and soggy—cooling seals crisp texture."
- "Store airtight, preferably glass or thick plastic container away from humidity - will keep for about 2 weeks but eats fast in my house."
- "If nut allergies or ingredient limits, swap peanuts for sunflower seeds or toasted pumpkin seeds. Bagel chips can be replaced by rye chips or small crunchy pita pieces for a different bite."
- "Hot honey is main flavor vector but can replace with spicy maple syrup or mix honey with dash cayenne for DIY. I once tried chili garlic honey—crazy good but watch heat."
- "Tossing gently isn’t just about broken cereal—prevents coating clumps which bake unevenly. Got this tip from too many failed batches."
- "If mix smells burnt—pull earlier, oven varies. Look for shiny butter coating and light golden edges, not deep brown. Smell is dead giveaway."
- "Some like adding a dusting of smoked paprika after baking for smoky hit. Don’t bake with spice dust—it burns. Add once cooled and toss in bowl."
- "Leftover snack mix sometimes soggy? Might be storage humidity. Try quick re-toast in toaster oven for a few minutes to revive crunch."
- "Avoid stirring while warm beyond the initial toss. Breaks cereal and softens coating."
- "Don’t skip parchment, sticky mess otherwise. Better than cooking spray or oil."
- "If shorter on time, bake 15 minutes first and toss, then 8 minutes more. Watch closely."
- "For nuttier flavor, toast the nuts separately in dry pan until fragrant before mixing in. Adds depth."
- "Butter amount can be adjusted for lighter coating but less adhesion for flavor."
- "Salt level depends on preference. Use kosher salt for crunch or fine salt to blend. Taste raw honey butter first."
- "To test doneness, poke piece lightly. Should sound hollow and snap cleanly."
- "Bagel chips can vary in thickness. Adjust bake timing accordingly."
- "If you want a sweeter finish, sprinkle powdered sugar after cooling—odd but nice contrast to spicy heat."
