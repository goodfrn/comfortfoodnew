ingredients:
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 cup light corn syrup"
- "1 cup raw peanuts, toasted"
- "3 tablespoons coconut oil (swap for butter)"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "Nonstick cooking spray"
- "Aluminum foil to line pan"
instructions:
- "=== Prep Pan ==="
- "Line a large baking sheet with aluminum foil. Spray foil thoroughly with nonstick spray—go heavy here or brittle sticks like a nightmare. Set aside."
- "=== Combine Sugar Base ==="
- "In a large microwave-safe glass bowl, stir together sugar and corn syrup evenly. Crowded bowl means bad swirl later—mix well."
- "=== First Microwave Phase ==="
- "Microwave mixture on high about 4-4.5 minutes. Watch closely. Sugar will bubble aggressively. No sugar stones should linger. Bubbling means near melting point—start smells caramelizing. Remove carefully."
- "=== Add Nuts and Fat ==="
- "Quickly add toasted peanuts and coconut oil. Toast nuts beforehand on dry skillet till fragrant, a golden tan, adds crunch and deeper aroma. Coconut oil swaps butter for lighter yet tropical twist. Stir promptly with heatproof spatula to mix; keep spatula sprayed or sticky mess."
- "=== Second Microwave Phase ==="
- "Return bowl to microwave for 3 to 5.5 minutes. Watch caramel deepening to amber-dark color between 300-310°F range—the hard crack stage. If no thermometer, look for thick liquid pulling away from sides and darkening. Smell shifts from sweet sugar to nutty burnt sugar. Overcook = bitter; undercook = chewy brittle."
- "=== Add Flavor and Aerate ==="
- "Remove from microwave. Stir in vanilla extract then baking soda. Mixture will foam and bubble rapidly. Stir JUST enough to blend soda but don't lose those bubbles—the airy texture, the classic brittle crunch, depends on this foam. Over stirring kills texture and flattens brittle."
- "=== Spread and Cool ==="
- "Immediately pour the foamy mix onto prepared foil pan. Use silicone spatula sprayed with nonstick spray to spread quickly and evenly. Thick spots cool hard too fast, causing uneven cracks. Let cool COMPLETELY, no touching—hard and brittle to the touch. Then break into bite chunks."
- "=== Storage Tips ==="
- "Store in airtight container. Brittle absorbs moisture fast, gets sticky. If stuck, toss with fresh rice grains in container overnight—they absorb humidity and restore crunch."
