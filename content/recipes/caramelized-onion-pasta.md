ingredients:
- "6 tbsp unsalted butter, divided"
- "5 large yellow onions, thinly sliced"
- "1 tsp salt, plus extra to taste"
- "1/4 cup dry white wine, optional for deglazing"
- "4 cloves garlic, minced"
- "2 sprigs fresh thyme, chopped"
- "1/2 tsp red chili flakes"
- "2 tbsp all-purpose flour"
- "4 cups beef broth, room temperature"
- "1 lb dried pasta (fettuccine or tagliatelle preferred)"
- "4 oz mascarpone cheese, softened"
- "Fresh grated Parmesan or Pecorino cheese, for serving"
- "Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish"
instructions:
- "Butter melt in heavy skillet or wide saucepan over medium-high. Watch carefully to avoid browning."
- "Add onions in handfuls, stirring often, seasoning lightly with salt early to draw moisture. They should look glossy and start softening after 5 minutes."
- "Lower heat to medium-low once softened. Patience essential here. Slow caramelizing for 50-60 minutes. Stir every 3-4 minutes. Look for deep amber hue, jammy texture, no burning."
- "If brown residue sticks to pan, splash wine or broth to loosen it, scraping bottom to get all flavor bits."
- "While onions work, boil salted water for pasta. Cook until just al dente, firm with slight bite."
- "Reserve two cups pasta water before draining. Set pasta aside."
- "Add garlic, thyme, chili flakes to onions. Stir until fragrant, about 1 minute. Not too much heat or garlic burns bitter."
- "Sprinkle flour over onions, stirring well to coat everything evenly. Cook 2 minutes to get rid of raw flour taste. Flour helps thicken sauce later."
- "Gradually pour beef broth, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Simmer gently 3-4 minutes until slightly thickened."
- "Toss pasta into skillet with onions. Pour in half cup reserved pasta water. Stir to combine, pasta soaking in sauce."
- "Add mascarpone next with another half cup pasta water. Stir over low heat to melt cheese completely, coating strands creamy and rich."
- "Taste now. Adjust salt, pepper. Add more pasta water if sauce feels thick or clumpy, aiming for luscious texture that clings but moves."
- "Serve with generous sprinkling fresh grated cheese and parsley for bright finish."
