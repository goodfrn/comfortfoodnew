ingredients:
- "2.5 pounds ground beef or lean ground turkey"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil optional if using lean meat"
- "1 packet taco seasoning chili powder cumin garlic powder blend"
- "1 cup chunky salsa medium heat"
- "2 (9.25 ounce) bags Fritos or Doritos"
- "1 (15 ounce) can black beans drained and rinsed"
- "Optional toppings shredded lettuce diced tomatoes sliced jalapenos sliced black olives chopped green onions sour cream sliced avocados shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
instructions:
- "Heat skillet on medium-high, add oil only if using lean turkey or chicken. Wait until oil shimmers or meat sizzles on contact."
- "Add ground meat in one even layer, let sit 2-3 minutes without stirring to develop slight crust. You’re looking for that faint crackling and browned edges. Then start breaking into chunks with spatula."
- "Keep stirring occasionally, scraping browned bits from pan bottom. Cook until no pink remains, about 6 minutes but don’t just rely on time—check color and firmness."
- "Drain excess fat or liquid well. Return beef back to hot pan, turn heat down medium."
- "Toss in taco seasoning packet and salsa plus drained black beans. Stir and fold to coat every morsel. Look for thickened sauce, louder sizzling sounds, faint tang from spices warming up."
- "Taste, adjust seasoning if needed because packets vary. Sometimes a pinch of smoked paprika helps or splash of lime juice brightens the mix."
- "Remove from heat once mixture glistens and bubbles slow down. Should cling nicely to meat and beans without drowning them."
- "Fill each Fritos or Doritos bag directly, no plates, no fuss. Top bravely—cheese, lettuce, creamy sour cream, or even pickled jalapenos for fire."
- "Serve immediately. Crunch versus tender meat contrast is key now. Messy but worth it."
