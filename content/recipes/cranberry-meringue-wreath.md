ingredients:
- "4 large egg whites"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar"
- "1 tablespoon cornstarch"
- "3/4 cup fresh cranberries"
- "1/4 cup fresh squeezed orange juice"
- "1/4 cup light brown sugar"
- "1 teaspoon cornstarch"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg"
- "pinch salt"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "powdered sugar for dusting"
- "fresh mint leaves or orange peel strips for garnish"
- "optional twist substitution: replace apple cider vinegar with lemon juice"
instructions:
- "=== Pavlova Wreath ==="
- "Set oven to 230 degrees Fahrenheit and position rack on lower third. Parchment paper on baking sheet ready."
- "Whip egg whites in a chilled bowl or stand mixer on medium speed till frothy, opaque, holds soft peaks, about 3-4 minutes."
- "Add sugar slowly, spoonful by spoonful, beating continuously. Whites brighten, become slick, shiny with stiff peaks by 6-7 minutes total. Watch closely for graininess, stop once smooth and glossy."
- "Stream in vanilla, vinegar or lemon juice, and cornstarch while mixer runs on low. Keeps meringue stable and sets texture. Whip additional 2 minutes to incorporate but avoid overbeating; if mixture falls apart it's too late."
- "Spoon roughly third cup portions spaced evenly. Use backside of spoon or cup to hollow centers forming little nests. These craters hold cream and sauce."
- "Slide pan into oven. Two hours baking, but watch for subtle golden edges and firm feel. Crisp to touch but still light. If meringues brown too fast, lower heat or tent with foil."
- "When out, crack oven door ajar, leave meringues inside until fully cool and dry. This prevents cracks from rushing the cool air."
- "=== Cranberry Topping ==="
- "Combine cranberries, orange juice, brown sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt in saucepan over medium heat."
- "Simmer gently stirring often. Sugar dissolves, berries pop, sauce thickens enough to coat a spoon’s back fully—look for slight translucency in liquid, not runny."
- "Remove from heat. Cool before assembly or it warps meringue texture."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Form cooled meringue rounds into a circle on serving plate. Spoon cold whipped cream (lightly sweetened, whipped to soft peaks) into each hollow."
- "Top with cranberry compote spooned precariously but prettily. Dust with powdered sugar like fresh snow."
- "Garnish with fresh mint or curled orange peel for color and zing. Serve immediately—meringue gets soggy fast."
- "==="
- "Tips: Egg whites aged a day in fridge at room temp whip better. No yolk allowed; even a drop kills volume. Use fresh cranberries—frozen bring excess water altering compote thickness. For quick vinegar substitute lemon juice but expect subtle flavor change."
- "If baking more than two hours, check every 20 minutes after first hour to prevent overbrowning. Store meringues separate from moisture; topping goes on at last minute."
- "Whipped cream can be stabilized with gelatin or cream cheese for standing time, but adds tang."
- "To rescue overbeaten whites turning grainy, add a teaspoon of cold water and whisk by hand till smoother; risk of fail, but can sometimes help."
- "Watch the visual cues over timers. Crisp edges, glossy smooth tops, dry bottoms, and hollow centers with no sticky feel mean done."

tips:
- "Egg whites at room temp whip faster; cold slows down. Separate carefully; yolk ruins peak stability. Add sugar gradually. Watch texture shift from frothy to shiny stiff peaks, stop with no graininess or overbeating."
- "Cornstarch in meringue and sauce crucial. Captures moisture, stabilizes. Without it, meringue softens during bake. Sauce too runny means not enough cornstarch or undercooked. Thick but not gluey is goal here."
- "Oven temp set low—around 230 Fahrenheit. Higher dries edges too quick, cracks. Watch edges turn faintly golden; surface firm yet light to touch. If browning too fast, tent foil or lower heat a few degrees mid-bake."
- "After baking, crack oven door ajar, leave meringues inside until cool. Sudden cool-down here leads to cracks. Slow drying inside oven finishes texture, keeps surface intact. Avoid fridge right after baking to limit moisture."
- "For vinegar substitute lemon juice for subtle tartness; flavor shifts, structure holds. Whites whip well if aged a day in fridge but brought to room temp before starting. Whipped cream: soft peaks only, overwhip risks butter formation."
