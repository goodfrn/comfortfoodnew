ingredients:
- "1 1/2 lbs chicken breasts boneless skinless"
- "1 cup long grain white rice rinsed"
- "1 3/4 cups chicken broth substitute water for lighter taste"
- "1 cup chunky salsa replace original medium salsa with spicy for kick"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "3/4 tsp salt"
- "1/2 tsp black pepper freshly ground"
- "1 1/2 cups shredded Mexican blend cheese cheddar mozzarella combo"
instructions:
- "Oven to 395F light spray on 13x9 inch dish. I fiddle with temp down from 400F to avoid crust burning before rice cooks through. Dish size key, too small traps moisture too much."
- "Lay chicken breasts flat in center. Surround with dry rice corn kernel size spread, not piled."
- "Pour warm chicken broth over rice. Avoid displacing rice with liquid splash. Season chicken: garlic powder, salt, pepper. Use fingertip pinch, no overuse or bitter."
- "Spoon salsa thickly over chicken. Don't saturate rice, keep direct flavors balanced. Using spicy salsa adds heat, chose medium if timid about fire."
- "Seal tightly with foil, snap edges under dish, no steam escapes. Bake 52-57 minutes. Rice should swell, look tender, poke with fork to check softness."
- "Chicken done when juices run clear or thermometer hits 160-165F in thickest part. Slight wiggle but firm to touch. Avoid overbaking—dry chicken sucks."
- "Pull from oven, lift foil carefully—steam releases slow to avoid burns. Scatter cheese evenly across chicken and rice top."
- "Return uncovered for 3-6 minutes watching cheese carefully. Melt until bubbly and just turning golden. Burnt cheese ruins whole batch in seconds."
- "Let rest 5 minutes before serving. Rice tightens, flavors settle. Chicken juice redistributes, keeps moist."
- "Serve with lime wedges or fresh chopped cilantro if you want brightness mid-bite."
