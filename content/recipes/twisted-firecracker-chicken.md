ingredients:
- "1 large chicken breast cut into 1 inch pieces"
- "2 tablespoons cornstarch slightly adjusted for coating"
- "2 eggs beaten"
- "1/4 cup vegetable oil for frying"
- "1/3 cup chili garlic sauce substituted for buffalo wing sauce"
- "2 tablespoons sugar"
- "1 tablespoon rice vinegar swapped for apple cider vinegar"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes"
- "Chopped green onions for garnish"
- "1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 395°F instead of 400 for gentler bake but still bubbles."
- "Line a rimmed baking sheet with foil and spray with oil spray. Foil traps juice making cleanup easier; spray prevents sticking."
- "Cut chicken breast into approx 1 inch pieces, uniform size helps even cooking."
- "Sprinkle cornstarch over chicken in a large bowl. Toss so every piece gets a thin coat—too much clumps, too little no crunch."
- "Beat eggs in small bowl nearby. After cornstarch dust, working in batches, drop 3-4 pieces into egg mix. Shake excess cornstarch off first—helps sauce stick better."
- "Arrange coated chicken on plate. Repeat until done."
- "Heat vegetable oil in wide skillet over medium-high. Should shimmer not smoke. Ideal temp around 350°F if you have a thermometer."
- "Add half the chicken pieces—spread out, no touching or overcrowding. Crowding cools oil, leads to greasy soggy pieces."
- "Fry each side about 1-2 minutes first side, 1 minute flip. Don’t fully cook here. Just a light crust forms. Chicken still raw inside but sealed envelope for baking."
- "Listen for soft sizzle sound from oil tipping off frying."
- "Drain on paper towels to absorb extra oil. Repeat with remaining chicken."
- "Make sauce by whisking chili garlic sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, salt, red pepper flakes. Taste for balance. Too much sugar? Add splash more vinegar."
- "Place fried chicken in single layer on your foil-lined sheet. Slow pour sauce evenly over chicken."
- "Use tongs or spoon to gently toss chicken so sauce coats every piece. Work quickly so sauce doesn’t pool too much on pan."
- "Bake 22-27 minutes. Sauce should bubble with edges caramelizing—a sticky glossy coating forms. Too long and chicken dries out; too short and sauce won’t thicken."
- "Halfway through bake, toss chicken gently on sheet to redistribute sauce and prevent sticking."
- "When done, remove from oven. Sauce thickens further as it cools—shiny and clings beautifully."
- "Transfer to serving dish with any juices. Sprinkle with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds for crunch and bite."
