ingredients:
- "3-4 rib sections cut from a full rack, 3 ribs each"
- "2 tbsp paprika"
- "1 tbsp chili powder"
- "1 tbsp brown sugar"
- "1 tsp ground cumin"
- "1 tsp black pepper"
- "1 tsp kosher salt"
- "1 tsp garlic powder"
- "1 tsp onion powder"
- "1 tsp apple cider vinegar powder (sub for celery salt)"
- "Water or apple juice for oven pan steaming"
- "Vegetable oil or cooking spray for grill"
- "BBQ sauce, homemade or store-bought"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Cut ribs into manageable chunks, 3-4 ribs each, sharp cleaver essential. Beware slipping bones, watch fingers closely. Smaller batches speed marinade penetration."
- "Preheat oven to 210°F not 200. Slightly higher to speed steam without drying edges."
- "Dry rub: In a bowl, blend paprika through apple cider vinegar powder. Swap vinegar powder here for subtle zing; celery salt made things too salty previously."
- "After washing hands thoroughly, rub each rack portion heavily, meaty side only. Don’t pile ribs flat; spread with space so steam circulates. Crowding kills crust formation."
- "Rack ribs on center oven shelf. Below on lowest rack, fill a rimmed baking pan with water or—better—apple juice. Apple juice helps caramelize sugars in steam, but risks rub washing if poured directly on meat."
- "Bake ribs for around 2 hours 50 minutes to 3 hours, checking color and meat shrinkage. Meat edges should pull back, skin growing tacky but not falling off yet. Steam prevents dryness but locks in connective tissue break down too."
- "While ribs steam, prep a BBQ sauce. Homemade, thick with some vinegar tang. Hold for later glazing."
- "After steaming, heat grill to medium-high. Spray or brush grates with veg oil to stop sticking. Wait for grill to hiss when ribs hit."
- "Place ribs meaty side down initially, listen for sizzle. Cook 6-8 minutes until grill marks brown. Flip to meaty side up; begin brushing with BBQ sauce every 2-3 minutes for about 15 minutes total. Thin layers build sticky glossy bark rather than swamp rub."
- "Final pass, give ribs a heavy slather. Allow sauce to coke slightly but not char black. Remove when edges caramelize, ribs wiggle tender but still hold shape."
- "Served hot with more sauce. Cracks in surface, aroma sharp and sweet. When you hear the sauce bubbling and smell smoke, you’re close. Not mushy but yielding."
