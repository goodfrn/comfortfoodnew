ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups cooked shredded chicken breast"
- "3/4 cup chunky salsa, drained if very watery"
- "1/2 cup Greek yogurt, full fat (substitutes cream cheese)"
- "1 cup shredded pepper jack cheese (substitutes sharp cheddar)"
- "1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste"
- "Tortilla chips, for serving"
instructions:
- "=== Mix it up ==="
- "Grab a medium bowl. Add shredded chicken. Chunkiness matters here. No mushy piles. Toss in salsa; listen for gentle plopping, no watery pools. Greek yogurt next, creamy, a little tangy twist replacing traditional cream cheese heaviness. Sprinkle cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder now — these spices punch up the base, no bland slop allowed. Fresh cracked pepper goes in; I skip salt sometimes depending on salsa saltiness."
- "=== Cheese time ==="
- "Fold in shredded pepper jack. You want peppery heat sneaking in, melted, but no need to microwave right away — flavors get better if it sits a couple minutes. Cheddar was fine, but pepper jack adds character. Mostly personal preference; use whatever cheese you have but sharper cheeses hold up better here."
- "=== Serve it strong ==="
- "Spoon into a rustic bowl, no need for fancy. Scatter chopped cilantro on top, bright green color contrast. Serve with sturdy tortilla chips or crisp veggies. Listen for crunch bites when dipped. A little messy, a little spicy, a little creamy — that’s the sign it’s done right."
- "=== Troubleshooting tips ==="
- "Too wet? Drain salsa more next time or pat chicken dry if store-bought moist. Flavor dull? Increase cumin and paprika slowly, taste mid-mix. No steam/microwave ready? Just mix and serve room temp; it’s alright — flavors open up either way. Want it warm? Quick 20-second zap in microwave, stir, repeat until cheese melts but not rubbery."
