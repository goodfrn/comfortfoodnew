ingredients:
- "6 cups cubed challah bread (about 1.5-inch pieces) — substitute brioche or day-old white bread"
- "3 large tart apples, peeled and diced — try Granny Smith or Honeycrisp for tartness"
- "6 large eggs, whisked thoroughly"
- "2 1/4 cups half & half — can swap with coconut cream for dairy-free"
- "1/2 cup light brown sugar (packed)"
- "2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted plus 6 tbsp for greasing and drizzling"
- "1 1/2 tsp apple pie spice blend (cinnamon, nutmeg, clove) — homemade mix recommended for freshness"
- "1 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "1/4 tsp salt"
- "For caramel sauce:"
- "4 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup half & half"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "pinch salt"
instructions:
- "Grease a 9x13 baking dish with about 2 1/2 tablespoons of melted butter. Use a pastry brush to coat sides and bottom evenly; helps avoid sticking and ensures browning."
- "In a large bowl, vigorously whisk the eggs until no streaks remain. Add half & half, brown sugar, vanilla, apple pie spice, and salt. Combine thoroughly for a smooth custard base that soaks evenly."
- "Spread half the cubed challah over the bottom of the dish in an even layer. Don’t overcrowd; give bread pieces room to absorb custard."
- "Distribute half the diced apples evenly over the bread. Apples release moisture; layering helps even cooking."
- "Add remaining cubed challah on top, then scatter the rest of the apples over."
- "Slowly pour custard over the layered bread and apples. Use a large spoon’s back to press down any dry cubes, making sure every piece is soaking."
- "Melt 4 tablespoons butter, drizzle evenly on top of the pudding. Fat on the surface creates a golden crust."
- "Cover tightly with foil and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. This rest time lets bread absorb custard fully, developing rich flavor; skip and risk dry or underdone center."
- "Preheat oven to 350°F. Remove pudding dish from fridge, let sit 20 minutes at room temp before baking, especially important for glass dishes to avoid breakage and promote even cooking."
- "Place foil-covered pudding in oven; bake 45-50 minutes until edges are bubbly and set but slight jiggle in center remains."
- "Remove foil, bake uncovered additional 25-30 minutes or until surface turns light golden brown and a knife inserted in center comes out clean with moist crumbs, not wet custard."
- "While baking finishes, prepare caramel sauce: melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add granulated and brown sugars. Stir constantly, cook 5-7 minutes until bubbling and temperature hits 215°F to 220°F. Watch closely—too hot burns sugars fast."
- "Remove from heat; slowly whisk in half & half, vanilla, and salt. Should be smooth, glossy. Use pot holders and caution; hot caramel can cause severe burns."
- "Once pudding is out of oven and barely warm, drizzle most of caramel sauce evenly over top. Reserve about ½ cup for serving at table."
- "Allow bread pudding to sit 10-15 minutes to absorb sauce, slice into squares. Serve warm with extra caramel, maybe whipped cream or toasted pecans if desired."
