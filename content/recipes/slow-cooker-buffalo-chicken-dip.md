ingredients:
- "1 small yellow onion sliced"
- "1 ½ cups chicken broth"
- "3 boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "6 ounces softened cream cheese"
- "½ cup ranch dressing substitute Greek yogurt mixed with dried dill and garlic powder"
- "½ cup Frank's RedHot original sauce substitute any vinegar-based hot sauce"
- "1 ½ cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese substitute pepper jack for extra kick"
- "1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
instructions:
- "Lay sliced onion thick on bottom of slow cooker, like a bed. Pour broth evenly over it, so onions start braising."
- "Nestle raw chicken right on top. Don't crowd but cover well. High heat setting, about 2 hours 45 minutes. Watch for chicken turning opaque and juicy but still holding shape. When juices run clear, ready."
- "Remove chicken gently. Place on plate. Shred with two forks—if a few chunks resist, even better texture. Take out onions from cooker. Drain broth completely; don’t leave soggy mess."
- "Turn slow cooker to low. Set timer around 35 minutes but keep an eye. Toss shredded chicken back inside. Add cream cheese chunks, dollops of ranch or Greek yogurt mix, hot sauce splash, shredded cheddar, and mozzarella."
- "Give everything a rough stir. Don’t worry about lumps of cream cheese, they’ll melt right into cheesy goo. Smell that? Onions are gone but depth remains."
- "Cover and let it bubble gently. Once cheese fully melted and dip looks glossy, almost sticky, test a spoonful. Too spicy? Swirl in extra yogurt or ranch. Need more zing? Drizzle extra hot sauce."
- "After 30 to 35 minutes, when dip is thick and chewy, ready to scoop. Best served warm. Chips, celery sticks, or crackers are key. Leftovers cold are hesitant but reheated? Even better."
- "Pro tip: Want sharper dip? Add minced garlic at shred stage. Cream cheese too crumbly? Microwave 10 seconds before mixing."
- "If broth runs low and chicken sticking, pour a little extra chicken broth or water to prevent burning. Onions can brown but avoid bitterness by careful sweeping before adding cheese."
- "If short on time, cook chicken separate in instant pot or poach then skip onion base—add chopped caramelized onions instead for aroma punch."
