ingredients:
- "3 ounces softened cream cheese"
- "1 1/2 tablespoons mayonnaise"
- "2 tablespoons sour cream"
- "1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground white pepper"
- "1 cup shredded smoked gouda cheese"
- "1 pound lump blue crab meat"
- "16 large egg roll wrappers"
- "1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water for egg wash"
- "Vegetable oil for frying (3-4 inches deep)"
- "FOR DIPPING SAUCE"
- "3/4 cup mayonnaise"
- "1 1/2 tablespoons Sriracha"
- "1/2 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning"
- "1/4 teaspoon coarse kosher salt"
- "Sliced scallions for garnish"
- "Extra Sriracha aioli or plain Sriracha for drizzle"
instructions:
- "=== Make Crab Mixture ==="
- "Beat softened cream cheese until quite creamy, almost silky. Dollop in mayo and sour cream, fold carefully. Texture should be rich but not too loose—adjust mayo if too wet."
- "Add Worcestershire, Old Bay, white pepper. Stir until evenly speckled; smell sharp briny hints, not overwhelming."
- "Gently fold in shredded smoked gouda, notice that sweet smoky aroma layering in. Then hand-fold lump crab meat—try not to break chunks apart too much."
- "Set aside. Crab mixture will firm slightly as it chills."
- "=== Assemble Egg Rolls ==="
- "Arrange a wrapper diamond-shape under your hand. Keep unused covered under damp towel; dry edges tear easily."
- "Brush edges with egg wash—room temp is easier to dab on. Spoon about 1/2 cup crab mix towards center but not too close to edges or bursting will occur."
- "Fold bottom up snugly, then fold sides inward. Roll firmly but don’t squeeze crab filling out. Tight enough to hold but not over-stretched."
- "Transfer rolls to parchment-lined tray. Pop straight into freezer minimum 1 hour or up to 2 months wrapped tightly with parchment between layers. Freezing stops filling leaking while frying."
- "=== Fry Egg Rolls ==="
- "Heat vegetable oil to about 300°F. Use a kitchen thermometer—too hot? Shell burns, crab inside cold. Too cool? Greasy, soggy rolls."
- "Fry 2-3 rolls at a time. Listen to the bubbling—not frantic chatter, gradual steady sizzling."
- "Flip after about 2 minutes or once bottom is golden. Cook until all sides are light golden brown without rushing. Texture should snap on break, not bend or feel soft."
- "Drain on wire rack over paper towels to catch excess oil. Don’t let sit too long or crust softens from steam."
- "=== Make Dipping Sauce ==="
- "Whisk mayonnaise with Sriracha, Old Bay, kosher salt until uniform and creamy. Adjust spice level to your nerve—add more Sriracha for fire."
- "Cover and chill at least 15 minutes to let flavors meld."
- "Serve egg rolls warm with sliced scallions for freshness and drizzle with Sriracha aioli or plain Sriracha for heat boost."
- "=== Notes and Serving ==="
- "Crab mix keeps a day or two in fridge but best fresh or frozen. If no lump crab, claw meat can substitute but expect less texture contrast."
- "Use smoked gouda for a subtler, richer cheese note, or sharp cheddar if preferred but might overwhelm delicate crab."
- "If wrappers tear, wet towel too cold or too dry. Hand warmth and dexterity count here. Egg wash acts like glue, don’t skimp."
- "Freeze well for grab-and-fry snacks. Defrost not needed, fry from frozen for crisp surface."
- "Adjust frying temp visually: bubbling too vigorous spells overheat—turn down burner. You want a happy moderate bubble, like steady boil, soft crackling sound."
- "Leftover sauce keeps a week refrigerated. Stir before use; Sriracha settles."
- "Savor the crunch then gentleness of crab inside. Scallions add snap of green freshness complementing rich fat and spice."
- "No timers fix all in crispy frying. Watch color, listen to the sizzle, and feel weight in spatula to judge doneness. Avoid soggy edges or burnt crisp."
- "Careful with oil reuse: filter strain; discard once smoked or rancid aroma appears."
