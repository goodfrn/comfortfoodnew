ingredients:
- "4 pork chops about 1 inch thick"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil or avocado oil"
- "2 medium onions sliced thin"
- "3 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves or ½ teaspoon dried thyme"
- "1 tablespoon tomato paste"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1 ½ cups chicken broth or bone broth"
- "½ cup apple cider or dry white wine"
- "1 tablespoon honey or pure maple syrup"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 410F to compensate for slight longer cooking in braise"
- "Pat pork chops dry to avoid steaming instead of searing; season both sides with salt and pepper liberally"
- "Heat oil in large heavy skillet—cast iron best—medium-high heat until shimmer, not smoking"
- "Place chops in single layer, no crowding, sear about 2 ½ to 3 minutes per side, edges golden, crust forming, resist moving them"
- "Transfer chops to a warm plate, cover loosely with foil to keep juices trapped"
- "Lower heat to medium; add sliced onions, cook slowly, stirring often, about 7 to 12 minutes until translucent and rich golden-brown with some softly charred edges"
- "Add garlic, thyme, tomato paste, and cumin; stir constantly for about 90 seconds to awaken spices, smell that earthiness developing"
- "Pour in broth plus apple cider and honey, scraping browned bits off pan bottom—those brown bits are flavor bombs—bring to quick simmer"
- "Nestle pork chops back into skillet, pressing gently into liquid but only partially submerged to avoid stewing"
- "Transfer skillet to oven uncovered; cook 40 to 55 minutes until broth reduces at least half and pork registers 145F internal temp; check around 40 minutes to avoid dryness"
- "Alternatively, cover skillet and simmer gently on stove over low heat for same duration, stirring once mid-way"
- "If sauce reduces too quickly and risks burning, add splash more broth or water"
- "Rest pork chops tented for 5 minutes before serving to let juices redistribute"

- "Onions need low and slow. Don’t rush caramelization. Stir often, watch edges gently darken, sweet smell signals ready. If onions burn black, flavor turns bitter. Add garlic and spices only when onions soft and translucent, garlic burns fast, ruins taste if added too early."
