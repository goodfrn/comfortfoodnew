ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2 tsp baking powder"
- "1/3 cup honey"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1/2 tsp ground ginger"
- "1/2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1/4 tsp ground nutmeg"
- "1/4 tsp ground cardamom"
- "1/2 cup coconut oil melted"
- "1/2 cup milk"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 3/4 cups finely grated carrots"
- "4 oz cream cheese softened"
- "1 egg yolk"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/4 cup sugar"
- "1/3 cup chopped toasted pecans"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar clumps broken up"
- "3 tbsp all-purpose flour"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 340 Fahrenheit. Line 24 muffin cups with liners — buy sturdy paper ones or grease if flimsy."
- "Whisk flour, baking powder, salt, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg and cardamom in a bowl. Small sieve works to break lumps. Set dry mix aside."
- "In mixer or big bowl, blend melted coconut oil, milk and egg aggressively but don’t overbeat. Just until merged."
- "Add dry slowly to wet. Stop immediately once no dry pockets remain. Batter looks lumpy but thick, don’t fuss. You want texture, not rubber."
- "Fold in grated carrots with spatula; no mixer here. Carrots give moisture and body. If too wet, add a spoon flour."
- "Scoop heaping tablespoon batter into liners. Not full to rim — gives room for rise. Trust me, overfilled means messy topple."
- "Beat cream cheese with fork or hand mixer until faintly whipped. Add egg yolk, vanilla, sugar. Blend till cream just combines; not overly liquid."
- "Drop small dollop cream cheese mix into center of each muffin batter cup. Resist mixing it in, let it ooze when baked."
- "Mix pecans, brown sugar and flour in little bowl, crushing brown sugar lumps to break evenly. More crumble means extra crunch. Use walnuts if preferred but toast ’em first."
- "Sprinkle streusel over each muffin top sparingly. Too much can dry edges or burn during bake."
- "Bake around 23 to 27 minutes. Oven watches vary — dome tops spring back to finger’s touch when done, golden brown edges. Toothpick test optional; expect crumbs not wet batter."
- "Cool on wire rack 10–15 minutes. Muffins settle, edges firm up but cream cheese center remains creamy with slight crust."
- "If muffins sink, likely over-mixed or oven too low. Next batch, fold slower, watch your oven’s hot spots. Frozen carrots not recommended — watery mess."
- "Store in airtight container or freeze layers between parchment. Toast slightly before serving restores some fresh-baked bite."
