ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups buttermilk"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted plus extra for cooking"
instructions:
- "Start by cracking eggs, pour buttermilk and vanilla into blender. Pulse mix 2-3 times just to combine — nothing smooth. Gritty texture is fine."
- "In separate bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Makes sure no lumps."
- "Add half the dry mix to blender, pulse 2-3 times — lumps are good here. Then add remaining dry mix, repeat pulsing."
- "Pour melted butter in last. Pulse just until incorporated. Stop. Overmixing kills fluff."
- "Preheat griddle medium-low. Butter it generously."
- "Scoop 1/3 cup batter per pancake onto hot surface - thick batter visible. Keep space between pancakes."
- "Watch edges; tiny bubbles form and pop on top — that’s when it’s ready to flip. Look for browned edges firming up."
- "Flip carefully, hear sizzle, cook another 1-2 minutes until golden and set. Touch gently to test doneness, springs back lightly."
- "Serve immediately or keep warm in low oven."

faq:
- "q: Why is batter lumpy? a: Lumps good. Means less gluten development. Overmixing kills fluff. Lumpy batter traps air better. Pulse just a few times, don’t go for smooth. Texture affects rise and softness."
- "q: Can I use regular milk instead of buttermilk? a: Yes. Add 1 tablespoon vinegar or lemon juice per cup milk. Let sit 5 minutes until thickened slightly. Acidity reacts with baking soda. Works well but flavor slightly different. Keeps rise but less tang."
- "q: Pancakes turn out dense, what’s wrong? a: Usually overmixing or batter too thick. Mix dry ingredients well before adding, add in halves to avoid clumps but no overpulsing. Batter should be thick but pourable. Heat too high can also burn outsides too fast. Check griddle temp."
- "q: How to store pancakes? a: Cool completely. Wrap tightly or freeze flat in zip bags. Reheat in toaster or oven 300°F 5-10 mins. Avoid microwave if you want to keep edges crisp. Leftovers keep well but best within 2 days refrigerated. Wire rack on warm oven prevents sogginess if keeping warm."
