ingredients:
- "12 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1 package dry ranch dressing mix 28g"
- "2 tablespoons coarsely cracked black pepper or more to taste"
- "wax paper for rolling"
- "optional twist 1/4 cup sharp white cheddar shredded"
instructions:
- "Start with cream cheese at room temp. No cold lumps. If it's stiff, zap 10 seconds at a time in microwave, but no melting."
- "Toss in ranch mix. Stir vigorously with handheld mixer or sturdy spoon. Texture goes fluffy, not gritty—no lumps visible."
- "Mix in shredded sharp white cheddar if using. Adds tang and texture contrast. Learn from past fails: no blocky chunks or flavor won’t integrate."
- "Shape mixture roughly into a ball with hands or spatula. If stubborn, refrigerate 1-3 hours to firm up – easier to handle, less sticky."
- "Before rolling, spread cracked pepper on wax paper – make a generous layer. Don’t skimp—the pepper coats flavor and surface texture."
- "Roll cheese ball gently in pepper until surface is thickly coated. Press pepper on spots that don’t stick well. Add more pepper as needed."
- "Wrap tightly in wax paper, then plastic wrap. Refrigerate at least 4 hours, better overnight. Flavor melds, texture firms, aroma deepens."
- "Remove from fridge 20 minutes before serving to soften slightly. Cheese ball should be firm but spreadable. Press with finger to test."
- "Serve with pita chips, stalks of celery, sliced radishes, or buttery crackers. Observe how pepper crust catches light and adds rough texture."
- "Leftovers keep wrapped tight, up to 4 days in fridge. Freeze not recommended—cream cheese changes texture when frozen."
