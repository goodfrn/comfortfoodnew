ingredients:
- "1/3 cup honey"
- "1/2 cup low sodium soy sauce"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 small yellow onion chopped"
- "2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "3 large boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "2 teaspoons cornstarch"
- "2 tablespoons cold water"
- "toasted sesame seeds for garnish"
instructions:
- "Honey, soy sauce, garlic, onion, vinegar, ginger, pepper. All in the crockpot. Whisk it. Honey sticks to the bottom if you don't."
- "Chicken goes in. Flip it. Onion pieces on top. Lid on."
- "Low heat. Go do something else. Check around 3 hours. Poke it with a fork. Falls apart easy — done. Still tough — wait more."
- "Take the chicken out."
- "Pour the liquid through a strainer into a pot. Toss the onion bits."
- "Heat the sauce till it bubbles. Cornstarch in cold water, stir till smooth. Pour it in slow. Whisk the whole time."
- "Stir till it gets thick and shiny. Low heat two more minutes."
- "Shred the chicken. Half the sauce on it. Mix it around."
- "Rice. Chicken. Rest of the sauce. Sesame seeds."
- "Need to swap something — tips are below."
introduction: "Ok so. My sister made this crockpot teriyaki chicken once and I literally stood in the kitchen waiting for it to be ready. Just the smell. You mix stuff in the crockpot, throw the chicken in, and leave. Come back three hours later and it's just done. The sauce though — first time I made it myself I just poured the liquid straight on the chicken. Watery. Kinda sad. You gotta strain it then heat it up with cornstarch. Gets all thick and shiny. Sticks to everything. That's the good part. I used to use red wine vinegar. Switched to apple cider one day because that's what was in the house. Tasted better. Lighter. Kept doing it. Don't stress the time too much. Around 3 hours just poke it. If it just falls apart you're good. If not leave it. Toast the sesame seeds too. Like 30 seconds in a pan. Trust me it's different."
ingredientsNote: "Low sodium soy sauce. Not regular. The sauce cooks down and gets stronger — regular soy makes it way too salty. Mince the garlic small. Big pieces don't mix in right. Yellow onion not white. Sweeter. Better here. Ground ginger not fresh. Fresh gets sharp after hours of heat. Cornstarch goes in cold water first. Always. Substitutions in the tips."
instructionsNote: "Whisk before the chicken goes in. Honey sinks. Uneven flavor if you skip it. Onion on top of the chicken looks random but it drips into everything while it cooks. Strain it out after. Fork test over timer. Always. Thick chicken in a slow cooker can take longer than you think. Strain the liquid before you thicken it. Bits in the sauce make it weird. Toss the chicken in the sauce while it's hot. Soaks in better."
tips:
- "Whisk the sauce first. Honey sits at the bottom. Doesn't mix itself. One minute of whisking and the whole thing tastes even. Skip it and some bites are too sweet some too salty."
- "Three hours is just when you start checking. Poke the thick part. Pulls apart easy — done. Still hard — ten more minutes. Check again. Every crockpot is different."
- "Cornstarch in cold water first. Stir till fully smooth. Then pour it slow into the hot sauce. Whisk the whole time. Dump dry cornstarch straight in and it clumps. Can't fix that."
- "No apple cider vinegar — white vinegar works. Rice vinegar works too. No honey — maple syrup is close. Brown sugar also works but sweeter so use less soy."
- "Toast the sesame seeds. Dry pan. Medium heat. 30 seconds. Shake so they don't burn. Raw ones taste like nothing. Toasted ones actually taste like something."
