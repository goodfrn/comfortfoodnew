ingredients:
- "15 ounces dry stir fry rice noodles"
- "2 teaspoons vegetable oil"
- "1 pound boneless skinless chicken thighs, sliced thin"
- "½ white onion, thinly sliced"
- "1 clove garlic, minced"
- "½ cup light soy sauce"
- "2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lime juice"
- "½ teaspoon ground ginger"
- "1 tablespoon honey"
- "½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes"
- "12 ounces shredded cabbage mix (substituted for broccoli coleslaw)"
- "1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds"
- "¼ cup scallions, thinly sliced"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Hydrate noodles just shy of soft; al dente means still a slight chew, not mush. Drain and rinse under cold water fast to kill cooking heat. Toss with 1 tablespoon sesame oil to keep strands from gluing together. If coated too soon, strands get slippery; best right before mixing."
- "Heat 2 teaspoons vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat till you feel the shimmer but not smoking. Add thin slices chicken thighs. Chicken gets better flavor with thighs; lean breast dries. Brown on edges, about 7 minutes, flipping often to get all sides golden. Chicken shouldn’t look pink inside; firm but moist."
- "Add thinly sliced white onion and the minced garlic. Onions go translucent, sweet aroma rises, garlic should not burn—stir quickly 2-4 minutes till just softened. Burnt garlic tastes bitter. Onion acts like buffer, slows chicken drying out."
- "Whisk soy sauce, sesame oil, fresh lime juice, honey, ground ginger, and crushed red pepper flakes in a small bowl. This sauce balances salty, sweet, earthy, tart, and spicy. Honey tames sharp lime, ginger adds earthiness, chili wakes the whole mix up."
- "Toss noodles, chicken, onion mixture, and shredded cabbage mix into the pan. The heat here is low—just enough to warm through and combine. Stir constantly, so noodles don’t stick to pan or to each other. Watch for cabbage to slightly wilt but keep crisp edges, about 2-3 minutes."
- "Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds and sliced scallions over everything. The sesame seeds crackle softly under tooth; scallions add sharp, fresh punch that cuts through richness. Serve immediately once scallions look vibrant and not wilted."
- "Cleanup tip: Noodles stick if left in water too long or overcooked. Toss immediately in oil after draining. Chicken cooks fast; slice thin even pieces to avoid drying. Onion and garlic timing crucial to avoid bitterness or raw pungency. Sauce can be doubled for extra flavor push or halved for subtlety."
- "Store leftovers separately if possible. Noodles will clump if left together overnight. Reheat gently with splash of water to loosen."
