- "3 1/2 pounds apples, cored and cut into 1-inch pieces"
- "1 tablespoon lemon juice"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons ground nutmeg (instead of cinnamon sticks used originally)"
- "1/2 cup apple cider (substitute for water)"
instructions:
- "Start by removing cores from apples, peel if you want that velvet texture but I often leave peel for rustic bits. Cut into roughly 1-inch chunks. Don't fuss over uniformity — uneven cuts cook just fine."
- "Drop apple chunks in your slow cooker. Splash lemon juice over to keep color bright. Sprinkle nutmeg evenly, skip cinnamon sticks for a change — nutmeg gives that punchy warmth without stringy spice bits. Pour cider in place of water for more body and flavor complexity."
- "Cook on HIGH for around 3 hours 15 minutes. You’ll hear bubbling and smell sweet orchard scents building. Stir once or twice mid-cook to gauge softness and mingle flavors, but it's not mandatory. Apples will fall apart easily once ready, but test by poking chunks. They should give with light pressure but not be mushy yet."
- "Once soft, turn off heat. Use a potato masher for chunky applesauce, giving a homely, lumpy texture with bits of peel visible. For smoother but still rustic, an immersion blender works well — just pulse gently. Want a velvet puree? Peel beforehand and blend fully after cooking with a blender or food processor."
- "Taste and adjust. Add a pinch of salt or more lemon juice if that sharp brightness feels missing. If too tart, stir in a touch of maple syrup or brown sugar. Serve warm or chill; flavors meld best once cooled."

faq:
- "q: Should I peel apples or not? a: Depends on texture wanted. Peel for silky smooth puree; no peel for rustic chunkier with fiber bits. Peeling needs extra step but smoother blend. Try both, you can mash unpeeled too just chunkier mouthfeel."
- "q: Can I use water instead of apple cider? a: Yes but expect less flavor depth. Cider adds natural sugar and aroma, water safe backup. Maybe add more spice if bland. Some like traditional water version, just watch sweetness and acid balance."
- "q: Why does my applesauce get gritty? a: Usually from over-blending peels. Peels fibrous - blend gently or peel prior to cooking. Over pulse with immersion blender makes gritty texture. Alternatively mash for chunkier, less risk gritty feel."
- "q: How to store leftover applesauce? a: Refrigerate covered few days, freeze in portions for months. Use airtight containers. Thawed sauce maybe thinner, stir well. Leftovers good for baking add-ins or breakfast topping. If thickening needed, simmer briefly uncovered on stove."
