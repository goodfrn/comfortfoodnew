ingredients:
- "2 1/3 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon cinnamon"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "1 cup brown sugar, packed"
- "1/2 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled"
- "1 whole large egg"
- "1 large egg yolk"
- "1 tablespoon vanilla extract"
- "1 1/4 cups old-fashioned rolled oats"
- "1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "1 cup toasted chopped walnuts"
- "1/2 cup sweetened shredded coconut"
instructions:
- "Whisk together flour, cornstarch, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg in medium bowl. Set aside."
- "In stand mixer bowl, combine brown sugar and granulated sugar."
- "Set mixer on low; slowly pour in melted, cooled butter. Watch texture turn sandy, moist but crumbly."
- "Once incorporated, add whole egg. Let it mix fully before adding egg yolk and vanilla, one after another. Crucial to emulsify well here."
- "Add flour mix in three distinct doses. Pause mixer, scrape sides with spatula each time before next addition."
- "Dough feels sticky but firm now. Fold in oats, chocolate chips, walnuts, coconut on low just till spread evenly. Overmix and cookies toughen."
- "Cover bowl with plastic wrap; refrigerate 40 to 50 minutes. Dough firms, easy to portion, flavors meld more deeply."
- "Heat oven to 345°F (175°C). Line baking sheet with parchment paper or silicone mat."
- "Scoop dough with 2-inch cookie scoop; space 6-8 balls on sheet. Press tops gently with fingertips to flatten slightly. Helps bake evenly, edges crisp, middles soft."
- "Bake 10 to 13 minutes. Watch for golden edges, centers just set but still soft. If edges brown too fast, reduce heat next batch."
- "Let rest 3 to 5 minutes on sheet; cookies fragile now. Transfer to wire rack to cool completely or fudge-like centers persist."
- "Repeat with remaining dough, keeping leftover balls refrigerated."
