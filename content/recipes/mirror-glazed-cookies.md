## Why You'll Love These Mirror-Glazed Cookies

Takes 55 minutes total—35 to prep, 20 in the oven. Fast enough for a weeknight dessert but looks like you spent actual time.

Vegan. Uses aquafaba instead of egg. The glaze comes out shiny anyway, mirror-smooth, with that fake yolk center that makes people stare. Tastes buttery. Genuinely. Most vegan cookies taste like nothing dressed up. These don't.

Crispy on the edges. Chewy in the middle if you underbake slightly. The thin cookies—that 0.4 cm thing—snap when you bite. Stays that way for two days if you don't eat them all first.

Lime instead of lemon. Less harsh. Balances the sugar without screaming at you. Works cold. Works at room temperature. Works basically everywhere.

No special equipment. Just a mixer, an offset spatula, your regular oven. The mirror glaze looks complicated. Isn't.

## What You Need for Vegan Lemon Cookies

Unsalted butter. 150 ml. Softened, not melted. Room temperature matters here.

Granulated sugar. 200 ml. Regular white sugar. That's what makes them crispy, makes them sweet enough that the lime doesn't overpower.

One large egg yolk—wait, no. One large egg yolk if you're not vegan. If you are, skip this entirely and go straight to aquafaba. The lime zest goes in either way. Grated from one lime. Fresh. Don't use the bottled stuff.

All-purpose flour. 400 ml. Sift it. I know that sounds annoying. Do it anyway. Clumps ruin the texture.

Baking powder. 7 ml. Just that much. Kosher salt. One pinch. A real pinch.

For the glaze that makes them shine: powdered sugar—900 ml. Aquafaba from two cans of chickpeas, so roughly three quarters of a cup. You're not using the chickpeas. Just the liquid. Vanilla extract. 5 ml. A turmeric pinch for color, because egg-free means no yellow naturally.

Water. Small amount. You'll add it if the glaze gets too thick, which it probably will.

## How to Make Mirror-Glazed Cookies

Start with the butter. 150 ml softened. Cream it with the sugar for exactly three minutes. The mixer changes sound halfway through—that's the signal. You're adding air. That air is what keeps the cookies tender, not tough. Don't skip this part by mixing by hand. It won't work the same way.

Add the egg yolk (or skip it entirely if you're vegan and doing straight aquafaba). Beat for 30 seconds. Add the lime zest. The zest goes in now because you want those oils distributed throughout, not sitting in chunks. Lime is sharper than lemon. Cuts through the sugar. Works better here.

Sift your flour with the baking powder and salt. This step matters. No shortcuts. Mix the dry into the wet with a wooden spoon. Don't use the mixer. Don't use your hands. Wooden spoon. Just until you can't see any white streaks. The second you can't see them, stop. Overworking makes tough cookies. You don't want tough.

Form a log. About 6 cm around. Smaller than you'd normally make. Tighter log means it chills faster, more evenly. Wrap it tight in plastic. Refrigerate for two and a half hours. It needs to be firm to touch, cold but not rock solid. Test it by pressing—there should be slight resistance.

## How to Get Crispy Lemon Cookies with That Glossy Mirror Glaze

Heat the oven to 175°C. Middle rack. The slightly lower temp bakes slower, gentler. The glaze will actually stick and cure properly instead of sliding off hot cookies.

Slice the log thin. 0.4 cm thick. Thinner slices crisp faster. They crack less when you cut them. Dampen your knife between cuts. Prevents dragging the dough. Arrange on parchment. Space them out. Not touching.

Bake for 18 to 20 minutes. Watch the edges. The slightest caramel tinge at the very edge—that's done. The top stays pale. The edges tell the story. Cool completely on a wire rack. This matters. If you glaze hot cookies, the glaze melts and slides. You want it to adhere, to cure into that mirror finish.

The glaze itself: powdered sugar with aquafaba. Mix them together until you get something thick but spreadable. Not runny. Not paste. Somewhere between. Add vanilla extract. Add a pinch of turmeric for color—it mimics egg yolk better than anything synthetic. This is the base coat.

Reserve about 120 ml of this glaze in a separate bowl. Darker yolk color. Set that aside. Spread the rest over the cookie tops with an offset spatula. Even coating. No pockmarks. Let it dry until it's firm but still sticky to touch. About 10 minutes depending on humidity. Your kitchen might be wetter than mine.

Take the reserved darker glaze. Small spoon. Dollop the center of each cookie. The yolk. Not perfectly round. Slightly wobbly. That's what makes it look real. The yellow spots should stay shiny. Contrast with the matte white underneath.

Let everything cure at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. Don't put them in the fridge. Cold dulls the glaze. Breaks the texture. Ruins the whole thing. Same-day eating is best. Next day is fine. After that, the snap starts to fade.

## Vegan Aquafaba Glazed Lemon Cookies Tips and Common Mistakes

Dough softens too fast? Chill the baking tray for five minutes after slicing before it goes in the oven. Humidity is your enemy with glazes. If your kitchen is wet, use less water when mixing. Add it in tiny amounts. Stir. Test by spreading on a scrap. Better to be too thick than too thin. You can always thin it. You can't thicken it once it's runny.

Aquafaba varies. Some cans are thinner than others. Start with less water than you think you need. Always.

Slice thickness kills more cookies than anything else. Too thick and they stay doughy. The glaze won't crack. The cookie feels heavy in your mouth. Doesn't work. Go thin. 0.4 cm. Use a ruler if you have to.

Underbake if you want chewy. Bake full 20 minutes if you want crispy all the way through. Neither is wrong. Just know the glaze won't hold as long on a softer, damper cookie. It'll start to weep if you leave it out overnight.

Don't glaze hot cookies. I said it twice because it matters.

Substitute coconut oil for butter and you'll get less rise, no buttery flavor. It works but it's different. Not better or worse. Different.

The lime zest—don't skip it, don't swap for lemon. Lemon is too sharp here. Lime is balanced.

Powdered sugar quality matters. Cheap stuff has cornstarch that makes the glaze grainy. Spend a dollar more. You'll see the difference.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Mirror-Glazed Vegan Cookies

**How long does the glaze stay shiny?**
Same day, perfect. Next day still glossy. After that it starts to dull a bit. Humidity makes it worse. Keep them in an airtight container but don't seal them until the glaze is completely set, or condensation fogs the shine.

**Can I use regular eggs instead of aquafaba?**
Yeah. One egg white whisked until foamy, mixed with the powdered sugar. Different texture though. Aquafaba gives you that specific mirror finish. Egg white is more matte.

**What if my glaze is too thin?**
More powdered sugar. A tablespoon at a time. Stir. Test on a scrap of paper. Thin and spreadable is the goal. Thick enough that it doesn't run off the edge.

**The yolk spots are sliding around. What went wrong?**
Base coat wasn't set long enough. Wait the full 10 minutes before adding the darker glaze. It needs to be tacky, not wet. In humid kitchens that might take 15.

**Can I make these ahead?**
The dough, yes. Wrap the log, freeze it. Bake whenever. Slice straight from frozen, add maybe two minutes to bake time. Already baked and glazed? Eat within two days for best snap. After that they get soft.

**Why lime instead of lemon?**
Lemon is too aggressive. Lime is brighter without shouting. The sugar is already sweet. Lemon pushes it. Lime balances it. You can swap if you want. Just expect a different cookie.
