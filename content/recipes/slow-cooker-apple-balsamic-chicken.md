ingredients:
- "¾ cup apple jelly"
- "⅔ cup apple juice"
- "⅓ cup water"
- "3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar"
- "1 cup low-sodium chicken stock"
- "1 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1½ teaspoons salt"
- "1 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "½ teaspoon black pepper"
- "4 boneless skinless chicken breasts"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "2 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "½ teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "Fresh parsley for garnish, optional"
instructions:
- "In the slow cooker bowl, vigorously whisk the apple jelly, apple juice, water, balsamic vinegar, chicken stock instead of bouillon, garlic powder, salt, thyme, smoked paprika, black pepper, and cinnamon. The cornstarch not added yet. The mixture should look glossy, a bit runny."
- "Nestle the chicken breasts into this liquid, flip once to coat well. Avoid floating breasts on top exposed to air; submerge as much as possible so they cook evenly."
- "Cover with a tight lid, cook on high for about 2 hours 45 minutes. Listen: a gentle simmer or soft bubbling. Not a rolling boil. Chicken should start blanching white but remain tender. Hot but not collapsing to shred at this point."
- "Remove the lid carefully, watch for foamy fat that rises to the surface. Skim off with a spoon; fat dulls sauce clarity and causes slick mouthfeel. If skimmed well, sauce tastes vibrant."
- "In a small microwavable bowl, melt butter then vigorously whisk in cornstarch until a smooth slurry forms. Slowly drizzle into the slow cooker while stirring gently, avoiding lumps."
- "Switch slow cooker to low heat, keep covered and cook additional 20 minutes. Watch sauce closely; it should thicken visibly, cling to the spoon. You want a satin nappe consistency, not gluey."
- "Test chicken texture by pressing; should yield easily but slice without shredding. If sauce too thin, add small cornstarch slurry increments. Too thick? Thin with a splash of apple juice or water."
- "Serve hot over mashed potatoes, egg noodles, or rice. Garnish with chopped parsley if desired. Aroma is a mix of sweet tart, warm balsamic, smoky paprika, a hint of cinnamon warmth; smells like cozy fall dinners."
- "Use leftovers wisely; sauce thickens up in fridge but loosens with reheating. Reheat gently to avoid drying chicken or breaking sauce."
