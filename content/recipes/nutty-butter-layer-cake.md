ingredients:
- "2 sticks unsalted butter"
- "2 1/8 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 1/4 cups granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs plus 2 egg yolks"
- "3/4 cup whole milk"
- "Vegetable shortening for pans"
- "Gelatin powder 1 tablespoon"
- "3 tablespoons cold water"
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "2 tablespoons coffee liqueur"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar (for mousse)"
- "1/8 teaspoon salt (for mousse)"
- "5 oz bittersweet chocolate, melted"
- "Powdered sugar for dusting"
instructions:
- "=== Cake ==="
- "Heat oven to 320°F. Grease two 8 inch round pans with shortening. Line bottom with parchment. Dust with flour, tap out excess. Keeps layers even, cakes clean to remove."
- "Melt butter slowly in saucepan over low heat. Stir often, watch milk solids shift from white specks to golden brown, smell shifts to toasted nuts. About 12 minutes here, not exact. Scrape butter plus browned bits into large heatproof bowl."
- "Set bowl in ice water bath to chill edges now. Butter softens but begins firming around outside, roughly 7 minutes. This step crucial for beating texture later."
- "In separate bowl, whisk flour with baking powder and salt. Measure precisely but flour here swapped to 2 cups cake flour plus 1/8 cup all-purpose for lighter crumb."
- "Retrieve butter from bath, scrape remainder and solid bits in. Transfer butter to room temp bowl, beat 4 minutes till creamy pale color, lightens notably. This traps air, builds volume—don’t skip or rush."
- "Add sugar plus vanilla. Beat medium-high until fluffy, about 3 minutes. Then beat in egg yolks one at a time, then whole eggs, mixing well after each. Should thicken smoothly, shiny texture."
- "Alternate adding dry ingredients and milk in 3 additions starting and ending with dry. Stir gently with spatula after each addition, avoid overmixing but no streaks."
- "Divide batter evenly into pans. Tap pans on counter to release any bubbles."
- "Bake middle rack 38-43 minutes total. Rotate pans halfway, watch golden tops with slightly firm spring back. Toothpick test—pull it out clean or mostly clean. Cool cakes 18 minutes in pans. Invert onto racks, peel parchment after fully cool."
- "=== Dark Chocolate Mousse Filling ==="
- "Sprinkle gelatin over cold water. Let hydrate 6-8 minutes till softened."
- "Whip cream until soft peaks form. Chill briefly until ready to fold in."
- "Warm coffee liqueur about 40 seconds in microwave. Stir in gelatin until fully dissolved. Scrape bowl walls or gelatin lumps carefully."
- "Beat egg yolks, sugar, salt on high until pale, thick, 4-5 minutes. Stream in coffee-gelatin liquid slowly while beating. Fold in melted chocolate, gently combine with rubber spatula."
- "Fold whipped cream in two additions, light and deliberate to preserve airiness. Mousse should be thick, glossy, spreadable after chilling 30-45 minutes but can soften at room temp if too firm. No microwaving mousse to soften; patience matters."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Place one cake layer on platter. Spread mousse generously but evenly, no peaks needed. Dust mousse surface lightly with powdered sugar for texture and balancing bittersweet mousse."
- "Top with second cake layer, press gently to secure but not squish mousse out."
- "Frost cake all over with chocolate buttercream (control thickness for neater slices). Final dusting of powdered sugar adds visual contrast and slight sweetness."
- "Rest cake chilled minimum 1 hour, flavors settle, mousse firms. Serve closer to room temp to let buttercream soften slightly."
- "=== Tips & Techniques ==="
- "Brown butter step—watch heat closely. Too hot burns; too cool no browning. Smell and color bigger markers than time. When browned bits clump and surface bubbles pop gently, done."
- "Butter beating after chilling rounds out the texture. Butter straight from pan too liquid, ruins volume."
- "Use bowl size wisely: eggs and sugar whipped in a room temp, dry ingredients sifted to avoid lumps."
- "Mousse gelatin step ensures stable set; skip & mousse falls flat. Filter dissolved gelatin through fine mesh if needed to catch stray bits."
- "Cake flour substituted gives a more delicate crumb than all-purpose."
- "If no coffee liqueur, substitute cold brew concentrate or omit and add 1 tsp espresso powder dissolved in warm water."
- "Rotating pans prevents uneven browning from oven hot spots. Better evenness in crumb."
- "Cool cakes in pans first to prevent collapse but not so long they steam and sog."
- "For frosting, temperature matters: too soft buttercream slides; too firm cracks. Beat to spreadable, not melting."
- "Mousse can be made ahead up to 24 hours but must be tightly covered; bring to room temp before using."
- "Use a sharp serrated knife to slice final cake for clean layers."
