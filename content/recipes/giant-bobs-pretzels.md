## What You Need for Homemade Soft Pretzels
Instant yeast. 12 ml. Not the brick kind.
Warm water — 280 ml. Not hot. Warm. Like bathwater.
All-purpose flour. 720 ml total. Don't mess with bread flour here.
Agave syrup. 25 ml. Sugar works if that's what you have.
Avocado oil. 25 ml. Olive burns too fast once the oven gets involved.
Boiling water. 3 liters. Comes from the kettle.
Baking soda. 175 ml. This is the secret. The boil bath.
Coarse sea salt. Not table salt. Not kosher. Coarse. It sticks.

## How to Make Homemade Soft Pretzels
Pour the warm water into a bowl. Add yeast. Stir gently until it gets frothy but still a bit cloudy — some sediment at the bottom is fine. Don't overstir it into oblivion.

Dump two-thirds of the flour into a big bowl. Add oil. Add agave. Add the yeast water. Mix it until dough forms. This takes a minute.

Use your hands now. Fold and press. Knead it. The dough should feel smooth after maybe eight minutes of actual work — not the whole two hours, just the kneading part. Shape it into a ball.

Oil the bowl lightly. Put the dough in. Cover it with a cloth. Let it sit for about 65 minutes. It'll double. Maybe more. The kitchen temperature matters — warm kitchen, might be 60 minutes. Cold kitchen, might be 75. Just watch it.

Turn the oven on to 230 C (445 F) while the dough does its thing.

## How to Get Crispy Pretzel Edges and Perfect Shape
Dust your work surface with the remaining flour. Cut the dough into six equal pieces. Roll each one into a thin rope about 1.5 cm thick — thinner than you think. They puff up.

Twist them into pretzel shapes. Or don't. Freeform knots work. Loops work. They don't have to be perfect.

Pour the baking soda into the 3 liters of boiling water. Swirl it around until it dissolves. This is hot. Actually hot. Be careful.

One pretzel at a time. Dip it in the soda water for 8 to 12 seconds per side — count it out or don't, just don't leave it in there forever or it gets weird and mushy. Lift it out carefully. Arrange on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Sprinkle coarse salt all over while they're still wet. The salt sticks.

Bake for about 7 minutes. Watch them. The edges go golden and start to crisp while the inside stays soft. Take them out before they go brown-brown. Golden is the target.

## Homemade Pretzel Tips and Common Mistakes
Don't skip the baking soda bath. This is what makes them pretzels and not just twisted bread. The alkaline changes the texture and crust completely. No shortcut here.

The yeast water should be frothy but not brown. Too hot and it kills the yeast. Too cold and nothing happens.

Rising time varies. 65 minutes is the average. Some rise faster. Check it.

If your pretzels come out doughy in the middle, the dough was too thick or the oven wasn't actually 230 C. Use an oven thermometer if you have one. Most ovens lie.

Coarse salt matters because it doesn't dissolve into the dough immediately — it sits on top and adds texture. Table salt sinks in and disappears.

Tried them cold the next day. Still good, but they're meant warm. Reheat them for 30 seconds if they've been sitting.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Homemade Soft Pretzels

**Can I make easy pretzels without letting them rise so long?**
Not really. That 65 minutes is when they develop flavor and get soft inside. Skip it and they're dense. Doesn't work.

**Are these vegan pretzels?**
Yes. Yeast, flour, water, oil, salt. No dairy, no eggs, no animal products anywhere.

**Can I freeze the dough or shaped pretzels?**
Shaped pretzels work better frozen. After you twist them, throw them on a tray, freeze solid, then boil and bake from frozen — add maybe 2 minutes to the bake time. Raw dough freezes but thawing it gets messy.

**What if I don't have agave syrup?**
Sugar works. Regular white sugar, brown sugar, honey — they all work the same here. The yeast needs something to eat. It's not about taste.

**Why does the baking soda bath change the pretzels so much?**
Changes the pH of the surface so it browns different and gets that specific chewy-crispy texture. Without it you just have bread shaped like a pretzel. The bath is the whole thing.

**Can I make soft pretzel buns instead of twisted pretzels?**
Yeah. Shape the dough pieces into buns instead of ropes. Same boil, same bake. Takes the same time. Works great for sandwiches.

**How long do homemade pretzels stay fresh?**
Day one they're perfect. Day two they're fine. Day three, rewarm them. After that they go stale and get hard. Not worth eating by day four.
