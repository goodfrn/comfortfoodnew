ingredients:
- "2 cups pumpkin puree"
- "1 package (3.4 oz) instant vanilla pudding mix"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg"
- "1/4 teaspoon ground allspice"
- "1 cup plain Greek yogurt (substitute for Cool Whip)"
- "extra ground cinnamon for topping"
instructions:
- "In a mixing bowl, dump pumpkin puree, the vanilla pudding mix and all the spices. Cinnamon is star here, don’t slack. Nutmeg adds warmth; allspice ties the whole vibe together. Stir until blended but don’t overmix or it’ll get granular."
- "Next, fold in Greek yogurt gently—slow and steady. Avoid beating; want creaminess, fluff, not soupy mess. The yogurt gives body, tang, and a natural lift. If you get impatient and whip it hard it turns runny, no good."
- "Dust some ground cinnamon over the surface. Looks rustic, smells like autumn memories. Cover tight with plastic wrap or container lid. Refrigerate for 30 minutes minimum. The cold sets pudding powder, thickens textures. The longer it chills, the better the flavors jive. Half an hour is bare minimum; if you can wait an hour, go for it."
- "When served, texture should be dense with air pockets—spoon sinks but holds shape, silk meets cloud. If it feels watery, you've either overfolded or the pudding powder was old. Use fresh and fold gently next time."
- "Variations: Swap spices for pumpkin pie mix if you find neat blends easier. Or add a splash of bourbon or vanilla extract for a grown-up twist. For vegan: tofu silken and coconut yogurt can replace dairy, but expect texture changes."
- "Problems to watch: Pumpkin puree hydration varies. Too wet? Pat it slightly with paper towels or reduce yogurt by a couple tablespoons to avoid watery fluff. Pudding mix consistency varies by brand—use instant, never cooked."
- "Efficiency trick: mix dry spices into pudding mix first so they distribute evenly. Saves re-stirring later. Folding yogurt last keeps mousse fluffy without sweating it."
- "No cook. No stress. Quick, chill, eat and repeat."
