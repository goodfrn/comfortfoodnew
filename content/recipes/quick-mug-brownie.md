ingredients:
- "0.32 cup flour"
- "0.31 cup sugar"
- "3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "pinch salt"
- "0.22 cup milk"
- "3 tablespoons coconut oil, cut in small cubes"
- "1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "Optional toppings: chopped nuts, peanut butter, whipped cream"
instructions:
- "Combine flour, sugar, cocoa powder and salt in a large coffee mug. Mix vigorously to blend but expect some powdery clumps—that’s okay. Dry mix first, trust me."
- "Add milk, cubed coconut oil and vanilla extract. Cubes melt more evenly with microwaving, avoid clumps of oil floating. Stir until most dry spots gone but batter a bit lumpy — butter remnants show richness."
- "Microwave on high for 25 seconds. Remove. Stir thoroughly — coconut oil should be glossy melted now, batter thickening. Smell chocolate warming."
- "Heat again for about 1 minute 15 seconds. Watch batter rise but not spill over. Check center with toothpick or finger — if gooey or sticky, nudge extra 10–15 seconds intervals. Don’t overcook or brownie hardens."
- "Let sit a minute out microwave — fudgy texture firms up here. Add toppings while warm — nuts crackle, peanut butter melts. Eat straight out of mug. Use spoon to scrape corners; no crumbs left behind."
- "If no coconut oil, use unsalted butter but melt and cube beforehand. Milk can switch to almond or oat; hydration and fat affect chewiness."
- "If too wet after cooking, batter may be overly wet from liquid. Add small spoon flour mix next time or shorten cook intervals."
