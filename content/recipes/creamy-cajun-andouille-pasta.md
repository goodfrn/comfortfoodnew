ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 medium green bell pepper diced"
- "1 small red onion diced"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "2 cups low sodium chicken broth"
- "8 ounces andouille sausage sliced (or turkey sausage for lighter)"
- "2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning"
- "1/2 cup heavy cream"
- "8 ounces cooked pasta (penne or rotini)"
- "1 cup cherry tomatoes halved"
instructions:
- "Heat butter in big skillet over medium heat. When it foams, throw in diced bell pepper and red onion. Stir, listen for gentle sizzle. After 3 minutes, veggies should soften but not collapse. Toss garlic last, keep stirring ~1 minute until fragrant but not brown."
- "While that cooks, whisk chicken broth steadily into flour in separate bowl. Aim for no lumps; smooth slurry that’s thin enough to pour easily but coats spoon thickly."
- "Add sausage slices to skillet. Let them brown 2-3 minutes per side until caramelized spots form. No crowding or they’ll steam not sear. Flip carefully, aromas will deepen."
- "Pour broth and flour mix into sausage pan. Stir vigorously. Liquid thickens fast, looks like a sauce coating bottom. Sprinkle Cajun seasoning evenly in, stir until blended. Taste at this point – adjust seasoning if too sharp or mild."
- "Pour in heavy cream slowly, stir constantly. Sauce turns creamy, silky. Avoid boiling now or cream could split. Texture should coat the back of spoon, thick yet pourable."
- "Add cooked pasta and tomatoes straight to skillet. Toss gently to combine. Tomatoes warm through, keeping shape and juiciness. Serve hot. Garnish optional but green onions add bite."
- "If sauce too thick, loosen with splash of pasta water or broth. Too runny? Simmer briefly, watch closely. Overcooking kills texture of veggies and pasta."
- "Substitution note: smoked turkey sausage cuts fat but keep spicy kick with extra seasoning. Bell peppers interchangeable for poblano or mild poblano for earthiness. Onion can be swapped with shallot for sharper aroma."
- "Common pitfalls: don’t brown garlic too long or it burns bitter. Flour slurry lumps make gritty sauce; whisk well. Pamper sausage heat to get crispy spots, not rubbery texture."
- "Listen for sizzle shifts, smell deepening aromas, watch sauce consistency often. Those are your cues, not stopwatch."
- "The balance between creamy and spicy is subtle and personal control here is key. Add cream gradually, taste frequently."
