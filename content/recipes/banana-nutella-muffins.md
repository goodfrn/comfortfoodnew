ingredients:
- "3 ripe bananas, mashed"
- "2 cups pancake mix"
- "1/2 cup milk or almond milk"
- "1/4 cup vegetable oil or melted coconut oil"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/2 cup brown sugar, packed"
- "1 teaspoon almond extract (instead of vanilla)"
- "1/4 cup Greek yogurt (added twist)"
- "1/3 cup Nutella, divided for swirling"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355℉. Line standard muffin tins with paper or silicone liners; sets foundation without sticking."
- "In large bowl, mash bananas thoroughly — lumps okay; moisture and natural sweetness. Avoid over-mashing, some texture adds character."
- "Add pancake mix, milk, oil, eggs, brown sugar, almond extract, and Greek yogurt to mashed bananas. Use wooden spoon, fold gently. Incorporation not homogenized batter; stop just before fully uniform — overmix toughens crumb."
- "Drop Nutella onto batter in three dollops. Use thin skewer or knife to swirl gently; visual marbling signals distribution without blending away the creamy pockets."
- "Scoop batter with tablespoon or muffin scoop into liners. About two-thirds full to give room for rise and avoid overflow—note uneven fill changes baking time slightly."
- "Bake 20-27 minutes. Listen for faint crackling sounds and watch golden top edges walk your timer back or forward. Insert toothpick; a few moist crumbs cling but no raw batter. Underbaking yields gummy centers; overbaking dries the crumb."
- "Cool muffins 10 minutes in pan before popping out. They finish setting as they cool; remove too soon, they break apart. Perfume of banana and Nutella fills kitchen, a classic marker."
- "If batter too thick, whisk extra splash of milk; too thin, a spoon more pancake mix prevents flattening. For dairy-free, swap Greek yogurt for applesauce. Nutella swap with peanut butter gives savory depth."
