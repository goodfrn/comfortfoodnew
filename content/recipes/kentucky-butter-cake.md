ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 1/4 tsp baking powder"
- "1/2 tsp baking soda"
- "1 1/2 cups granulated sugar"
- "4 large eggs"
- "1 cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "1 tsp rum extract (substitute with bourbon or apple juice concentrate)"
- "1 tsp butter extract"
- "3/4 cup buttermilk"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter (for sauce)"
- "1 cup granulated sugar (for sauce)"
- "1/4 cup water"
- "1 tsp rum extract (for sauce)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 320°F instead of 325°F; temperature drop slows crust formation and promotes softer rise. Grease and dust a 10-inch bundt pan thoroughly with flour; skip if using nonstick but grease well with butter. Shake out extra flour to avoid dry spots."
- "Whisk together sugar and eggs in a large bowl. Add softened butter and beaten until light, fluffy, pale yellow - takes about 3 minutes with hand mixer on high speed. Creaming well traps air and avoids dense cake. Pour in rum and butter extracts along with buttermilk. Mix on medium-high until combined but don’t overdo — silky and airy, not soupy."
- "Sift together flour, baking powder, and baking soda separately—no clumps tolerated. Add dry mix gradually, folding in or beating on low. Scrape sides with spatula often. The batter should feel slightly thick but pourable. Overmixing develops gluten; cake turns chewy, not tender. Avoid streaks but stop once uniform."
- "Pour batter evenly into prepared bundt pan. Tap pan a few times on counter, releasing large air bubbles. Bake in center rack for 50 to 60 minutes. Don’t just rely on time—look for cake pulling slightly from sides, golden crust, and toothpick with moist crumbs still stuck but no wet batter."
- "Allow cake to cool in pan for 15-20 minutes. Too hot sauce application melts crust; too cold cake won’t absorb syrup well. The cake will still be warm but sturdy to touch. Invert carefully onto wire rack or plate if ready; else remain in pan for sauce soak."
- "For sauce, melt butter, sugar, water in medium saucepan over low-medium heat. Whisk gently. The sugar must dissolve without crystallizing; keep heat below simmering — watch for cracking tiny bubbles but never boil. Couple minutes—soft thick mixture, fully melted, glossy appearance."
- "Remove pan from heat. Stir in rum extract for warmth and aroma. Reserve 1/3 cup of sauce aside for drizzling. Pour remaining hot sauce slowly over cake while it sits in bundt pan to take in maximum moisture. Wait 10-12 minutes, or until liquid fully absorbs. Don’t rush dunking or sauce will pool or slide off."
- "Gently flip cake onto serving plate. Drizzle reserved sauce over top. Should glisten, give sticky but not soggy texture. Slice with serrated knife for cleaner cuts. Best served warm or room temp. Refrigerate leftovers tightly wrapped to maintain moist crumb."
