ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "4 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 teaspoon ground cumin"
- "1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper"
- "2 cans (15 oz each) pinto beans, drained and rinsed"
- "1/2 cup diced green chilies (substitute with roasted poblano for milder heat)"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese (try pepper jack for twist)"
instructions:
- "Heat oil over medium heat in heavy-bottom saucepan; swirl to coat. Toss in garlic first. When it sizzles and aroma hits (about 90 seconds), sprinkle cumin and cayenne. Watch closely—spices toast fast; you want them fragrant, not burnt."
- "Add beans straight from the can. Stir, listen for a soft bubbling sound, rims beginning to shimmer. Heat until beans steam and betray their broken skins, about 3-4 minutes. This preps for mashing while flavor infuses."
- "Mash beans with potato masher or large fork. Some chunks okay — rustic feel. Press until creamy but not gluey. Too much mashing = pasty. Adjust with a splash of broth or water if too thick."
- "Fold in green chilies—watch for their fiery scent mixing with cumin earthiness. Toss in shredded cheese last. It melts into pockets of molten sharpness, linking texture and taste. Let sit 1-2 mins off heat so cheese spreads nicely."
- "Serve hot. Garnish optional but a squeeze of lime lifts heaviness. If too thick, thin with hot water or broth bit by bit while stirring."
- "Always taste for seasoning before serving. Some beans canned salty; others play bland. Balance cayenne, cumin, and salt to your liking."
