Three overripe bananas. Maple syrup instead of sugar. That one time the loaves came out dense and I swore off banana bread for a year. Then I figured out the yogurt trick—added moisture, kept the crumb tender, no gumminess. Now it's the only way.

## Why You'll Love This Banana Chocolate Chip Bread

Takes just over an hour total. 25 minutes of actual work, then you walk away.
Tastes better the next day. Seriously. The flavors settle overnight and it gets softer somehow.
One loaf feeds a crowd or lasts all week in your freezer. Makes three.
Comfort food that doesn't feel heavy. The Greek yogurt cuts the richness. The maple syrup adds depth without tasting like candy.
Dark chocolate chunks, not chips. They stay chunky instead of melting into nothing. Brown sugar layers inside mean each slice has surprise pockets of caramel.

## What You Need for Banana Chocolate Chip Loaf

Unbleached all-purpose flour. Not bread flour. The protein ratio matters for texture here.
Baking powder and baking soda. Both. The combo gets you lift without that soapy baking soda taste.
Softened butter and maple syrup. Not honey. Maple has a different viscosity—changes how the batter comes together. Swap it out and the moisture balance shifts.
Two large eggs. Room temperature works better but cold is fine too.
Four ripe bananas. Like, actually ripe. Brown spots. The darker they are, the sweeter and more banana-forward it tastes. Use green ones and you're basically just making bread with banana flavor as a rumor.
Plain Greek yogurt. The full-fat kind. Low-fat tastes thin.
Brown sugar—packed. That means press it down in the measuring cup. Loose brown sugar throws off the ratios.
Dark chocolate bits. Not chips. Bigger, chunkier, holds its shape better. About a cup and a quarter.
Flaky sea salt. A pinch on top. Sounds weird. Not weird.

## How to Make Banana Chocolate Chip Bread

Line three loaf pans with parchment. Leave overhang on two sides—makes pulling them out easy, no loaves sticking and tearing. No greasing needed if you use paper.

Whisk together flour, baking powder, and baking soda in a separate bowl. Set it aside. Ratios matter here. Mess this up and you get flatness, bitterness, dense lumps that don't rise.

Cream the softened butter with maple syrup. Use medium speed—not high. You're not making whipped cream. You want it light and pale and airy, but the syrup makes it more liquid than regular sugar would, so the texture shifts. It comes together differently.

Crack the eggs in one at a time. Stir until just blended. Add vanilla. Then mash the bananas in—use a fork, doesn't have to be perfect. Some small chunks are good. Stir moderately. Don't beat it to death or gluten develops and the crumb gets stiff.

Now the part that saves this whole thing: alternate folding the dry flour mixture with spoonfuls of Greek yogurt. Start with dry. End with dry. Fold gently. This back-and-forth keeps everything tender and moist. No wild mixer action.

Divide the batter in half across the three pans. Half-full. Sprinkle half the brown sugar shards on top, then half the chocolate bits. Add the rest of the batter. Finish with the remaining brown sugar and chocolate on top. That layering means every slice has pockets of caramel and chocolate running through it. Top with a pinch of flaky sea salt. Sounds like a choice but it cuts the sweetness and you notice it immediately.

## How to Get Chocolate Chip Banana Bread Perfectly Baked

Oven goes to 350°F. About 50 minutes total. Watch the sides first—they'll turn golden and the top will crack. That crack is normal. Good, actually. Means the inside rose and the top couldn't keep up.

The smell is the real timer. Deep, caramel-banana, almost overwhelming in a good way. That's when you know you're close.

At the 45-minute mark, touch the top of the crust gently near the edge. Should bounce back slow. Not wet. Not sticky. Not leaving an indent.

Insert a tester (toothpick, skewer, whatever) into the middle of one loaf. A bit of melted chocolate might cling to it. That's fine. Raw batter isn't. You'll know the difference. Batter sticks wet. Chocolate sticks and looks glossy.

If the tops are browning too fast and the inside still feels soft, cover with foil for the last 10 minutes.

## Banana Chocolate Chip Bread Tips and Common Mistakes

Cool the loaves in the pans for 15 minutes first. Then use that parchment overhang to pull them out and cool completely on a rack. Trying to cut into warm banana bread means falling apart and crumbling everywhere.

Moisture wins. That's the whole thing. Too much flour and it's dry. Too little and it's gummy. The yogurt is what fixes that balance.

Brown the banana spots if you can. They're sweeter. That matters for depth.

Dark chocolate chunks only. Milk chocolate gets lost. White chocolate is just noise.

Make it a day ahead if possible. The flavors marry. It slices cleaner. It tastes better. Not sure why exactly but it does.

Freeze one or two loaves whole, wrapped tight. Lasts three months easy. Thaw at room temp for a few hours and it's like fresh-baked without the work.

Don't skip the sea salt on top. It's not traditional. It's not a mistake either.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Banana Chocolate Chip Bread

**Can I use overripe bananas or frozen ones?**
Overripe is better. Frozen works too—thaw them first, squeeze out the liquid, or the bread gets soggy. The mushier the banana, the sweeter and more intense the flavor.

**What if I don't have Greek yogurt?**
Sour cream works. Buttermilk too but use a bit less because it's thinner. Regular yogurt is fine if it's the only option. Haven't tried applesauce. Might work. Changes the taste though.

**Can I make this in a regular loaf pan instead of three?**
One big loaf takes longer—maybe 75 minutes. Two pans takes about 55. Check early because oven behavior varies. Thicker loaves bake uneven sometimes.

**Why do my loaves sink in the middle?**
Either the oven temp is off or they came out too early. Oven thermometers are cheap and fix this instantly. Most ovens run hot or cold by 25 degrees without you knowing.

**Can I add nuts?**
Yeah. Walnuts or pecans work. Use about three-quarters cup, chopped. Replace some of the chocolate bits with them or add both. Toasted nuts taste better.

**How long does banana chocolate chip bread last?**
Room temp in a sealed container—about 4 days before it starts drying out. Fridge makes it last longer but the texture gets off. Freezer is where it lives if you're keeping it more than a week.

**Do I need the flaky salt or is it optional?**
Optional but stop skipping it. One pinch on top does something to the sweetness that you can't explain without tasting it. If you hate salt, leave it. Most people change their mind once they try it.
