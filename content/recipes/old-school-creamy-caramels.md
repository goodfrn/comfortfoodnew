ingredients:
- "2 cups granulated sugar"
- "1 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter"
- "2/3 cup light corn syrup"
- "1/4 tsp salt"
- "1 cup whole milk slowly added"
- "Optional twist: replace milk with 1 cup canned coconut cream"
instructions:
- "Line a 9×13-inch pan with parchment paper pressing firmly to get corners clean."
- "Combine sugars, butter, corn syrup, salt in heavy-bottomed saucepan. Start over medium heat. Stir frequently. Butter melting first telltale sound and shine. When it boils, bubble noise steady, move fast here."
- "Now the tricky part—add milk drop by drop, whisking nonstop. Boil must keep rolling, no slowing or else grainy caramel soon. Expect this slow dance to take about 21 minutes; you’ll see a thickening, almost velvety viscosity."
- "Turn heat down to medium-low. Clip candy thermometer spoon-side. Stir every 30 seconds to avoid scorching. When temp hits 238°F (soft-ball stage), pull from heat immediately. That temperature gives chewy but soft bite."
- "Pour immediately into prepared pan—thick liquid glossy, swirls of caramel color. Let sit at room temp for 4 to 5 hours undisturbed. Patience here is non-negotiable."
- "Use a sharp knife dipped in cold water to slice. Cut 10 rows across, 8 lengthwise or your desired chunk size. Cold water keeps knife fettling neat edges instead of messy drag."
- "Wrap each square with wax paper or candy wrappers. Store in airtight container room temp, lasts multiple days but rarely last that long."

faq:
- "q: Why add milk so slowly? a: Heat balance, milk cools sugar mixture instantly. Fast addition causes lumps, breaking caramel structure. Whisk always, keep boil rolling. No boil means grittiness sets. Milk's fat content also matters. Whole milk adds smoothness; lower fat risks grainy, watery caramel."
- "q: Can I skip corn syrup? a: Technically yes but expect grainy results. Corn syrup prevents sugar crystallizing. Alternatives glucose syrup or thick honey work but flavor shifts. Without syrup, careful stir—no scraping pan. Adds chew and keeps glossy finish too. Experiment but expect texture changes."
- "q: What if thermometer misses 238°F? a: Overheat past 238 means brittle candy, snap hard. Under 238 results in too soft, gooey squares that don’t hold shape. Visual cues help—thick syrup coats spoon, pulls shiny ribbons, slight stretch but doesn't break easy. Timing tricks, tactile feel key with experience."
- "q: How to store leftovers? a: Airtight container keeps freshness, warmth or fridge messes texture. Avoid plastic cling wrap; moisture causes sticky lumps. Wax paper wrapped squares best bet—prevents sticking. Room temp stable for days but expect softening or toughening over time. Freezing not recommended; condensation melts surface."
