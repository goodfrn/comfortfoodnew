ingredients:
- "Graham cracker sheets about 200g, finely pulsed"
- "3 Tbsp granulated sugar; adjust to taste"
- "6 Tbsp butter melted; any unsalted works"
- "Pinch of fine salt"
- "16 oz cream cheese softened, room temp to avoid lumps"
- "5 oz sour cream (can swap with Greek yogurt or crème fraîche)"
- "1 tsp vanilla bean paste; vanilla extract ok but paste adds speckles"
- "3 large eggs at room temperature for better emulsification"
- "1 cup semisweet chocolate chips for ganache"
- "2/3 cup heavy cream warmed but not boiling"
- "Fresh raspberries for garnish"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 320 F rather than 325 to avoid cracking; gentle heat equals silky texture."
- "Line 18 muffin tin cavities with cupcake liners; fill remaining spots with water to keep oven humidity up."
- "Pulse graham crackers in food processor till coarse crumbs, not dust. Mix in sugar, melted butter, and salt until mixture clumps like wet sand."
- "Press firmly but evenly into liner bottoms, creating uniform crust thickness around 1/4 inch. No bare spots; crust is foundation."
- "Cream cheese and sour cream together using paddle attachment until no lumps appear about 1-2 minutes on medium speed. Scrape sides once or twice."
- "Add sugar and vanilla bean paste, mix just until integrated—shiny, silky batter. No graininess."
- "Add eggs one at a time, room temp crucial here; medium-low speed to prevent bubbles that cause cracks. Scrape bowl sides between each."
- "Spoon or preferably scoop with 2 Tbsp cookie scoop into crusts. Tap tin gently to settle mixture evenly but avoid forced leveling. Slight doming expected."
- "Bake for about 25-30 minutes watching centers. Edges should be set and slightly pulling from liners; center jiggles but isn’t liquid."
- "Cool on wire rack until no longer steaming. Transfer to fridge for minimum 2 hours or overnight for tighter texture."
- "Warm cream over medium low until steam just rises; don’t boil or cream separates. Pour over chocolate chips in heatproof bowl. Wait 3 minutes. Stir slowly until glossy ganache forms."
- "Cool ganache until it thickens to spoonable consistency—about 5-10 minutes at room temp. Spoon over chilled cheesecakes gently to cover tops."
- "Garnish each with a fresh raspberry. Return to fridge 1-2 hours till ganache sets firm but soft. Too cold and ganache cracks while cutting or biting."
- "Remove liners carefully—crust sticks if warm, so always chill first. Use small offset spatula if stubborn."
- "Store leftovers covered in fridge no more than 3 days. Could freeze bites un-ganached wrapped tight, thaw overnight refrigerated before ganache."
