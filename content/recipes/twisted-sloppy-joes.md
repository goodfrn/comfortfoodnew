ingredients:
- "1 pound ground beef"
- "1 pound bulk chorizo sausage"
- "1 medium onion chopped"
- "1 green bell pepper chopped"
- "1/2 cup chipotle tomato paste"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons yellow mustard"
- "1 tablespoon brown sugar"
- "2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1/2 teaspoon white pepper"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "Sesame seed buns"
- "6 strips crispy bacon crumbled"
- "cooking spray or 1 teaspoon neutral oil"
instructions:
- "Heat large skillet medium-high. Use spray or teaspoon oil. Listen for faint sizzle, not smoke."
- "Add ground beef and chorizo. Break apart vigorously with spatula. Cook until no pink, fat rendering well but not burnt. Expect 5-7 minutes. Drain briefly on paper towels to avoid greasiness."
- "Pour off excess fat but keep thin coating in pan so veggies don't stick and get flavor."
- "Toss in onion and green pepper. Saute stirring often. Look for softened edges and slight translucence after 4-5 minutes; don't let brown or lose crunch totally."
- "Mix in chipotle tomato paste, mustard, brown sugar, Worcestershire, smoked paprika, white pepper and salt. Stir until sauce thickens slightly, smells smoky and sweet. Taste and tweak heat or sweetness now—chipotle paste can vary."
- "Return meats to pan. Combine thoroughly. Let simmer gently 5-6 minutes. Squish with spatula occasionally. Sauce should cling to meat, not pool separately."
- "Toast sesame buns while meat simmers—dry heat crisps crumb and prevents sogginess. Quick flip 1-2 minutes each side."
- "Assemble sandwiches sloppily. Meat piled high, bacon sprinkled over top for crunch and salty kick."
- "If sauce thickens too much, splash a little water or broth; too loose, cook longer to reduce. Don't overcook; dry sloppy joes ruin burgers."
- "Serve immediately. Leftovers reheat well but sauce may need stirring and moisture adjustment. Great with pickles or sharp cheese."
