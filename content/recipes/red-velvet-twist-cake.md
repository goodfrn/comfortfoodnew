ingredients:
- "2 cups cake flour"
- "1/2 tsp fine salt"
- "2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 3/4 cups granulated sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/4 cup canola oil"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1 tbsp white vinegar"
- "1 cup buttermilk"
- "1 1/2 tbsp red food coloring gel"
- "1 cup chopped walnuts toasted"
- "8 oz cream cheese softened"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "4 cups powdered sugar sifted"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "=== Cake ==="
- "Heat oven to 350°F. Butter and lightly flour two 8-inch pans; avoid too much flour to prevent dryness."
- "In a large bowl, whisk together cake flour, salt, cocoa, and baking soda. Important to sift or at least stir vigorously to avoid lumps. Set aside."
- "Cream butter and sugar using mixer for around 2 minutes until fluffy but watch them closely; overbeating leads to grainy texture here."
- "Add eggs one by one, beating fully after each. Don't rush, or batter breaks down. Blend in canola oil, vanilla, and vinegar next. Vinegar cuts richness, makes crumb tender — do not skip, despite odd scent at this stage."
- "Alternate adding dry ingredients with buttermilk. Start and end with dry. Mix on low speed only, barely combine before next addition. Overmixing = tough cake. Splash of cold buttermilk is fine to maintain moisture bounce in crumb."
- "Add red food coloring gel last, fold gently to maintain batter aeration. Too vigorous mixing dulls brightness; you want vivid scarlet, not muted brick."
- "Divide batter evenly between pans. Roughly smooth tops, then bang pans lightly on counter to release bubbles."
- "Bake 27 to 32 minutes. Don’t rely solely on time; watch for pull away from sides, lightly springy top, toothpick inserted should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter."
- "Cool 5 to 10 minutes in pans, then turn out onto racks. Leave cakes exposed, no cover to avoid soggy bottoms. Should be fully cooled before frosting, or frosting melts into mess."
- "=== Frosting & Nuts ==="
- "Preheat oven to 350°F again. Spread walnuts evenly on baking sheet. Toast 5 to 8 minutes until fragrant and just golden. Watch carefully, nuts shift from toasted to burnt fast."
- "Beat cream cheese and butter together until silky and lump-free. Takes about 3 minutes on medium-high speed. No shortcuts here—lumps = unpleasant frosting."
- "Gradually add powdered sugar, 1 cup at a time, beating well after each. Too fast and sugar clouds kitchen. Finish with vanilla extract; important for deep aroma."
- "When frosting ready, fold in cooled walnuts if you want crunch inside. If not, frost then sprinkle on top to keep nuts crunchier longer."
- "Use offset spatula in gentle strokes to frost cake layers. Frosting should hold shape but spread smoothly. Too soft? Chill briefly, but not too long or it hardens."
- "Assemble cake, press walnuts on sides if desired. Nuts add texture and a nutty bitterness to cut sweetness — necessary balance."
- "Keep refrigerated if not serving soon. Remove 30 minutes before slicing to bring cream cheese to softer consistency."
- "=== Tips & Tricks ==="
- "If no buttermilk, use milk + 1 tbsp vinegar or lemon juice, rest 5 minutes. Fresh is best though."
- "Oil choice affects moistness; canola smoothest here, olive or coconut add odd undertones."
- "Cake crumb should bounce lightly when poked. If dry, you probably overmixed or baked too long."
- "Cracked tops not a failure—shield with foil partway if too aggressive browning."
- "Red color fades when baked; don’t skimp on gel dye, liquid fades too much."
- "Overwhipped cream cheese frosting will become grainy; stop when just smooth and hold peaks."
- "Serving tip: pair with a calm black coffee or tart berry sauce if wanting contrast."
- "Storage: Wrapped tightly can last 3 days fridge, freeze layers separately with plastic wrap for longer keeping."
