ingredients:
- "1 cup jasmine rice"
- "2 ¼ cups water"
- "dash of salt plus pinch for seasoning"
- "¾ lb ahi tuna steak, cut ¾ inch cubes"
- "3 stalks green onions, chopped"
- "2 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated"
- "3 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)"
- "1 tsp toasted sesame oil"
- "1 tbsp rice vinegar"
- "½ tsp red chili flakes"
- "1 tsp pure maple syrup (instead of sugar)"
- "1 medium cucumber, diced (swapped avocado)"
- "½ cup shelled edamame (frozen, thawed)"
- "1 tbsp sesame seeds, toasted"
- "Wasabi peas and extra sesame seeds for garnish"
instructions:
- "=== Rice ==="
- "Add rice to fine mesh and rinse under cool water, swirl fingers. Stop when water runs clear—any starch, rinse again. Crucial for fluffy grains, no clumps. Drain well."
- "In medium saucepan, add rinsed rice, 2¼ cups water, dash salt. Bring to rolling boil, no lid yet, stir once with wooden spoon—listen for subtle crackle sounds signaling heat. Lower heat to simmer, cover snug. Let bubbles whisper away for 18 minutes. You’ll see steam condensation form on lid."
- "Turn off heat but no lifting lid—important. Let sit 12 minutes to firm up. Fluff gently with fork, separate grains not mush."
- "=== Tuna Mix ==="
- "Slice tuna into bite-sized cubes about ¾ inch. Use sharp knife—clean cuts avoid mashed flesh. Transfer to big bowl."
- "Add green onions, garlic, ginger. Pour soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, chili flakes, pinch salt, and maple syrup over fish. Stir lightly with spatula, coating evenly but no mush. The syrup adds a subtle bright contrast—experimented with honey, but maple's earthiness won."
- "Add diced cucumber (crisp change from avocado’s creaminess), edamame, and toasted sesame seeds. Fold carefully to keep tuna chunks intact."
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Spoon rice into bowls, mound tuna mix on top. Garnish with crunchy wasabi peas, scatter green onion curls, and sprinkle extra white and black sesame seeds. Optional extras: pickled ginger, thinly sliced radish for peppery bite."
- "Serve immediately or chill slightly to let flavors meld. I prefer just warm rice; ice-cold kills textures."
- "=== Tips & Tricks ==="
- "If no fine strainer, use regular sieve but rinse rice in small batches. Avoid rinsing in a big bowl—rice sticks, water cloudy longer."
- "If stuck without fresh tuna, frozen previously-thawed sashimi grade works but pat dry well, less juicy."
- "Rice cooker rinse still needed—never skip. Jasmine rice is slight starchier than sushi rice—texture difference noticed."
- "Toast sesame seeds in dry pan over medium heat 2-3 minutes till golden and fragrant—watch they don’t burn fast."
- "Don’t overcrowd bowl; poke should be the star with fresh, clean taste between bites."
- "Chili flakes can vary—add cautiously; balance heat with maple sweetness and acidic vinegar punch."
- "Leftover poke? Eat within 24 hours, keep fish cold. Sometimes I repeat drizzle soy-based sauce to rehydrate the tuna before serving again."
