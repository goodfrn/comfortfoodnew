ingredients:
- "2 teaspoons vegetable oil or avocado oil"
- "5 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped"
- "1 can black beans, drained and rinsed"
- "1 cup frozen corn kernels, thawed"
- "3 cups shredded cooked chicken (rotisserie or poached works well)"
- "1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped"
- "1 tablespoon ground cumin"
- "8 medium whole wheat tortillas (substitute corn if gluten-free but watch moisture)"
- "3 3/4 cups shredded cheese (Mexican blend or sharp cheddar)"
- "2 cups enchilada sauce (homemade or store-bought, smooth not chunky)"
- "Sour cream or Greek yogurt for serving"
instructions:
- "Start by preheating oven to 380°F. Spray a large casserole or 9x13 baking dish lightly with oil. Avoid too heavy spray or oil puddles will cause uneven browning."
- "Heat oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Toss in the spinach, stir and turn quickly until just wilted but still bright green. Remove spinach promptly to a big bowl to avoid sogginess from residual heat."
- "Add rinsed black beans, thawed corn, shredded chicken, chopped cilantro, and cumin to the bowl. Stir in 3 cups of shredded cheese. Mix but don’t overwork; want loose stuffing."
- "Spread a thin layer of enchilada sauce on the bottom of the dish. This prevents sticking – a step I always skip at my own peril."
- "Lay one tortilla flat on a clean surface. Scoop about 1/2 cup to 3/4 cup filling into center, leaving about an inch on edges. Roll it snugly but not stretched. Ends should overlap slightly for good hold – loose rolling means spill."
- "Arrange rolled enchiladas seam side down in baking dish, snuggly but not too tight. Some spaces okay to let bubbling sauce pool lightly."
- "Pour remaining enchilada sauce evenly on top. Enough to coat but avoid pools that make bottoms soggy. If sauce is thick, gently spread with back of ladle."
- "Sprinkle leftover shredded cheese evenly over the sauced tortillas, for that browned cheesy crown."
- "Bake uncovered in hot oven until cheese bubbles and browns in spots, roughly 20 to 22 minutes. You’ll hear sizzling, see sauce bubbling edges. Tortilla edges crisp but not burnt."
- "Pull out and cool 3 to 5 minutes. Let settle to firm up inside. Use a large, thin spatula to carefully lift each enchilada to serve without tearing."
- "Top each serving with a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt. Bright fresh tang cuts richness, balances heat."
- "No complex herbs or steps here – straightforward layered flavors. Adjust the cumin or cilantro for punchier or subtler profiles depending on palate."
