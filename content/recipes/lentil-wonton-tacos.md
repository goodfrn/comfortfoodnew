ingredients:
- "12 wonton wrappers"
- "1 cup cooked lentils (slightly undercooked holds shape better)"
- "1 Tbsp chipotle black beans, mashed smooth (sub: refried beans)"
- "1 1/4 tsp taco seasoning (avoid salty blends; better fresh mix)"
- "Non stick spray"
- "Toppings: diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, sliced jalapeños, cilantro leaves"
instructions:
- "=== Preheat + Prep ==="
- "Set oven to 380° F—just a tad hotter to crisp up edges evenly. Spray a cupcake pan thoroughly with non stick spray. Don’t scrimp here or wontons stick like glue."
- "=== Layering Assembly ==="
- "Mix lentils with taco seasoning. Less powder keeps lentils distinct in texture, don’t overdo. Mash chipotle beans smooth with a fork; smoky heat adds a deeper note than plain refried beans."
- "Start with 1 wonton wrapper pressed flat into each cupcake space. Pat down 1.5 tsp of the mashed beans on the bottom—not too thick or you lose crispness. Layer another wrapper on top, then add 1 tbsp lentil mix. Alternate until you run out. Usually 2-3 layers works best—too many and wrapper melts into mush."
- "=== Baking ==="
- "Slide pan in oven. Listen for the soft crackle around 12 mins; edges going golden with spots darkening gently signals the start. By 17-18 mins will be golden, crispy. Don’t jump on oven alarm clock; watch edges and smell nutty toasty scent. Too long and they brown bitter."
- "=== Serving ==="
- "Remove from pan with a small offset spatula or toothpick easing between edges to prevent tearing. Garnish with your fave taco toppers—fresh cilantro and tomatoes cut richness, jalapeños introduce bite."
- "Serve warm. Leftovers lose crunch fast; reheat briefly under broiler to revive textures."
