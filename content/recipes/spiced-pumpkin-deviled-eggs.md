ingredients:
- "12 large hard-boiled eggs"
- "4 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1/4 cup pumpkin puree (not pumpkin pie filling)"
- "3 tablespoons mayonnaise (or Greek yogurt for tang)"
- "1 tablespoon Dijon mustard"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika plus extra for garnish"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons apple cider vinegar"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "8 sage leaves"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil (or avocado oil for frying sage)"
instructions:
- "=== Filling Prep ==="
- "Peel eggs then slice in half. Gently pop yolks out into a bowl. Arrange white halves on a platter. Don’t rush popping yolks or whites will break and spill."
- "Roughly mash yolks with a fork or pass through potato ricer if you want velvety texture. I prefer ricer – no lumps no grainy bits."
- "Add softened cream cheese to yolks. Beat or stir until creamy without chunks. Cream cheese thickens and steadies the filling—skip it, and mixture gets too loose."
- "Stir in pumpkin puree, mayo, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper. Blend thoroughly. Creaminess plus aromatic smokiness plus tanginess. The pumpkin pulp is key; not too watery."
- "Cover bowl and chill for about 25 minutes. Cold filling pipes easier and stays put in whites. If too stiff afterward, stir briefly before piping."
- ""
- "=== Sage Garnish ==="
- "Heat olive oil medium heat in a small skillet. Use oil with neutral flavor or mild fruitiness—extra virgin olive oils can burn quickly."
- "Fry sage leaves in batches—4 or 5 at a time. About 20 to 35 seconds. Leaves will look limp and slick but not brown or crispy yet. Browning saps flavor. Timing is tricky. Listen for faint sizzle, leaves soften but edges stay smooth."
- "Transfer leaves to paper towels to drain oil. Store in airtight container at room temp if not using immediately. They stay good a few hours crisp."
- ""
- "=== Assembly ==="
- "Fill a piping bag or sturdy zip-top bag with filling. Snip a small hole in corner—adjust size depending on how thick your filling is."
- "Pipe filling into egg white cavities. Don’t overstuff; spilling looks messy and wastes filling."
- "Top each filled egg with one crispy fried sage leaf and dust with smoked paprika. The sage crunch surprises with its earthy whisper."
- ""
- "Store covered in fridge until serving. Bring to room temp 10 minutes before eating to unlock full flavors."
- ""
- "=== Tips & Substitutions ==="
- "No cream cheese? Use full-fat Greek yogurt or mascarpone but filling gets softer, pipe carefully."
- "Pumpkin can be swapped with roasted butternut squash puree for nuttier flavor."
- "No fresh sage? Try fried fresh thyme leaves or crispy rosemary for woody herbaceousness."
- "If mayo missing, swap with extra cream cheese or sour cream, adjust seasoning."
- "Piping bag not on hand? Small spoon and steady hand work fine, just slower."
- "Don’t overcook sage. If edges brown, remake batch. Bitter burnt taste ruins topping."
- "Use your nose and eyes as cooking guide for frying leaves and eggs doneness. Avoid timers as much as possible."
- "Eggs older than a week peel better."
- "If yolk mix too dry add splash milk or cream."
