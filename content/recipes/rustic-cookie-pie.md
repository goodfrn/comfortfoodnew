ingredients:
- "1 unbaked pie crust chilled"
- "1/2 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 cup oat flour"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter melted and cooled"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup packed light brown sugar"
- "1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "1/2 cup chopped toasted pecans"
instructions:
- "Oven cranked to 320°F, pie shell slid into freezer for chill while prepping batter."
- "Whisk flours and baking soda until even – oat flour adds rustic chew, but all-purpose works too."
- "Eggs beaten vigorously on medium speed, until frothy and thick, signals air trapped for lift."
- "Butter poured in slow stream, sugars and vanilla dropped in next, keep scraping bowl sides to avoid lumps."
- "Flour mix folded gently, not overworked; batter should be thick but not heavy."
- "Chocolate chips and warm toasted pecans folded in, extra crunch and flavor depth."
- "Spoon batter into frozen crust, smooth surface with spatula."
- "Bake 48-53 minutes, watch top tan and crack slightly; center jiggles softly when nudged but isn’t liquid."
- "Cool on rack 35 minutes minimum, edges set while center finishes firming. Overnight rest intensifies flavors."
- "Slice with serrated knife to keep edges intact. Serve with cold vanilla ice cream, drizzle bittersweet chocolate syrup for contrast."

tips:
- "Keep pie crust frozen until batter’s ready. Warm crust means soggy bottom, no crisp edges. Scoop batter quick then bake so crust stays cold longer. Cold crust traps air bubbles better once batter’s in."
- "Whip eggs medium speed, watch foam build thick and frothy, like tiny bubbles trapped for lift. Butter must cool first. Hot butter kills air pockets, folds heavy batter, slows rise. Patience here pays."
- "Swap oat flour up to half all-purpose mix. Adds rustic chew, rougher texture; makes crust denser slightly but richer in flavor. Don’t overmix flour fold, stops gluten overdevelopment. Batter thick, airy but tender."
- "Toast nuts dry pan or oven 350°F, few minutes til fragrant. Nuts smell sharp, oil releases, cuts raw edge. Pecans better than walnuts here - sweeter, softer. Fold in warm nuts, spreads heat and flavor gently."
- "Watch bake cues: golden cracks edge top, center jiggles gently but not watery. Jiggle means moist batter inside, still set enough to slice when cooled. Overbake makes dry crumb, underbake leaves gooey core."
faq:
- "q: Can I use only all-purpose flour? a: Yes, 100% all-purpose works. Oat adds texture but not mandatory. Texture less rustic, softer crumb. Keep folding careful or batter toughens."
- "q: Nuts alternatives okay? a: Try almonds or hazelnuts, toasted same way. Different oils, flavors shift pie tone. Chop size matters for crunch. Skip nuts if allergic, add more chips instead."
- "q: How to fix soggy crust? a: Chill crust before filling. Bake blind if possible or freeze crust solid. Avoid too wet batter. If crust soggy post-bake, try heat under crust or re-bake low to dry bottom."
- "q: Best storage method? a: Room temp wrapped good 2 days. Fridge slows crumb change but can dry edges. Freeze slices wrapped tight, thaw mid-temp. Chocolate drizzle best fresh; syrup thickens cold."
