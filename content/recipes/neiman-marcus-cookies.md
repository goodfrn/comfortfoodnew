ingredients:
- "2 cups unsalted butter softened"
- "1 cup white sugar reduced to ¾ cup"
- "1 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 tsp pure vanilla extract"
- "3 ½ cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup almond meal (replace ground oatmeal)"
- "½ tsp salt"
- "1 tsp baking powder"
- "1 tsp baking soda"
- "1 ½ cups semi-sweet chocolate chips"
- "½ cup grated dark chocolate or bittersweet"
- "optional sea salt flakes for topping"
instructions:
- "=== Preparation ==="
- "Preheat oven to 380°F. Hotter by a few degrees to compensate for different flour absorption. Lines: parchment paper or silicone mats, saves scrubbing burnt mess."
- "=== Mixing ==="
- "Butter at room temp, not melted. Cream butter with sugars until pale and light, 2–3 minutes. Watch texture not clock. Using electric mixer helps but don’t overbeat or cookies turn cakey."
- "Add eggs one at a time, beating after each to emulsify properly. Splash vanilla, mix in."
- "In separate bowl, whisk flour, almond meal, salt, baking powder, baking soda. Almond meal adds moisture, slightly denser than oats."
- "Fold dry mix into wet. Folding, not pounding. Lose fluff, chew lost too."
- "Throw in chocolate chips and grated dark chocolate. The grated bits, melt slower; chunks for pockets of texture."
- "=== Forming ==="
- "Tablespoon-sized balls. Press slightly flat if you want crisper edges. Space no less than 2 inches. Crowding leads to merging and uneven baking."
- "Optional: sprinkle sea salt flakes on top before baking; cuts sweetness and adds pop."
- "=== Baking ==="
- "8–12 minutes usually. Edges light golden, centers still soft but set. Lift one carefully; bottom shows toasted tan. Too pale means undercooked gooey center - fine if you like that. Too brown, burnt butter taste creeps in."
- "Listen for faint crackling, smells rich chocolate butter mix, scents fill kitchen."
- "=== Finishing ==="
- "Cool on racks at least 10 minutes. Hot cookies are fragile, cool creates structure. Bake in batches to avoid warm air pooling."
- "=== Storage ==="
- "Store airtight. Softer if sealed slightly before cool; harder, dry if left longer uncovered."
