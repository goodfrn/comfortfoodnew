ingredients:
- "4 large Yukon gold potatoes dry"
- "3 tablespoons olive oil"
- "Coarse sea salt"
- "Freshly cracked black pepper"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter softened"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "2 teaspoons whole grain mustard substitute Dijon"
- "1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "4 tablespoons sour cream"
- "1 tablespoon fresh chopped chives"
- "3 tablespoons cooked pancetta bits substitute bacon"
- "Optional: pinch smoked paprika for finishing sprinkle"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven around 420°F. Set rack mid-height to avoid burning edges."
- "Pat potatoes very dry, the drier the crisper skin. Cut thin parallel slits about 1/8 to 1/5 inch apart, stopping just before bottom slice stays connected. It helps to rest on a damp towel for grip."
- "Optional: slice a small flat on bottom to prevent rolling, steady slicing."
- "Arrange potatoes on rimmed baking sheet. Brush 3 tablespoons olive oil inside slits and whole surface, really get at those cracks."
- "Generously sprinkle sea salt and fresh cracked pepper over entire potato, salt seals crust formation."
- "Bake 40 to 50 mins until slices visibly open and edges start crisping with golden tips."
- "While baking, soften 3 tablespoons butter in small bowl; whisk in garlic powder and whole grain mustard instead of Dijon for a rustic tang."
- "Remove potatoes, brush butter mixture thoroughly over and in between slices for flavor and moisture."
- "Return to oven for 15-20 mins till layers crisp distinctly and deeper golden tone appears."
- "Turn oven off but leave potatoes inside to rest, consolidates texture without drying out. Size dictates final crisp times, bigger need longer after glazing."
- "Stuff cheese evenly into slits, use back of spoon to push deeper. Return to warm oven 1-2 minutes, watch so cheese melts but does not burn or dry."
- "Top with dollops of sour cream, sprinkle chives, scattered crisp pancetta (or bacon). Optional pinch smoked paprika for extra aroma."
- "Serve while still hot so cheese gooey, skins brittle-crisp. Adds texture contrast and smoky richness."
- "Leftovers reheat but lose crisp. Best fresh."
- "If potatoes resist slicing, chill for 20 minutes before—firmer for precision cuts, helps avoid breaking bottom."
- "Alternate toppings like sautéed mushrooms or caramelized onions work too. Finish with drizzle of truffle oil for luxury take."
