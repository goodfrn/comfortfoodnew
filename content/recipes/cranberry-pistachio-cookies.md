ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/2 tsp salt"
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "1/2 tsp almond extract"
- "3/4 cup dried cranberries chopped"
- "3/4 cup shelled pistachios chopped"
- "1 cup powdered sugar"
- "2 tbsp milk"
- "fleur de sel or coarse salt for sprinkling"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345 degrees F and line baking sheet with parchment paper; temperature tweak shortens bake to avoid overly dry cookies."
- "Sift flour and salt together, no shortcuts here to keep lumps away; ensures even dry mix."
- "In mixer bowl, paddle attached or hand mixer ready, beat softened butter until it’s light and airy; fluffy butter traps air, key for texture."
- "Add sugar gradually, cream them well. Goes from gritty to silky paste; the base for cookie lift. Then beat in egg, vanilla, almond until fully incorporated, shiny mixture."
- "Fold flour mix in two halves, gently but quickly. Overmixing activates gluten, ruins tenderness. Dough should just come together."
- "Stir in chopped cranberries and pistachios carefully, don’t overwork or crush the nuts; cherries or chopped dried apricots swap well here."
- "Roll dough into 1-inch balls or use two-tablespoon scoop for size uniformity. Spacing: about 2 inches apart so they don't merge into a giant cookie."
- "Bake 8-12 minutes. Edges firm and pale golden, centers shiny but not jiggly. If middles are matte, you overbaked—dry city."
- "Cool on sheet for 3-5 minutes. Shape firms but inside stays soft. Transfer to wire rack until fully cool to avoid soggy bottom from steam."
- "Mix powdered sugar and milk into thick icing paste; too thin, it runs off. Snip just a corner of plastic bag for controlled drizzle."
- "Drizzle with icing while cookies still cool. Immediately sprinkle with fleur de sel or coarse salt, contrast to sweetness vital. Salt clings onto tacky icing."
- "Let rest 5-10 minutes for glaze to set; impatient? I’ve seen ruined drizzles smeared all over."
- "Store airtight; pistachios can go stale if exposed. Freeze dough for future baking sessions. Reheat gently for ‘fresh out of oven’ feel."
