- "Mix baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, all-purpose and oat flour in a bowl; set aside."
- "Cream butter and sugar until light, fluffy — beat hard, scrape sides. Stir salt and lemon zest in."
- "Add apple cider powder gradually, one packet at a time — a cloud of dust warning here. Then crack in eggs, one by one, mixing thoroughly after each."
- "Add dry ingredients slowly, half cup increments — mix but don't overwork. Dough rough but not crumbly."
- "Cover tightly with plastic wrap; refrigerate at least 2 hours — longer if dough feels tacky or soft to avoid spreading."
- "Preheat oven to 345°F. Line sheets with parchment paper."
- "Unwrap caramels; cut each into halves with kitchen shears. Keep close."
- "Mix sugar and cinnamon for rolling in a small bowl."
- "Scoop 1 tablespoon dough; roll into balls and flatten slightly in palm. Place caramel piece in center, fold dough tightly over — no seams or caramel leakage or you’ll have sticky messes."
- "Roll balls in cinnamon sugar mixture to coat — this adds texture contrast and stops spread."
- "Place on sheet 2"
- "apart; bake 12-15 minutes. Watch edges for golden glow but centers still soft and slightly puffed — poke gently if unsure."
- "Cool on sheet 5 minutes, then transfer to rack; tops firm, bottoms just set. Too firm means overbaked."
- "If dough too soft after chilling, freeze 15 minutes. If dough too dry, moist with few drops cold water. Substitutions for caramel: homemade dulce de leche cubes or chopped dried apples rehydrated."
