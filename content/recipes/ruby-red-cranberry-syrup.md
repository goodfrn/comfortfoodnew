- "Once boiling, stir to dissolve sugar completely. No gritty sugar remnants visible. Lower heat slightly; simmer commands attention now. Bubbles should steady but not frantic."
- "Keep an eye for syrup thickening and mixture turning sticky. That sticky sheen means sugar is caramelizing just right. About 8-12 minutes total but don’t rely solely on timing here."
- "Remove from heat. Set a fine mesh sieve over a bowl — a cheesecloth or even a clean dish towel works in a pinch. Push down on solids gently with back of spoon to crush and release that deep red syrup."
- "Whisk lemon juice into strained syrup swiftly. It freshens—balances acidic punch with tartness. Cool to room temperature or chill 10 minutes before use."
- "Store in airtight container. Will keep in fridge up to 3 days. Freezing extends life; freeze in small portions for quick thawing."
