ingredients:
- "For Cheese Sauce"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup whole milk, more if needed"
- "1 cup shredded smoked gouda (substitutes cheddar or Monterey Jack)"
- "½ teaspoon salt"
- "For Burritos"
- "1 tablespoon vegetable oil"
- "1 medium bell pepper, thinly sliced"
- "1 medium onion, thinly sliced"
- "2 cups shredded cooked chicken"
- "1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese"
- "1 teaspoon chipotle powder (instead of chile powder)"
- "¾ cup red enchilada sauce"
- "4 large flour tortillas"
- "Cooking spray"
instructions:
- "=== For Cheese Sauce ==="
- "Melt butter in small saucepan over medium heat, whisk in flour fast to avoid lumps. Cook paste until it smells faintly nutty, about 2 mins max but watch closely."
- "Slowly pour in milk, whisk constantly so mixture turns glossy without clumps; should be pourable but thickening."
- "Toss shredded smoked gouda in, stir vigorously until sauce blends into creamy, thick texture—no graininess allowed."
- "Keep fire moderate; sauce thickens further—skin forms slow bubbles, coat back of spoon well."
- "Taste, add salt. If sauce feels too goopy, splash in 1-2 tablespoons more milk, whisk like mad, get pourable but clingy finish."
- "Remove from heat, hold warm but not hot to stop breaking."
- "=== For Burritos ==="
- "Preheat oven to 360°F—a bit higher than usual, targets browned tortilla edges without drying filling."
- "Heat oil in skillet over medium heat. Toss in sliced bell pepper and onion; sweat until soft and shiny but still with a bit of bite, around 6-8 minutes. Avoid browning too much or you’ll lose veggie sweetness."
- "Dump veggies into a large bowl. Add shredded chicken, Monterey Jack, chipotle powder, and enchilada sauce."
- "Mix well, taste—chipotle should kick sulfurous, smoky heat, a nice change from regular chile powder’s sharper tone."
- "Spray a 9x13 pan. Divide the mixture evenly over tortillas, roll snug and tight—loose wrapping here means soggy middle."
- "Nestle burritos close side by side in baking dish, room for cheese sauce on top."
- "Ladle over your cheese sauce generously. If feeling daring, add extra enchilada sauce drizzles for acid punch."
- "Slide into oven uncovered; bake 17-22 minutes or until tortilla rims turn golden and crispy, edges bubble with heat. Smell will shift—smoky, cheesy, toasty all at once."
- "Pull out, let rest 5 minutes to set layers."
- "Top with chopped cilantro, sliced radishes, or dollops of sour cream. Always the fun finishing touch."
- "Serves four. Store leftovers refrigerated—reheat covered in microwave at low power to keep cheese from splitting."
