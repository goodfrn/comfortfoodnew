## What You Need for No Bake Chocolate Cheesecake

Medium to large strawberries—10 of them. Really medium. Tiny ones disappear under chocolate, giant ones get too thick and the point doesn't coat.

White candy melts work fine. Or white couverture if you want silkier chocolate, but honestly the melts do the job.

Blue sparkling sugar sprinkles. Or crushed freeze-dried blueberries if you want less crunch and more tartness. Both work.

That's it. Three ingredients. One bowl. One sheet pan.

## How to Make No Bake Cheesecake Strawberries

Clean the strawberries under cold water. Shake them dry. Actually dry—not damp. Put them in the fridge uncovered and leave them for 90 minutes minimum. This matters more than you think. Moisture makes the chocolate slide right off and pool at the bottom in clumps.

Microwave the white chocolate in 20-second bursts. Stir between each one. You're looking for glossy, fully melted, zero graininess. Takes maybe three bursts, four if you're being cautious. Too thick? Add a tiny splash of coconut oil or any neutral oil and stir until it loosens up. Don't drown it—a teaspoon's probably enough.

Hold the strawberry by the leafy top. Dip it all the way in. Let it sit in the chocolate for a second so it coats fully, then lift with a twisting motion to shake the excess off. Sloppy chocolate clumps and sticks unevenly. You want a thin, even coat that actually holds.

## No Bake Cheesecake Dipping and Setting

Right after the white chocolate coat sets just enough to touch—maybe 10 seconds—dip the pointed end into the blue sprinkles or blueberry bits. Speed matters here. Wait too long and the chocolate dulls and hardens. The sprinkles won't stick as clean. Don't coat the whole berry with sprinkles. Looks messy. Just the point. Just enough to see color.

Lay them on parchment paper as you go. Listen for the slight click as the chocolate firms up. That's your cue that it's setting right. Refrigerate for 8 to 11 minutes. Eight minutes is the floor—anything less and the chocolate's still tacky and falls apart. Eleven minutes is the ceiling—go longer and condensation pools on the surface and your sprinkles start dissolving into the chocolate. Gross.

## No Bake Cheesecake Tips and Storage

Humidity kills these. If your kitchen's damp, pat them dry with a paper towel right before serving. Condensation's the enemy.

Store in the fridge. Max 10 hours. After that the chocolate gets that white film and the texture changes. Not dangerous. Just not great.

Don't try to make these ahead and freeze them. The thaw brings condensation and everything falls apart.

Humidity spikes? Keep them in a container with a paper towel to absorb moisture. Works most of the time.

## Frequently Asked Questions About No Bake Cheesecake Recipe

**Can I use regular chocolate instead of white candy melts?**
Dark or milk chocolate works. Looks different. Changes the whole thing. White's cleaner with the blueberries.

**What if my chocolate gets grainy?**
Overheated. Start over with new chocolate. Can't really fix it. The 20-second bursts prevent this—don't skip them.

**Do I really need to refrigerate the strawberries for 90 minutes first?**
Yeah. Moisture makes the chocolate slide off. Trust it.

**Can I make these without the sprinkles?**
Sure. They still work. Less visual, but the chocolate holds fine on its own.

**How long do these actually last?**
10 hours in the fridge before things get weird. Could probably push to 12 if your fridge is dry. Not worth the risk.

**Can I use freeze-dried berries instead of sprinkles?**
Crushed blueberries work better than sprinkles texture-wise. More tart. Some people like it more. Try both.
