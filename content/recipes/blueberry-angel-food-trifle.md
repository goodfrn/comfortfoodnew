ingredients:
- "1 angel food cake cooled completely and cut into 1-inch cubes"
- "1 can cherry pie filling substitute for blueberry pie filling"
- "1 package instant vanilla pudding mix 3.4 ounces"
- "1 1/2 cups milk use whole or 2%"
- "1/4 cup sour cream adds richness and tang"
- "1 tub 8 ounces whipped topping Cool Whip or homemade whipped cream"
instructions:
- "Cut fully cooled angel food cake into cubes about 1 inch, use serrated knife for clean cuts without crushing airy texture."
- "Pick a 9x9 metal or glass baking pan or better yet a clear trifle bowl if you want to show off layers. Spread half the cake cubes evenly on the bottom."
- "Spoon just over half the cherry pie filling evenly atop cake pieces, careful not to break cake cubes."
- "Place the remaining cake cubes on top of the pie filling layer, pressing gently but not smashing."
- "In a medium bowl mix pudding mix, milk, and sour cream. Stir vigorously about 2 minutes to avoid lumps, mixture should thicken slightly but still spreadable."
- "Spread pudding-sour cream combo over the top cake layer to cover entirely without disturbing layers below."
- "Generously spread the whipped topping evenly over the pudding layer for creamy cover."
- "Drop remaining cherry pie filling by spoonfuls on top, don’t overly smooth; rustic look is fine."
- "Refrigerate uncovered at least 4 hours to let cake soak up moisture and flavors. Overnight chilling yields better melding but keep wrapped if longer."
