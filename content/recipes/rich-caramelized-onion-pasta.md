ingredients:
- "6 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced"
- "3 cloves garlic, minced"
- "2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves"
- "1 teaspoon chili flakes"
- "1 teaspoon sea salt"
- "2 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "5 cups beef broth or low-sodium vegetable broth"
- "12 ounces pasta (fusilli or rigatoni work well)"
- "6 ounces cream cheese, softened"
- "1/2 cup dry white wine (optional, can replace with broth)"
- "Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish"
- "Grated Parmesan or pecorino for topping"
instructions:
- "Butter melting in heavy-bottom skillet. Swirling, heating medium-high until it murmurs and just browns on the edges."
- "Onions go in handfuls. Stir for 3 minutes until softening. Add next batch. Salt lightly, breaks down onions faster. Keep stirring every couple minutes. Softening to glossy translucence."
- "Heat down to medium-low. Now it’s patience hour. Stir every 3-5 minutes. Wait for deep amber color. No rushing. Sniff sweet, dark, almost jammy. Around 50 minutes. Add splash of white wine or broth to lift browned bits if bottom sticks. Deglazing vital here or bitter burnt notes will sneak in."
- "While onions slow-cook their magic, boil pasta in salted water. Cook until just shy of tender. Al dente— slight chew, not mush. Reserve 2 cups cooking water before draining. Stash it away for sauce fix-ups."
- "Add garlic, thyme, chili flake, and a pinch more salt to the caramelized onions. Stir and let aromas bloom, 1-2 minutes, low heat so garlic doesn’t scorch."
- "Sprinkle flour over onions, stir well so it clings, bubbles up lightly. This thickens sauce gently. Then pour broth in gradually. Simmer, scraping bottom, for 3-4 minutes until sauce thickens but still fluid."
- "Add drained pasta directly to pan. Splash in half cup pasta water— bind those noodles with the sauce. Stir thoroughly so each strand picks up that rich onion glaze."
- "Cream cheese chunks go in now. Another half cup pasta water to loosen sauce. Stir until velvety smooth, coating noodles evenly. If sauce feels thick, splash more pasta water, less risk of drying out."
- "Taste, tweak salt and chili. Sauce should hug pasta like silk and a little fire. More pasta water can fix dry or thick spots. Finish with fresh parsley and grated cheese on top before serving."
