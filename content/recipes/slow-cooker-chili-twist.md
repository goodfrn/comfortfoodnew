ingredients:
- "1 pound lean ground beef, browned and drained"
- "1 large yellow onion, diced"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil (instead of butter)"
- "1 can (15 oz) fire-roasted crushed tomatoes (swap tomato sauce)"
- "1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed"
- "1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed"
- "1/2 cup tomato paste"
- "2 teaspoons ground cumin"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika (swap chili powder)"
- "1/2 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon onion powder"
- "Salt and pepper to taste"
- "1 cup beef broth (low sodium)"
- "Greek yogurt, shredded sharp cheddar cheese and chopped green onions for serving"
instructions:
- "Brown ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once no longer pink, drain fat thoroughly to avoid greasy chili. Transfer beef to slow cooker."
- "In same skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion, cook stirring regularly, 7 minutes till translucent and sweet fragrance, careful not to brown heavily. Onions release natural sugars here; patience pays off."
- "Add sautéed onions to slow cooker with meat."
- "Dump in crushed tomatoes, kidney and black beans, tomato paste, beef broth. Sprinkle cumin, smoked paprika, garlic and onion powders. Season lightly with salt and pepper, remember easy to adjust later. Stir everything vigorously; coat ingredients well."
- "Cook on low for about 4 and a half hours. Rather than rely on timer alone, watch chili consistency: it should thicken, beans tender but intact, beef soft yet meaty. Give a stir halfway through. If too thick near end, add splash more broth."
- "Taste test before serving; adjust seasoning if needed."
- "Serve hot topped with dollop Greek yogurt, sharp cheddar cheese melting on top, and thick-sliced green onions for crunch and fresh bite."
