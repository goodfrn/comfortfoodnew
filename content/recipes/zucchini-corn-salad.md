ingredients:
- "2 medium zucchini spiralized on smallest blade"
- "1 1/4 cups fresh corn kernels sautéed lightly"
- "1/2 cup chopped tomatoes"
- "1/2 cup canned chickpeas rinsed and drained"
- "3 tablespoons fresh lime juice"
- "1 tablespoon honey"
- "1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil"
- "1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "1/4 teaspoon garlic powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt or to taste"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley"
- "1/3 cup crumbled feta cheese"
instructions:
- "Spiralize zucchini with the tiniest blade. Aim for fine strands, almost vermicelli-thin. No spiralizer handy? Use a sharp knife or julienne peeler to mimic. The goal: zucchini delicate enough to mingle smoothly without drowning."
- "Heat pan moderately, toss corn in dry or lightly oiled. Listen for gentle popping and slight fragrance rise. Corn should soften but keep bite—about 6-8 minutes. If kernels blacken, reduce heat next try."
- "Dump zucchini strands in a medium-large bowl. Add warm corn kernels, chopped tomatoes and rinsed chickpeas. Toss cautiously—zucchini fragile. Mix until combined but not crushed."
- "In a small bowl, whisk lime juice, honey, olive oil, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, salt and pepper. Whisk vigorously until glossy, slightly thickened. Mustard helps emulsify; don’t skip or dressing partitions."
- "Pour dressing over zucchini mix gradually, tossing gently with two forks or salad spoons. Coating must be even; flavor must marry each thread and kernel."
- "Scatter fresh parsley, then crumble feta cheese over top. The herbal brightness and salty creaminess cut through raw zucchini’s mildness."
- "Serve immediately or let sit 10-15 minutes, allowing zucchini to soften slightly and flavors meld. Don’t wait too long—too much sitting turns zucchini watery."
- "Notes: To swap kidney beans for chickpeas was deliberate—creamier texture, earthier bite. Black beans suggested alternative, adds depth. Honey can be subbed with agave or maple syrup to change sweetness dimension."
- "Watch zucchini strands when tossing; bruising makes a watery mess fast. Use gentle folding motions rather than stirring aggressively."
- "If salad feels flat, lift with dash of extra lime juice or sprinkle coarse sea salt on top for crunch contrast."
