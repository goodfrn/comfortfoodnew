ingredients:
- "1 package softened cream cheese (225g)"
- "1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese (reduced 5%)"
- "1 3/4 cups cooked shredded chicken (change from 2 cups)"
- "1 packet ranch seasoning mix (1 tbsp)"
- "2 tbsp mayonnaise (cut from 3 tbsp)"
- "1 cup chopped pecans (or substitute toasted sunflower seeds)"
- "2 green olives"
- "Fresh chives for garnish"
- "Plastic wrap for shaping"
instructions:
- "===Mix and Form==="
- "In a large bowl, beat softened cream cheese until creamy but not runny—watch for that workable dense texture that's not cold-stiff."
- "Add shredded sharp cheddar, measured just under a full cup to reduce dryness, then toss in shredded chicken. Using half a cooked chicken breast shredded fine ensures moisture without clumps."
- "Sprinkle ranch seasoning—not heaping—about a tablespoon. Stir it all up with mayonnaise reduced to two tablespoons; swapping mayo for Greek yogurt gives a tang but thinner consistency, adjust accordingly. Combine evenly. The mixture should stick together but not be soupy."
- "Lay a large piece of plastic wrap, scoop mixture onto center. Begin shaping into a ball by tucking plastic wrap edges under, pressing gently to compact and smooth out lumps. Avoid over-compressing or it gets rubbery."
- "Place wrapped ball on plate, refrigerate for roughly 3 hours 45 minutes (tweak depending on fridge chill strength), until firm and holds shape when grabbed without drooping."
- "===Coating and Details==="
- "Unwrap carefully on a clean surface."
- "Chop pecans (or sunflower seeds if allergic or no nuts) finely but not powder—want biting texture."
- "Roll the firm cheese ball gently in nuts, pressing them on but not crushing the shell."
- "For ‘hair’ pierce toothpick-sized holes randomly on one side’s top third. Insert fresh chive pieces—snip longer bits; they should curl naturally, adding a wild look."
- "Eyes made with olive slices pushed in gently; olives can be pitted, or for a twist, use small pickled pearl onions for a sharper contrast."
- "Nose: Spear two green olives each on its own toothpick, cross and press bases together forming a bulbous nose. Balance carefully; if it slips, refrigerate again fifteen minutes sharp to re-firm before retrying."
- "Chill at least 20 minutes once decorated to set look and prevent olive sliding."
- "===Serving and Tips==="
- "Bring out about 15 minutes before eating for easier spreading. Should yield a creamy but firm bite—too cold and it's stiff, too warm and it melts away."
- "If mix seems too dry, stir in small splashes of milk or chicken broth; too wet, add a pinch of instant dry crumbs or shredded cheese."
- "Chilling time varies—when it feels dense but not rock hard, it's ready. Too long leads to chalkiness. Experience counts here."
- "Plastic wrap trick: gather edges, twist tight to shape rapidly, then flatten slightly for stability in fridge."
- "Use kitchen shears to cut chives quickly, no bruising or crushed flavor."
- "For allergies substitute pecans with finely crushed pretzels for crunch and salt contrast."
- "Hands down, avoid overmixing—cheese gets gluey."
- "Listen for clicking pecan crunch when slicing to judge coating and bites."
- "Olive toothpicks work well but check for sharp ends—safer to trim tips to avoid guests’ accidents."
- "Good for parties, easily scaled or reshaped; can even freeze wrapped tight then thaw slowly overnight in fridge back before decorating."
