ingredients:
- "2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 tablespoon arrowroot powder"
- "½ teaspoon baking soda"
- "½ teaspoon sea salt"
- "1 cup unsalted butter, melted"
- "1 ½ cups packed light brown sugar"
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract"
- "1 cup mini M&M's, divided"
- "½ cup crushed pretzels"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 345°F. Grease 9x13 pan with shortening. Line bottom with parchment—helps for perfect release. No quick wax paper substitute; messes the crust."
- "Sift together flour, arrowroot, baking soda, salt in medium bowl. Arrowroot makes crumb lighter than cornstarch, subtle but noticeable. Set aside."
- "In big bowl, stir melted butter + brown sugar until granular texture appears, like wet sand. Crucial. Too smooth means sugar dissolved prematurely; texture will suffer."
- "Add eggs one at a time, plus vanilla. Scrape sides bottom frequently; eggs need full incorporation to avoid dense blondies. Overwork here and you get dense, tough bars."
- "Add dry stuff slowly. Mix just until few streaks remain; overmixing develops gluten. Batter should be thick, slightly sticky—if runny, flour measurement off, expect flat blondies."
- "Fold in all but 2 tablespoons of M&Ms and crushed pretzels—salt cuts sweetness, adds crunch. Pretzel substitution unexpected but wakes up the chew and adds flavor depth."
- "Spread batter into pan evenly. Smooth top gently. Scatter reserved M&Ms on top, makes colorful pop and prevents sinking into batter"
- "Bake 23-30 minutes. Watch edges firm and pull slightly away from pan. Center jiggles gently but not liquidy. Insert toothpick must come out with moist crumbs. Too dry = overbake, no crackly top."
- "Cool completely—blondies still warm crumble or stick messily. Patience here pays off for clean slices."
- "Lift parchment, slice into 12 squares. Serve or store airtight. Stale quickly so no dawdling."
