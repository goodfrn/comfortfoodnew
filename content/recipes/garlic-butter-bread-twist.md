ingredients:
- "1 loaf French bread"
- "1/2 cup softened unsalted butter"
- "1 1/2 tsp sea salt"
- "4 cloves garlic minced finely"
- "3 tbsp fresh parsley chopped"
- "1 tbsp olive oil"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 430°F, toss the numbers a bit but watch bread, don’t burn crust."
- "Cut French loaf lengthwise, split it like a book but not too thin; thick enough to soak butter."
- "Mix butter softened, salt, garlic, parsley; skip mechanical mixers, hand stirring better - feel butter texture smooth."
- "Spread little more than half of butter mix on one bread half—load it but not sloppy. Press the halves back together snugly."
- "Wrap tightly in foil; pack foil well to trap steam. Slide foil parcel into oven rack center - close it."
- "Bake 28-32 mins, crack a slit at 28 to peek inside; butter should pool and bread steam through, slight golden tint under foil."
- "Meanwhile, melt leftover butter mix in small pan with olive oil - low heat, about 2-4 mins till garlic scent hits. Don’t burn! Listen for soft sizzle, bubbles form, aromatic steam."
- "Pull bread out, unwrap carefully (steam sting warning). Check softness by pressing top gently, should feel warm, pliable, butter fully melted in crumb."
- "Brush or drizzle melted garlic butter evenly on warm bread surface – extra punch, shiny gloss, fresh parsley sprinkled for bright green pop."
- "Slice thick chunks; grab while warm, crust crunchy, center buttery soft, garlic punch hits upfront, herbs linger."
- "If parsley scarce swap with basil or chives – brings softer herb floral notes. Butter can swap for ghee or flavored oil in pinch. Use fresh garlic always, powdered dulls depth."
- "Tight foil wrap crucial; trap steam so bread heats through without drying, avoid opening before 28 min unless smell tells story."
- "Leftover butter mixture melts fast, don’t walk away or garlic scorches - smells burnt ruin fast."
- "No French bread? Try good Italian loaf, adjust baking time 3-5 min less for thinner crumb."
- "Quick check for doneness: top crust slight give when poked, middle warm to touch but firm. If tough, add 3-5 min wrapped again."
- "Serve hot with soups, stews, or use as sandwich base. Cold next day? Toast lightly, butter fresh again."
