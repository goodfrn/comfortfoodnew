ingredients:
- "2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour spooned and leveled"
- "1 tablespoon sugar"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter cut into pea-size pieces or frozen and grated"
- "1 cup buttermilk or unsweetened almond milk with 1 teaspoon lemon juice (rested 5 minutes)"
- "2 tablespoons melted butter for brushing"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 460°F for an extra quick rise; line pan with parchment or silicone mat."
- "Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, baking soda in large bowl. No sifting unless lumpy flour."
- "Cut butter into flour till pea-size bits remain; frozen butter grated saves time and creates texture variation for flakiness."
- "Pour almond milk mixture in, stir gently just till dough holds together; should feel like thick mashed potatoes, tacky but not sticky. If too wet, dust flour in small pinches; too dry, splash extra milk."
- "Turn dough to floured surface. Knead very briefly if crumbly. Press dough into rectangle roughly 10x6 inches."
- "Fold dough into thirds (like a business letter). Flour surface and dough lightly to avoid sticking."
- "Press out again to 10x6 rectangle. Repeat tri-fold twice more — three times total — builds layers. Handle gently, don’t overwork or layers toughen."
- "Final flattening to approx 3/4 inch thickness. Flour 2 1/2 inch sharp cutter, cut straight down. Avoid twisting cutter—twist seals edges shut, no rise."
- "Place biscuits close together on pan for tender edges; spaced apart for crunch. No gaps greater than 1 inch."
- "Gather scraps, reroll cautiously; fewer layers but worth it for more biscuits."
- "Brush tops with melted butter to encourage browning and add rich aroma during baking."
- "Bake 13 to 15 minutes. Look for golden tan, puffed tops, sides slightly firm but yielding to touch."
- "Remove and cool 5 minutes on rack. Serve warm with honey, jam, or savory butter."

faq:
- "q: Can I substitute milk for buttermilk? a: Yes but acidity important. Use almond milk plus lemon juice rested 5 minutes or plain milk with vinegar. No acid equals less rise, denser crumb. Pasteurized milk lacks tang but still bakes. Chemical leavening needs acid to activate fully."
- "q: Why fold dough three times? a: Folding traps air and folds layers of butter with dough. This has huge effect on flakiness. Skip or over-knead and biscuits toughen or shrink. Each fold builds layers. Patience here pays off. Layer count defines texture more than precise minutes."
- "q: Dough too sticky how fix? a: Dust flour in slow, cautious pinches. If after flour addition still sticky, rest in fridge to firm. Too dry? Splash almond milk or buttermilk. Feel key, no measuring here. Stickiness varies by humidity, flour brand. Adjust on sight and touch."
- "q: How store leftovers? a: Cool fully at room temp. Wrap tight in foil or airtight container. Refrigerate up to 3 days, reheat in oven to restore crispness. Freeze wrapped in plastic then foil for 1 month. Toast frozen biscuit halves straight from freezer or warm whole baked to revive."
