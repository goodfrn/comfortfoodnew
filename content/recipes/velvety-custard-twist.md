ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons granulated sugar"
- "2 teaspoons cornstarch"
- "3 cups half-and-half (substituted for milk to add richness)"
- "3 large egg yolks"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1/2 teaspoon almond extract (replacement for vanilla extract)"
- "pinch salt"
instructions:
- "=== Custard Base ==="
- "Use a heavy-bottom saucepan, 3 to 4 quart capacity. Combine sugar, cornstarch, and half-and-half (room temperature) in the pan. Whisk vigorously to eliminate lumps pre-heat."
- "Set stove to medium heat. Stand ready, whisk continuously. The mix will remain thin, then subtle bubbles start forming at the edges—low simmers, not crazy boiling. Watch the surface: it thickens visibly around 7 to 9 minutes here. You want it like warm pudding consistency. If it’s sluggish or stuck, increase heat slightly but don’t scorch the bottom."
- "=== Egg Tempering ==="
- "Off heat now. Ladle about 1 cup of warm custard mix into a separate bowl. Pour slowly into egg yolks while whisking nonstop. This gradual warming prevents curdling and gets the eggs ready."
- "Pour the egg yolk mixture back into the pan. Return to medium-low heat. Whisk steadily for 3 to 4 minutes more, until the custard thickens further and looks glossy and smooth—almost coats the whisk. Important to keep whisk moving to avoid scrambling."
- "=== Finishing Touches ==="
- "Remove from heat promptly once thickened. Toss in butter pieces. Whisk until fully melted and incorporated. Stir in almond extract and a pinch of salt to lift flavors."
- "Strain custard through fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to catch any cooked egg fragments or clumps. This step transforms texture from good to superbly velvety."
- "Immediately press plastic wrap directly onto the custard surface—no air gaps. Stops the skin forming, which is both annoying and visually unappealing. Chill at least 2 hours before serving to let flavors meld and custard set firm."
- "=== Notes ==="
- "If half-and-half not on hand, milk can be used, but custard less rich and creamy. For dairy-free, full-fat coconut milk works, but expect texture shift and stronger flavor."
- "Make sure to use cornstarch, not flour; flour yields a grainy feel."
- "Don’t rush the cooking process. Low bubbles, visual thickness, and whisk coat tests beat timer every time."
- "If lumps form, whisk aggressively off heat or pressure thru sieve again."
- "Almond extract swap imparts subtle nutty aroma that pairs well with stone fruits or berry desserts."
- "Keep bowl and utensils ready before starting—timing tight during tempering."
- "Plastic wrap method is a game-changer—don’t skip if texture matters."
