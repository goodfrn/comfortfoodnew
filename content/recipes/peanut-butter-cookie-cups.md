ingredients:
- "1/2 cup brown sugar"
- "1/2 cup white sugar"
- "1 cup natural peanut butter (smooth or chunky works, I prefer natural for less sweetness)"
- "1/3 cup coconut oil (replaces shortening, adds subtle nuttiness and better for texture)"
- "1/3 cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking powder"
- "24 mini Reese’s peanut butter cups, unwrapped"
instructions:
- "Whisk dry ingredients — flour, salt, baking soda, baking powder — in medium bowl until evenly mixed; sets foundation for rise and texture."
- "Brown and white sugars, peanut butter, coconut oil, butter, and egg tossed into a large bowl. Hand mixer, medium speed. Beat ‘til creamy, sugar dissolving into peanut butter’s oily richness. Stop scraping sides; impatient? Resist."
- "Slow speed next. Dry blend folds in, just shy of homogenous dough. No overmixing; gluten development means toughness. Gritty bits gone, combined but not silky."
- "Cover dough, fridge chill minimum two hours, preferably up to three. Cold firm dough rolls easier, prevents spreading and sloppy shapes. Tried baking fresh — disaster; cookies run and lose body."
- "Oven preheated to 380°F. Temperature tweak from usual 375 for slight extra browning without drying."
- "Grease mini muffin tins liberally — coconut oil spray works. Prevent sticky mess; use parchment circles if you lack spray."
- "Pinch dough one tablespoon-sized balls, about 1 to 1 1/4 inch diameter. I eyeball by rolling dough between fingers. Less messy when hands chilled too."
- "Drop each ball in muffin cup. Slightly press but don’t flatten. They’ll puff, rise, crisp on edges, chewy center. Dense enough to hold cool peanut butter cup later."
- "Bake 14 minutes or when edges shift from pale to light golden brown. Listen for faint crackles, smell nutty butter aroma swelling. Avoid dark brown rims; signs of overbaking."
- "Pull from oven; hot, still soft. Press unwrapped mini Reese’s peanut butter cups into cookie center immediately. Heat softens candy cup into melty embrace. If wait, candy stays hard and uninviting."
- "Let cool fully in pan before removals. I jiggle pan gently to free cups from tin. Place on wire rack to cool and resist soggy bottoms."
- "Cookies store well in airtight container up to five days—if they last. For chewiness refresher, zap one for 10 seconds microwave."
- "Notes — coconut oil swap adds depth; not too much; keeps dough pliable. Shy on egg? Use half; too much makes cookies cakey. No shortening? Butter-coconut oil blend is my workaround."
- "Sugar swap? Yes: reduce brown sugar by 2 tbsp and add 2 tbsp maple syrup, adjust flour slightly to compensate moisture."
- "Mini Reese’s are key for melty surprise; chopped peanut butter cups make mess. Use a small knife for unwrap when impatient."
- "Mistake alert: dough too soft after mixing? Chill longer. Dough crumbly? Add teaspoon milk or egg, fold gently."
- "Do not overbake. These cookies are fleetingly soft at center when perfect. Overdo and chewiness turns dry."
- "No mixer? Use sturdy spatula and arm power. Cream fats well first for emulsification."
- "Thought about subbing peanut butter for almond? Try it; flavor changes but still lush. Texture subtly shifts; add splash vanilla extract to boost aroma."
