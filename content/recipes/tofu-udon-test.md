Udon noodles with tofu and green beans. No fuss. Crumbled tofu browned until it gets some crust. Garlic and ginger build the base. The sauce — ketchup, soy, peanut butter, brown sugar, chili paste — sticks to everything without being heavy. Green beans stay crisp. Noodles stay chewy. It comes together fast and feeds four people without drama.

## Why You'll Love This

Tofu gets actual texture here — not soft and sad. Browning it properly takes 12 minutes but the difference is real. The sauce thickens around it and coats every noodle. Green beans cut lengthwise add crunch without fighting the noodles. The whole bowl is plant-based but nobody at the table is going to miss the meat.

## What Makes This Sauce Work

Peanut butter and ketchup sound wrong together. They're not. The ketchup adds acidity and body. Brown sugar rounds it out. Sambal oelek cuts through the richness. Lowered the peanut butter slightly from the original — the sauce coats better and doesn't go heavy. Adjusted brown sugar up a touch to balance the soy salt. Sauce quantity bumped so every noodle gets covered.

## Tips for Getting It Right

Brown the tofu longer than you think. Stir constantly over medium-high. The goal is a real crust on the outside — that's what holds up in the sauce. If you add garlic and ginger too early they burn before the tofu is ready. Wait until the browning is done.

Blanch the beans for exactly 4 minutes then straight into cold water. Cut them lengthwise after cooling, not before — they stay crisper that way. Cutting before blanching makes them soft.

Save the pasta water before you drain the noodles. The sauce thickens a lot after simmering with the tofu. A splash of pasta water loosens it back without diluting the flavor.

## FAQ

**Can I use other noodles?**
Yes but udon works best here. The chewiness holds against the thick sauce. Thinner noodles get lost. Rice noodles are less sturdy. Fresh noodles preferred if you can find them.

**How do I make it nut-free?**
Omit the peanut topping. The peanut butter stays in the sauce for body — swap it with sunflower seed butter if needed. Flavor changes a bit but the sauce still thickens.

**Tofu sticking to the pan?**
Pat it dry before cooking. Moisture is the problem. Use non-stick or a well-oiled skillet. Stainless steel needs significantly more oil.

**Can I reheat leftovers?**
Yes — store up to 2 days in the fridge. Reheat in a skillet with a splash of water to loosen the sauce. Microwave works but the noodles dry out. Not suitable for freezing.
