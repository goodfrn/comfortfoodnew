ingredients:
- "1 cup (226g) unsalted butter softened"
- "3⁄4 cup (150g) granulated sugar"
- "1⁄2 cup (100g) light brown sugar packed"
- "1⁄2 cup (120g) pumpkin puree or substitute 1⁄3 cup Greek yogurt for tangier"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2 1⁄4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour sifted"
- "1⁄2 tsp cream of tartar"
- "3⁄4 tsp baking powder reduced from usual"
- "1 tsp fine sea salt"
- "1 1⁄2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "1 tsp ground cardamom replacing pumpkin pie spice"
- "For sugar coating: 1⁄4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tsp cinnamon"
instructions:
- "Whip softened butter with sugars until mixture is light in color but stop before overly fluffy — overbeating makes cookies spread too thin."
- "Stir in pumpkin or Greek yogurt for silkiness, then egg and vanilla until combined but don’t overmix or batter gets runny."
- "Sift together flour, cream of tartar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and cardamom for even distribution and lift."
- "Add dry ingredients in two batches to wet, mixing gently. Dough should look cohesive, dense but not sticky — if too wet, toss in tablespoon flour."
- "Cover bowl tightly and chill minimum 1.5 hours. Chilling firms butter helping cookies hold shape, encourages flavors to meld."
- "Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Prepare baking sheet lined with parchment or silicone mat — no greasing, cookies will spread otherwise."
- "Mix sugar and cinnamon for coating in small shallow bowl."
- "Scoop 1 1/2 tablespoons cookie dough and roll loosely in sugar mix, coating all sides but not compressed."
- "Place dough balls spaced minimum 2 inches apart on sheet. Use flat bottom glass to press down gently — aim for around 1/2 inch thickness. Pressed too hard? Cookies end up dense."
- "Listen for quiet crackle of edges starting to firm; skim a glance after 10 minutes. Look for puffed tops with slightly golden edges but centers still soft — oven times vary."
- "Remove from oven, leave on sheet 2 minutes to settle — cookies fragile when hot."
- "Transfer carefully using a spatula to wire rack to cool completely to set texture. Cookies firm as they cool but resist touching warm or they break apart."
- "Drop back if you tried variations — especially use of Greek yogurt or cardamom—that subtle changes bring a whole new cookie life."
