ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 tablespoon fresh lemon zest"
- "12 tablespoons unsalted butter cold, cut into cubes"
- "2 large eggs beaten"
- "5 cups diced fresh or frozen strawberries"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice"
- "3 tablespoons cornstarch"
- "3/4 cup packed light brown sugar"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 355°F. Line 9×13 pan with parchment paper leaving sides to lift bars out easy."
- "Toss flour, sugar, salt, lemon zest in bowl. Blend low with cold cubed butter until butter bits no bigger than peas. Not smooth dough."
- "Drizzle beaten eggs over butter-flour mix. Stir just till dough clumps into coarse crumbs. Too much mixing? Tough bars. Too dry? Add teaspoon cold water."
- "Press about two-thirds of dough evenly onto bottom of pan. Pat firmly. No cracks. Set rest aside for topping."
- "In big bowl, fold chopped strawberries with lemon juice, cornstarch, and brown sugar. Watch for liquid pooling - stir gently."
- "Spoon berry mix evenly over crust, spread gently. Expect some juice to seep through dough base."
- "Scatter remaining dough in small chunks and crumbles over strawberry layer. Resist flattening—rustic clumps bake best."
- "Bake 47 to 52 minutes until crumble top is golden brown and bubbling juices peek through edges. Listen for gentle crackling shrink sounds."
- "Cool bars fully on wire rack in pan. When firm, lift with parchment edges. Chill a bit if you want cleaner cuts."
- "Cut bars once cooled. Soft but holds shape. If bars are wet on bottom, bake a few more minutes next time or chill longer."
