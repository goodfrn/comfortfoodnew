ingredients:
- "8 small-medium red potatoes"
- "4 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 teaspoon hot chili sauce"
- "2 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper"
- "1/3 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese"
- "3 tablespoons crumbled cooked bacon"
- "1/3 cup sour cream"
- "2 tablespoons thinly sliced scallions"
instructions:
- "Fill a medium saucepan with water about three-quarters full. Bring rapidly to a rolling boil. Meanwhile, scrub the potatoes under cold water, removing dirt and blemishes. The key is clean skins for crispness later."
- "Drop potatoes into boiling water. Boil more or less depending on size—usually between 18 to 25 minutes. Test with a fork; it should pierce easily but potatoes need to hold shape—no mushy falling apart."
- "Preheat oven to 390F for gentle browning while keeping soft interior. Remove potatoes with slotted spoon onto paper towels. Pat dry thoroughly—wet skins won't crisp up."
- "Prepare baking sheet with parchment paper. Arrange potatoes spaced apart. Smash each once with the bottom of a small plate or a heavy glass, just enough to crack skin and flatten slightly. Should keep potato mostly intact."
- "Pop baking sheet in oven for about 18 minutes. Listen for crackling sounds indicating skin crisping. Meanwhile, melt butter gently, stir in hot chili sauce, minced garlic, salt, and fresh cracked pepper until combined. Hot sauce variation adds subtle heat; smoked paprika or cayenne works too."
- "After initial bake, pull potatoes and drizzle spicy butter mixture evenly over them. Return to oven another 5 to 7 minutes. Butter seeps in, skins turn golden and bubbling at edges."
- "Remove, sprinkle sharp cheddar cheese and bacon crumbs immediately so cheese melts from residual heat. Dollop sour cream on top, scatter scallions last for fresh bite and color pop. Serve right away or crispy edges soften."
- "If pressed for time, bake potatoes whole wrapped in foil first to speed boiling stage, but expect less crispy skin. For vegetarian swap, smoked sausage or sautéed mushrooms add meaty texture. Always watch potato doneness visually—overcooking makes them dry inside."

faq:
- "q: How to know when potatoes are done boiling? a: Fork test mostly. Should pierce easily but not fall apart. Test several potatoes. Too firm means tough to smash, too soft means mushy. Watch boiling time range but adapt by feel. Size varies, so check even as you boil."
- "q: Can I substitute bacon for something else? a: Yes, smoked sausage or sautéed mushrooms works best. For vegetarian, toasted walnuts add crunch and umami. Avoid sweet substitutes. Bacon smoke is hard to mimic but alternatives get close. Add at same step for texture balance."
- "q: Why won't my potatoes crisp up in oven? a: Usually wet skins. Pat dry after boiling, use parchment paper, spaced apart. Oven temp too low or covered potatoes trap steam. Also, smashing gently keeps surface area but not mashed paste. Try higher heat or longer bake, watch for crackles, smell butter and garlic aroma. If no sizzle, no crisp."
- "q: How to store leftovers? a: Store cool in airtight container. Reheat in oven or toaster oven for crisp edges. Microwave softens skins, loses texture. Can freeze but expect softer texture. Best eaten same day, edges soften and toppings lose freshness fast."
