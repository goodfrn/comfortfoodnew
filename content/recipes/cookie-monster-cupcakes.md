ingredients:
- "1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "3/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1 cup buttermilk, room temperature"
- "1/3 cup vegetable oil"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 cup boiling water"
- "1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened"
- "3 and 1/2 cups powdered sugar"
- "2-3 tablespoons heavy whipping cream"
- "2 large candy eyes per cupcake"
- "12 chocolate chip cookies, halved"
instructions:
- "=== For The Cupcakes ==="
- "Prepare cupcake liners in the tins, preheat oven to 345°F. Flour and sugar should always be sifted together—avoids lumps sneak."
- "In a wide bowl toss together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Dry mix must be even—lumps mean uneven rise."
- "In a different container, beat eggs until pale; add buttermilk, oil, and vanilla extract. Buttermilk here adds a tang that brightens chocolate rich notes."
- "Pour wet into dry. Stir gently until just mixed. Avoid overmix or risk dense cakes."
- "Switch to electric mixer, medium speed. Beat batter 3 minutes exactly. It's the air incorporated here that lifts up crumb."
- "Add boiling water gradually, first a few spoonfuls, then the rest in a thin stream while mixing slowly. Batter thins out—that's right."
- "Scoop into liners filling about 70% full. Overfill, they spill; underfill, cupcakes end up flat and dry."
- "Bake in center rack for roughly 20 minutes. The smell of cocoa fills kitchen, edges pull slightly from pan, and toothpick inserted near center comes out with a few moist crumbs but no batter."
- "Cool in pan 8 to 12 minutes. Remove cupcakes to wire rack and cool completely—warm tops make frosting melt into sticky disaster."
- "=== For The Frosting ==="
- "Beat butter and powdered sugar until pale and creamy. If it resists, add heavy whipping cream, a teaspoon at a time."
- "Test frosting’s hold by scooping and flipping spoon upside down; if it stays, you’re set. If not, powder sugar in small increments until stiffness achieved."
- "Fill pastry bag fitted with large star tip. Twist end, push frosting to tip—no air pockets, or spikes form oddly and ruin look."
- "Pipe frosting vertically, straight up and down for defined peaks. A quick flick at the top adds charm but avoid overhandling or frosting deflates."
- "=== Assembling ==="
- "Press two candy eyes immediately on top of frosting before it firms up fully."
- "Halve a chocolate chip cookie. Stick one half in frosting center like cookie monster chewing mid-bite."
- "Admire work. Be ready for sweetness—relentless, almost obscene."
- "Storage: cupcakes stay moist covered loosely at room temp up to 2 days or refrigerated for 4, but let come to room temp before serving."
- "Substitutions: whole milk instead of buttermilk works; cake texture less tangy but still fine. Butter can be swapped with margarine but reduces flavor depth."
- "Troubleshooting: Dry cupcakes? Check oven temperature accuracy. Over-beat frosting? Add more cream and beat again. Cookie breakage? Use softer cookies if problems persist."
