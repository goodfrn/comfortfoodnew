ingredients:
- "2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour"
- "2/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 tablespoon baking powder"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon ground cinnamon"
- "3 large ripe bananas, mashed"
- "1/3 cup olive oil"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/2 cup light brown sugar, packed"
- "1 cup semisweet chocolate chips, divided"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 355F. Line two standard muffin tins with 18 paper liners—skip grease; liners good here."
- "In medium bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon. Whisk to combine evenly. Set aside."
- "In small bowl, mash bananas with fork till mostly smooth but a few lumps don't hurt the texture."
- "Add olive oil, vanilla, eggs, light brown sugar to bananas. Stir just till blended. Don’t overwork; batter gets tough otherwise."
- "Add wet mixture to dry ingredients. Fold gently with sturdy spatula till just combined—still some dry streaks better than overmixed batter. Fold in ¾ cup chocolate chips carefully."
- "Divide batter evenly among liners. Fill ¾ full is key for good dome rise without overflow."
- "Scatter remaining chocolate chips on tops, press slightly to set them in place."
- "Bake 23-27 minutes. Watch for golden edges; tops spring back lightly when touched. Toothpick test essential—should come out clean or with few moist crumbs."
- "Let cool completely in pan before unwrapping muffins. Warmer muffins—too soft inside, fall apart."
- "Store leftovers airtight; they keep moist 2 days. Freeze for longer."
