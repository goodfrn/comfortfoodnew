- "24 small pretzels"
- "24 soft caramel squares or cubes, slightly larger than original"
- "7 ounces Candy Melts or chopped semisweet chocolate"
- "Assorted decorative toppings like colored sugar or sea salt flakes (optional)"
- "Substitution option: Use peanut butter chips for a nutty twist instead of chocolate"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 380°F. Line baking tray with parchment paper or silicone mat to avoid sticking and easy cleanup."
- "Place pretzels single layer spaced evenly. Put caramel piece on top of each pretzel, slightly offset so air can escape during bake."
- "Bake about 4 to 7 minutes. Look for caramel edges softening, glossy but not liquefied. Don’t aim for caramel puddle; it will keep settling as it cools."
- "Remove from oven. Let cool completely at room temperature. Caramel firms a bit but stays chewy — critical for chocolate dip adhesion."
- "Melt Candy Melts or chopped chocolate gently over double boiler or microwave in short bursts, stirring between. Avoid overheating—chocolate grainy if scorched."
- "Dip pretzels halfway in melted chocolate. Let excess drip off by lightly tapping side of bowl. Place back on parchment or silicone sheet immediately."
- "Add sprinkles, salt flakes, or chopped nuts while chocolate still soft. Set aside at room temp for 20 to 40 minutes or refrigerate 10 minutes to speed firming."
- "Store in airtight container at cool room temperature or fridge if your house is warm. Prevents chocolate bloom and keeps caramel chew fresh."
