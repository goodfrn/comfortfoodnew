ingredients:
- "500g beef tenderloin cut into thin strips against the grain"
- "3 tablespoons all purpose flour"
- "4 tablespoons avocado oil"
- "Salt and pepper"
- "2 large onions thinly sliced"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "3 cups shiitake mushrooms sliced"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "Half cup dry red wine"
- "1 cup beef broth"
- "Half teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 tablespoon whole grain mustard"
- "Three quarters cup Greek yogurt"
- "Fresh tarragon chopped"
- "Fresh chives chopped"
instructions:
- "Cut the beef thin. Against the grain. About a centimeter wide. Dust the strips with flour. Just enough to coat. Not clumps."
- "Skillet on high with the avocado oil. When it shimmers put the beef in. Small batches. Don't crowd or they steam instead of brown. Salt and pepper while they sear. When they've got color on both sides, into the slow cooker."
- "Same pan. Turn it down a bit. Butter in. Onions go soft and golden. Takes about five minutes. Then mushrooms and garlic. Another five minutes until the mushrooms lose their water and start to concentrate."
- "Red wine in the pan. Scrape the bottom. All those dark bits are the good stuff. Let it bubble for a minute then pour everything into the slow cooker."
- "Add the broth, smoked paprika, and mustard. One stir. Lid on."
- "Low for about 4 hours. High for 2 if you're short on time. The beef should be tender enough to cut with a fork when it's done."
- "Kill the heat. Stir the Greek yogurt in gentle. Don't let it boil or it curdles. Taste it. Adjust salt."
- "Tarragon and chives on top right before serving. Over egg noodles or rice. Mashed potatoes work too."

Skip that and the whole thing tastes flat. Five minutes in a hot pan makes the difference. That's the only real effort in this beef stroganoff crock pot recipe. Everything else just sits there and cooks."

tips:
- "Against the grain. Thin strips. Matters more than anything else for texture. Cut wrong and the beef is chewy no matter how long it cooks."
- "Hot pan. Small batches. Beef needs space to brown. Crowded pan means steam and grey meat. Two or three rounds. Takes a few extra minutes but the flavor is worth it."
- "Mushrooms need time to lose their water before they start to brown. Don't rush them. Five minutes on good heat. When they shrink and the edges go dark they're done."
- "Yogurt off the heat. Most important step. Boil it and you get curdled sauce with grainy bits. Stir it in gentle once the slow cooker is off. Taste after. Usually needs a pinch more salt at this point."
- "Egg noodles are the classic. They hold the sauce. Rice soaks it up differently. Mashed potatoes underneath with the stroganoff on top is my favorite honestly. This beef stroganoff crock pot dinner works with all three."
faq:
- "q: How long does it take? a: Four hours on low. Two on high. Low is better for the beef. Gets more tender. High works fine if you're in a rush but the texture changes a bit."
- "q: Can I use regular mushrooms? a: Yeah. Button or cremini both work for this slow cooker stroganoff. Less earthy than shiitake but still good. Use more of them since they shrink down a lot."
- "q: Sour cream instead of yogurt? a: Sure. Traditional. Greek yogurt is tangier and thicker which is why I switched. Either way add it off heat or it curdles."
- "q: What cut of beef works? a: Tenderloin is what I use. Chuck works too for a slow cooker steak stroganoff version. Tougher cut but the long cook breaks it down. Just go longer, maybe 5 to 6 hours on low."
- "q: Leftovers? a: Fridge three days in a sealed container. Sauce thickens overnight. Add a splash of broth reheating on the stove. Don't microwave the yogurt sauce too aggressively or it separates."
