ingredients:
- "24 mini paper liners"
- "1 cup chocolate chips (preferably semi-sweet; substitute milk or dark as desired)"
- "1 teaspoon coconut oil (can use butter, but coconut oil helps with sheen and texture)"
- "1/3 cup creamy peanut butter (almond butter swap for twist)"
- "1/4 cup powdered sugar (adjust slightly if you want less sweet)"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter (softened; salted butter okay but reduce salt added)"
- "1/4 teaspoon table salt (balance sweetness; fresh flakes work if careful)"
instructions:
- "Set mini paper liners snugly on a baking tray; helps keep shape and avoids mess."
- "Melt chocolate and coconut oil together. Use a microwave-safe bowl uncovered. Heat at 65-70% power first for 30 seconds—watch for soft bubbling edges. Stir thoroughly to blend. Repeat heating 15 seconds max, stir after each burst. Melt until totally liquid, no gritty crumbs. Avoid overcooking or chocolate seizes. If that happens, warm gently with a splash of coconut oil or butter to smooth out."
- "Mix peanut butter, powdered sugar, softened butter, and salt. Must be lump-free and homogenous. Texture here is crucial—too stiff will crack, too soft will spread all over. Use a sturdy spoon or your hands if needed. Taste test for balance; more salt or a touch more sugar can pull flavors up."
- "Spoon about 1 to 1 ¼ teaspoons melted chocolate per paper cup. Tilt the tray and rotate liners to coat sides about halfway up liner height. Acts like a shell; thicker means more bite but longer setting time. Listen for a faint crackle or small snap as it cools, that’s key texture forming."
- "Roll peanut butter mixture into small balls. I learned to squish them slightly between palms—flattening just enough to sit firmly on chocolate base without sinking. Gently nestle each ball atop chocolate in liners."
- "Top peanut butter with approximately 1 teaspoon melted chocolate. Spread edges if needed to seal peanut butter completely. Closes the cup, traps creaminess inside, and stops it from drying out."
- "Refrigerate for 15 to 25 minutes until fully firmed. Freezer works fine but monitor closely—over chilling can dull flavor and dry texture. Remove when you see a firm set, slight sheen return on chocolate surface. Snap off paper liners easily once chilled."
- "Store covered in fridge for up to a week. Let rest a few minutes before eating to soften slightly, especially if very cold."
