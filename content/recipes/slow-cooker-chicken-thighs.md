ingredients:
- "6 boneless skinless chicken thighs"
- "Salt and pepper"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 medium onion chopped"
- "2 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 teaspoon celery salt"
- "2 tablespoons tomato paste"
- "1 tablespoon all purpose flour"
- "Half cup chicken broth"
- "Half cup water"
- "2 bay leaves"
- "4 medium potatoes peeled and cut into chunks"
- "3 large carrots peeled and cut into chunks"
instructions:
- "Get your skillet hot with the olive oil. Medium heat. Wait for the shimmer. Season the chicken thighs on both sides, salt and pepper, don't be shy with it. Skin side down first. Let them sit. Don't touch them for 6 to 8 minutes. You want a deep golden crust. If they stick, they're not ready. When they release on their own, flip. Another 5 minutes on the other side. Pull them onto a plate."
- "Turn the heat down. Butter in. Let it foam. Onions go in, stir them around for 4 or 5 minutes until they soften up and start to brown a little. Then garlic, celery salt, tomato paste. Stir it all together so the onions get coated. Sprinkle the flour in. Keep stirring for about 45 seconds. You're making a quick roux. Don't let it burn."
- "Pour the broth and water in slow. Scrape the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. All those dark bits stuck to the pan are flavor. Get them all. Drop in the bay leaves. Let it simmer for a couple minutes until it thickens up. Should coat the back of a spoon. Glossy. No lumps."
- "Potatoes and carrots go into the slow cooker first. Spread them out even. Pour the sauce over the top. Don't stir. Lay the chicken thighs on top skin side up. Lid on."
- "High for 3 hours. Check it at 2 and a half. Fork the chicken. If it goes through easy and the juices run clear, it's done. Potatoes should be fork-tender but not falling apart. Carrots soft and sweet. Pull the bay leaves out before you serve."
- "Spoon the sauce from the bottom over everything when you plate it. That's the best part. Leftovers keep well. Sauce thickens in the fridge on its own. Splash of water when you reheat."

tips:
- "Dry the chicken. Season it well. Hot pan. Don't move it until the crust forms on its own. 6 to 8 minutes skin side down. If it sticks you're flipping too early. When it releases clean, it's ready. That sear is what makes this different from every other slow cooker chicken recipes out there."
- "Butter foams before the onions go in. Medium low heat. Stir occasionally. You want soft, slightly golden, not burned. Tomato paste and garlic last. Coat everything. Flour goes in fast and you stir the whole time. 45 seconds. Quick roux. Keeps the sauce thick and glossy."
- "Liquids slow. Scrape the bottom. Every brown bit is flavor. Simmer until it thickens enough to coat a spoon. If it's too thick, more broth. Too thin, let it reduce another minute. This sauce is what makes the whole meal."
- "Veggies on the bottom. Flat and even. Sauce over them. Chicken on top. Lid on. Don't open it for at least 2 hours. Every peek adds time. High for 3 hours or low for 5 to 6. Fork test the chicken. faq:
- "q: Can I use bone-in thighs? a: Yeah. Adds more flavor actually. Cook time might go up 30 minutes. Just check with a fork. Meat should pull away from the bone easy. For this slow cooker chicken thigh recipes method bone-in works fine, just harder to serve."
- "q: What if the chicken sticks when browning? a: It's not ready. Leave it. When the crust forms it releases on its own. Don't force it or you rip the surface and lose that sear. Use cast iron or a good heavy pan. Non-stick works but you don't get as deep a crust."
- "q: Can I skip the browning step? a: You can but don't. That's the difference between bland crockpot chicken and something that actually tastes good. Five extra minutes in a skillet changes the whole dish. Worth it every time."
- "q: Sauce too thin after cooking? a: Take the lid off and set to high for 15 minutes. Reduces quick. Or pull some liquid out, mix with a teaspoon of cornstarch, pour it back. Thickens in a minute. Usually the flour roux handles it though."
- "q: What sides go best? a: The potatoes and carrots are already in there so you've got a full meal. Crusty bread for dipping into the sauce. Simple green salad on the side. Rice works too if you want to stretch it. This is one of those chicken thigh recipes that doesn't need much else."
