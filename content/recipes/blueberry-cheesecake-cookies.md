ingredients:
- "2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "3/4 cup unsalted butter softened"
- "4 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "1 cup granulated sugar"
- "1/2 cup sour cream (can substitute Greek yogurt)"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "3/4 cup white chocolate chips (substitute chopped white chocolate)"
- "3/4 cup fresh blueberries (avoid frozen)"
instructions:
- "Set oven to 340-345 degrees Fahrenheit. Line two trays with parchment. Spray lightly or use silicone mats. This keeps bottoms from overbrowning or sticking."
- "Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt thoroughly. Air incorporates, stops dense dough. Set aside out of mixer reach to prevent contamination."
- "Cream softened butter, cream cheese, and granulated sugar in stand or hand mixer at medium-high speed for 1 to 1 1/4 minutes. Look for smooth, pale mixture, no gritty sugar bits. Avoid over-whipping once shiny peaks form or texture fades; it breaks fat emulsion."
- "Add sour cream, egg, and vanilla extract. Mix medium speed until just uniform. Egg aids binding, sour cream brings moisture and slight tang. Visual cue: batter should look homogeneous, thick but slightly loose."
- "Drop speed to low before adding dry mix. Stir flour mixture until just combined; stops gluten developing tough dough. Small clumps okay. Overmix — stiff, tough cookies. Use rubber spatula to finish if needed. Fold in chocolate chips next. Gently fold in blueberries last to keep them mostly whole, preserve shape and avoid bleeding color."
- "Use one tablespoon scoop or heaping tablespoon to portion dough. Leave 2 1/2 inches between mounds. They spread more than expected because of cream cheese moisture."
- "Bake on center rack. Look for edges shifting from pale cream to soft golden tan, tops matte, subtle cracks forming. Centers still soft but not jiggly. Check 13-16 minutes. If bottoms brown too fast, lower rack next batch."
- "Cool cookies on sheet 6 to 9 minutes before gently transferring to wire rack. Cooling on parchment sets edges firm but is forgiving to gently lift. Immediate transfer risks breaking fragile cookies still warm inside."
