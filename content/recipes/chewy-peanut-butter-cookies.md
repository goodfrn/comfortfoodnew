## Why You'll Love These Chewy Peanut Butter Cookies

Takes 32 minutes total. Twenty of that is just sitting around waiting for the oven.

Tastes exactly like the homemade ones from actual childhood, not some Instagram version of childhood. Soft in the middle. Still holds together.

One bowl for the dry stuff. One mixer bowl. That's cleanup.

They don't go hard overnight like some peanut butter cookies do — stays soft for days, maybe better on day two. Not sure why. It just does.

Works cold straight from the fridge if you have weird cravings at 6 AM. Which you will.

## What You Need for Soft Peanut Butter Cookies

Creamy peanut butter. Not natural, not powdered. The regular stuff you grew up with. Half a cup of it.

A cup of butter, softened — actually soft, not cold, or the mixer will hate you. Brown sugar half a cup, packed. Granulated sugar a third cup. More granulated for rolling the dough.

One egg plus one yolk. The yolk is important. Room temp. Seriously room temp — cold eggs don't mix clean.

Two tablespoons of milk or cream. Vanilla. One teaspoon.

Flour, baking soda, salt. All-purpose flour one and three-quarter cups. Half a teaspoon of baking soda — not a full teaspoon, that's the mistake people make. Half teaspoon of kosher salt.

Chocolate chips for dipping if you want. Sprinkles. Both optional but like, why wouldn't you.

## How to Make Chewy Peanut Butter Cookie Dough

Heat the oven to 345. Just under 350. Lower than you think. This is where the chewiness starts — slow heat means the center stays soft while the edges firm up.

Line two baking sheets with parchment. Doesn't have to be perfect.

Whisk the flour, baking soda, and salt together in a bowl. Just mix it so there's no clumps hiding in the corners. Takes two minutes.

Grab a stand mixer. Add the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar to the bowl. Use the paddle attachment. Medium-high speed. Run it for three to four minutes. You're looking for something that's creamy and light and fluffy — not dense like it started. Scrape down the sides of the bowl a few times or pockets of unmixed sugar will stay stuck.

Crack the egg and separate the yolk into the mixer. Pour in the milk. Add vanilla. Mix until it's just combined. Don't keep going after it looks mixed — overmixing makes these tough instead of chewy.

Drop the speed down. Add the dry ingredients slowly while the mixer turns. The dough will feel kind of airy and loose, not sticky. If it feels too slack and won't hold a shape, stick it in the fridge for ten minutes. The flour absorbs moisture and it gets easier to scoop.

## How to Get These Peanut Butter Cookies Soft and Chewy

Scoop the dough into two-tablespoon balls. Yeah, two tablespoons — use an actual scoop or just eyeball it. You'll get about fourteen to sixteen cookies.

Roll each one in granulated sugar. Coat it. The sugar does two things — it creates a subtle crunch when you bite it, and it makes them shine. Visually it looks intentional.

Space them out on the sheet. Leave an inch between them. They spread a tiny bit but not drastically.

Bake for eight to twelve minutes. This is the critical part. Watch the edges. You want them light golden. The centers should still look puffed and soft — like they're not done yet. That's the point. Slight underbaking is what locks in the chewy texture. They firm up while they cool on the sheet. If you bake until the center looks cooked, you've gone too far.

Pull them out. Let them sit on the hot pan for five minutes. They'll flatten a little bit. This is fine. After five minutes, move them to a wire rack.

Here's a trick nobody mentions. Take a cup and swirl it over the warm cookies while they're still on the pan. Rounds out the edges. Makes them look less blob-like.

## Chewy Peanut Butter Cookie Tips and Common Mistakes

Room temperature matters. Cold eggs and cold butter won't blend right. They'll leave little chunks. Just let them sit on the counter for an hour before you start.

The baking soda amount. Half a teaspoon. I see people dump in a full teaspoon because they think more lift means more tenderness. It doesn't. It makes them cakey.

Don't overbake. Seriously. The center should look like it needs two more minutes. It doesn't. This is the hardest part because your brain says "it's not done" but your brain is wrong.

If you dip them in chocolate, do it while they're completely cool. Warm cookies melt the chocolate weird. Wait an hour after they come off the rack.

Sprinkles go on immediately after the chocolate while it's still wet. If you wait, they slide off.

Storage is airtight. Cookie jar. Ziploc bag with the air squeezed out. They last five days minimum without going hard. Probably longer but I've never had them around long enough to test that.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Chewy Peanut Butter Cookie Recipe

**Can I make the dough ahead of time?**
Yep. Roll it in sugar, stick it on a sheet in the freezer for an hour or two, then bake straight from frozen. Add a minute or two to the bake time. Works great actually.

**What if my dough is too sticky to scoop?**
Ten minutes in the fridge. That's all. The flour absorbs the moisture and it's suddenly manageable.

**Should I use natural peanut butter?**
Don't bother. The kind with oil on top doesn't give you the same structure. Creamy peanut butter is the move here.

**Why is the oven temperature 345 instead of 350?**
It's not a huge deal if you go to 350. But 345 gives you those extra couple minutes for the edges to set while the center stays soft. Slow and low.

**Can I add chocolate chips to the dough?**
Half cup of chips mixed in. Sure. Won't change much texture-wise but why not.

**How long do these actually stay chewy?**
Days. I'm not exaggerating. Four or five easy. They don't go hard and stale like some cookies do.
