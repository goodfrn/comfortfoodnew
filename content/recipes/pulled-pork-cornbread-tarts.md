instructions:
- "Start with butter so soft it nearly melts but hasn’t turned liquid. Dump it into a bowl with the cornbread mix. Stir fast, a dense dough must form. If dry or crumbly, add drops of water — don’t drown it, just in small increments to help hold together."
- "Roll tablespoon-sized portions quickly in hands into balls. Press gently down on parchment lined baking sheet. One pan won’t cut it—prepare two to three sheets. Cornmeal absorbs moisture fast; dough firms up, so work fast before becoming powdery."
- "Pop into 350 degree F oven (no convect). Bake 10 to 13 minutes. Watch the edges, tiny brown undersides start to crisp. That toasty scent is your sign. They should be firm-ish but not rock-hard. Cool in pan 10 minutes; they’ll solidify suitably for handling."
- "Transfer to rack. Cooling fully necessary. Then dollop pulled pork over each tart. Drizzle with favorite barbecue sauce — tangy, smoky, or spicy. Your palate chooses the punch."
- "If you don’t have butter, swap with equal avocado oil and a bit more water. Pulled pork can be swapped with shredded chicken or mushroom for twist. Texture matters; too dry dough breaks; too wet gets mushy. Patience with tactile feel, not strict timers."
