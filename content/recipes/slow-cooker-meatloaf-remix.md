ingredients:
- "1 cup heavy cream"
- "1 1/2 cups plain breadcrumbs"
- "2 large eggs"
- "1/2 cup diced yellow onion"
- "2 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 teaspoon seasoned salt"
- "1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper"
- "2 lbs ground beef and pork blend"
- "1/4 cup ketchup"
- "3 tablespoons packed light brown sugar"
- "1 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "2 tablespoons sweet pickle relish"
instructions:
- "Take a long strip of aluminum foil; fold over twice for strength. Fit it in the slow cooker pot so sides hang over edges—makes a sturdy sling later. Coat foil with generous spray of oil or nonstick spray."
- "In a big bowl, dump cream, breadcrumbs, eggs, onion, garlic, Worcestershire, salt, pepper. Stir until you get a sticky paste; paste means right moisture, no dry crumbs."
- "Tear ground meat into chunks over bowl; toss with hands gently. Don’t overmix—overworked equals tough loaf. Form into a fat oval matching pot shape; set gently onto foil sling. The meat should be squishy not dense."
- "Whisk ketchup, brown sugar, Dijon, and sweet pickle relish. Relish stands in for balsamic vinegar here—gives sweet tart punch, texture lift. Spoon thick glaze evenly over top."
- "Cover slow cooker; cook on low for 6.5 hours. During cooking, aroma deepens; edges firm and pull inward; top glaze bubbles slightly but not burnt. If smell turns sharp or meat feels dry—overcooked."
- "Use foil sling edges to lift meatloaf carefully from cooker. Let rest 10 minutes uncovered on cutting board—resting traps juices, meat firms up. Slice with serrated knife."
- "Serve hot or room temp; leftovers fantastic in sandwiches or fried slices next day."

- "q: What if meatloaf is crumbly? a: Moisture off, maybe breadcrumbs too dry or mix too rough. Paste stage skipped? Add cream or beaten egg next time. Folding hands gentle, no kneading; prevents overworked meat. Could be old breadcrumbs too."
- "q: Can I use other ground meats? a: Beef-pork blend balanced fat, flavor. Turkey or chicken lean, dries out faster; add more cream or use fattier parts. Season well, watch cooking time shorter. Lamb changes flavor profile; best adjust glaze sweeter or sharper to match."
- "q: What if glaze gets burnt? a: Too thin or thick coating causes uneven caramelization. Puddled glaze bubbles then crisps fast. Spread thick but thin enough for slow melting. Reduce brown sugar or cook on very low heat if glaze burns. Cover slow cooker tight but not airtight to reduce hot spots."
