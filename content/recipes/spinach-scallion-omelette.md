ingredients:
- "2 large eggs"
- "2 tbsp heavy cream (or whole milk)"
- "1 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "50g cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined (substitute smoked salmon or cooked tofu)"
- "1/4 cup fresh spinach, roughly chopped"
- "2 scallions, finely sliced"
- "5 cherry tomatoes, quartered"
- "1/4 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (replace with feta for tang)"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Warm butter in medium skillet over medium heat. Watch carefully. Wait for butter foam to subside but not brown. Butter sizzling gently, not burning—best sign."
- "Crack eggs into bowl. Add cream or milk. Whisk vigorously or blitz in blender briefly to trap air. Eggs pale, thickened, slight froth on top."
- "Pour eggs evenly into hot pan. Legs of bubbles will form at edges; do not disturb immediately. Let eggs surface set slightly till edges firm but center still soft, roughly 50-55 seconds."
- "Sprinkle shrimp, scallions, spinach, tomato quarters evenly over half the eggs. Add half the mozzarella right on top. Salt and pepper sprinkled gently."
- "Surface may bubble up sporadically. Use toothpick or fork tip to puncture bubbles to prevent uneven pockets. Listen for faint sizzling, bubbling slows."
- "Slide thin spatula under entire omelette, loosen edges. Add small pat more butter if pan seems dry or omelette sticking."
- "Flip omelette carefully in one swift movement; if intimidating, flip in two parts. Cook second side 1 to 1.5 minutes. Surface should turn pale golden without browning. Cheese melting within is key."
- "Remove from heat. On plate, fold omelette in half or thirds using spatula. Top with remaining mozzarella, let sit 30 seconds to melt from residual heat. Serve immediately."
