ingredients:
- "=== Pumpkin Cheesecake Layer ==="
- "16 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "3 tablespoons all-purpose flour"
- "1/3 cup granulated sugar"
- "1 cup pumpkin puree or substitute 1 cup evaporated milk for creaminess change"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 large egg"
- "=== Brownie Layer ==="
- "3/4 cup unsalted butter melted"
- "1/4 cup granulated sugar"
- "3/4 cup light brown sugar packed"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "3 large eggs"
- "1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder sifted"
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking powder"
instructions:
- "=== Prepare Pumpkin Cheesecake ==="
- "Preheat oven to 345 F to prevent burning sugar edges. Line 9x13 with parchment, let edges hang for easy lift."
- "Beat cream cheese with paddle attachment till no lumps remain, pale and kinda fluffy – key for creamy texture."
- "Add flour and sugar, mix slow just combined; overmix and you get dense, no air left."
- "Stir in pumpkin puree or evaporated milk, vanilla, egg. Watch batter thicken and pull away in peaks when lifted."
- "Set aside, resist overbeating cheesecake mix; it’ll bake denser if you do."
- "=== Brownie Batter ==="
- "In large bowl or stand mixer, combine melted butter with both sugars. Cream 2-3 minutes til sugar dissolves and mix feels velvety."
- "Add vanilla and eggs, one at a time, beating until just combined but not fluffy."
- "Whisk dry ingredients cocoa, flour, salt, baking powder separately to avoid clumps."
- "Gently fold dry into wet, mix till no visible flour but don’t over stir or brownies toughen."
- "=== Build and Bake ==="
- "Pour about 3/4 brownie batter into pan, spread evenly but not pressing down; preserves air pockets."
- "Spoon cheesecake layer evenly, scrape sides to level without mixing layers."
- "Drop remaining brownie batter by spoonfuls on top. Use knife or toothpick to swirl gently, creating marbled effect. Be quick or batter sets."
- "Bake 38-40 minutes. Look for edges pulling from parchment and cake tester with few moist crumbs, no wet batter."
- "Big jiggle center means underdone, slight spring back is good. Surface cracks okay – can signal caramelized edges."
- "Cool completely on rack at least 2 hours or overnight for cheesecake layer to firm, easier slicing and cleaner edges."
- "Lift from pan using parchment overhang; cut with sharp knife wiped between cuts to reduce stickiness and crumbling."
