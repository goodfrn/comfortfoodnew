ingredients:
- "1 stick (4 ounces) unsalted butter softened to room temperature"
- "1 teaspoon finely grated lemon zest (avoid bitter white pith)"
- "1 1/4 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about half a lemon)"
- "3/4 teaspoon Dijon mustard (sub: grainy mustard for texture contrast)"
- "2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (can swap with cilantro or tarragon for different herbaceous notes)"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt (adjust to taste)"
instructions:
- "Softened butter in bowl. Use whisk attachment on handheld or stand mixer. Whip vigorously 60 to 90 seconds until visibly aerated — fluffy but not melting. Scrape down sides frequently so no lumps cling to bowl walls."
- "Add lemon zest, lemon juice, mustard, chopped parsley, salt. Pulse or mix at low speed just until fully incorporated. Color shifts lighter, aroma sharp and fresh. Taste cautiously — mustard strong, tweak accordingly."
- "Chill in fridge for minimum 10 minutes but can rest up to 72 hours sealed. Flavors marry even better over time but don’t wait if pressed. Butter soft enough to spread but firmer than room temp consistency."
- "If herb or mustard substitution used, adapt chilling time as some herbs may darken or lose vibrancy with extended rest. Always eyeball texture—looks creamy, holds shape on softened bread without melting away instantly."
- "Use immediately or store airtight in fridge up to 3 days. Freeze for longer storage, thaw gently to preserve whipped texture."
