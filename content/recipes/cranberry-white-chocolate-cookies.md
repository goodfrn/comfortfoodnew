ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 cup cake flour"
- "1 tablespoon cornstarch"
- "1/2 teaspoon baking soda"
- "3/4 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1 stick cold unsalted butter, cut into cubes"
- "2/5 cup white granulated sugar"
- "1/3 cup packed maple sugar"
- "1 large egg"
- "1 large egg yolk"
- "1 teaspoon vanilla extract"
- "1 cup dried cranberries"
- "1 cup white chocolate chips"
- "Splash whole milk (optional)"
- "Additional 4 ounces white chocolate melting wafers or chips for dipping"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 395°F. Watch the hot air ripple inside the oven when it reaches temp."
- "Whisk all dry ingredients together in a large bowl: all-purpose flour, cake flour, cornstarch, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Make sure no lumps linger; cornstarch helps keep the cookies tender."
- "In a stand mixer with paddle attachment, cream cold butter cubes with sugars until pale, fluffy, and just slightly aerated, around 2-4 minutes. Cold butter slows spreading and maintains cookie thickness."
- "Add whole egg, yolk, vanilla. Let mixer blend until just combined. Too vigorous here toughens dough."
- "Slowly add dry mix in 2 parts, mixing low speed. Don’t overmix; dough should feel dense, slightly dry. If it looks crumbly, add a splash of milk to bring it together."
- "Fold in white chocolate chips and dried cranberries by hand. You want pockets of melted chip and chewy fruit in every bite."
- "Divide dough into 6 large portions (~5 oz each) for chunky cookies. Press lightly into rounds. No parchment or greasing needed on baking sheet; the butter enough."
- "Bake about 11 minutes. Edges golden, centers soft, just set on surface but still pliant to touch. Oven temps vary; eyeball edges browning, listen for faint crackle as sign of readiness."
- "Cool cookies on sheet at least 15 minutes. They firm up yet stay tender inside. Too soon and they crumble when moved."
- "Melt remaining white chocolate in microwave in 25-second bursts. Stir in-between until glossy and smooth. Dip each cookie halfway, shake off excess."
- "Set dipped cookies on parchment until chocolate solidifies. Avoid fridge—it dulls gloss and causes bloom."
- "Store in airtight container once chocolate sets. Keeps freshness for 3 days, but best eaten first day for texture contrast."
