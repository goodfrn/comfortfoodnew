ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups whole milk ricotta cheese, drained"
- "2 large eggs"
- "3/4 cup all-purpose flour"
- "1/4 cup almond flour"
- "1 teaspoon baking powder"
- "1 tablespoon fresh sage, finely chopped"
- "1/2 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "1 teaspoon lemon zest, finely grated"
- "1/3 cup finely grated aged gruyere cheese"
- "Vegetable oil for frying"
instructions:
- "Start whipping ricotta and eggs in a large bowl or mixer until perfectly smooth. No lumps—helps batter bind and airy. Takes about 1-2 minutes, watch texture."
- "In another bowl, toss flours, baking powder, chopped sage, salt together. Sage adds woody scent, don’t skip, but finely chopped for subtle bursts."
- "Fold dry mix gradually into wet ricotta combo. Don’t overmix—stop when just combined. Overworking toughens fritters. Then fold lemon zest and grated gruyere in last. Zest bright, gruyere sharp, melts inside golden crusts after frying."
- "Cover bowl, slap in fridge minimum 25 to 30 mins. Chill lets flour hydrate, batter firms up slightly, easier to shape. Tried skipping chill before—batter too wet, fritters fall apart in pan."
- "Pour about 1-inch vegetable oil in large skillet, medium heat. Test: sprinkle tiny batter; it should instantly sizzle and float up, not burn fast. Too cold → greasy soggy fritters. Too hot → dark outsides, raw middles."
- "Scoop tablespoon or small cookie scoopfuls batter gently into oil. Don’t overcrowd pan; space for flips, crisping."
- "Fry each side 1½ to 2 minutes until golden brown with crunchy crust. Watch edges curl slightly and bubble sound slow down—signs it’s ready to flip."
- "Flip carefully with metal spatula or slotted spoon, avoid breaking. Second side less time, 1 minute usually."
- "Drain on wire rack over baking sheet to keep crisp, not soggy. Keep warm in oven set to 200°F if needed, but best fresh out of pan."
- "Dust with flaky sea salt just before serving. Cuts richness, lifts flavors. Serve with tart yogurt or garlic aioli for contrast."
- "If gruyere not on hand, aged asiago or manchego also work. Almond flour substitution adds texture and cuts gluten if needed."
- "Trouble with greasy fritters? Oil temp too low or overcrowding pan usually culprit. Patience and space are everything here."
