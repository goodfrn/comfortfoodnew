ingredients:
- "12 ounces egg noodles, cooked al dente and drained"
- "1 cup frozen peas, thawed"
- "2 cans (5 ounces each) tuna in water or oil, drained"
- "1 can (10.5 ounces) cream of mushroom soup, condensed"
- "1 cup milk (whole or 2%)"
- "1¼ cups mild cheddar cheese, shredded, divided"
- "½ teaspoon smoked paprika (substitute for celery salt)"
- "Freshly cracked black pepper to taste"
- "1 teaspoon dried parsley flakes or 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped"
- "¾ cup fresh white bread crumbs or panko"
- "3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 355°F (a bit higher than usual). Lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish with oil or non-stick spray, cover top edges with foil folded loosely for easy removal later."
- "In a large mixing bowl, combine noodles still warm since it helps meld flavors better; stir in peas, tuna, the mushroom soup, milk, 1 cup shredded cheddar, smoked paprika, pepper, and parsley. Taste mixture — should be creamy but not soupy; adjust with a splash more milk or a pinch of salt if needed. Avoid over-mixing to keep noodles intact."
- "Spoon mixture evenly into the prepared casserole dish. Press down gently to compact slightly, but don’t mash. Cover tightly with foil to trap moisture and encourage bubbling."
- "Place casserole on lower middle rack. Bake covered about 22 minutes until you see edges bubbling and smell that mushroom-cheese aroma warming the air. Noodles absorb sauce and swell a bit—peek after 20 minutes just to check without disturbing too much."
- "While it bakes, toss bread crumbs with melted butter until completely coated. This ensures even browning and crisp topping. If no fresh bread crumb, pulse stale bread slices in food processor; toasted panko also works but less buttery, so add a bit more butter."
- "Remove casserole from oven carefully to avoid steam burns. Take off foil – steam billows out, hot and fragrant. Sprinkle a thin even layer of buttery crumbs, then scatter remaining ¼ cup cheddar cheese over."
- "No cover now. Return casserole to oven rack, but move to upper third for better top heat exposure. Bake uncovered 7 to 12 minutes, watching closely for breadcrumbs turning deep golden brown and a few cheese spots bubbling and melting lazily. Visual = your best timer, not clock."
- "Once top looks toasted and cheese melted, pull from oven. Let sit at least 5 minutes before serving—this rest thickens sauce and cools it just enough."
- "Serve warmth with a crunch on top and creamy, soft noodles below. You’ll notice the smoky paprika subtle lift against the traditional flavors. If craving brightness, squeeze fresh lemon juice or scatter chopped chives on individual plates."
