ingredients:
- "3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil"
- "1 medium yellow onion, chopped"
- "4 cloves garlic, minced"
- "1 1/2 cups tomato puree"
- "28 ounces crushed fire-roasted tomatoes"
- "1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes"
- "1 tablespoon Italian seasoning mix"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons sugar"
- "2 tablespoons tomato paste"
- "1/4 teaspoon baking soda"
- "2 pounds mixed seafood (shrimp, scallops, calamari, clams, mussels)"
- "Salt to taste"
- "Freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish"
- "Grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese, for topping"
- "Twist: substitute half the tomato puree with roasted red pepper sauce for smoky flavor"
instructions:
- "Heat olive oil in a heavy large pot over medium-high heat until shimmering. Toss in chopped onion. Stir often, watching it soften to translucent and just starting to brown on edges—takes about 7 minutes. That smell, sweet and savory, sets the base."
- "Add minced garlic, stir for about 45 seconds until fragrant but not browned. Old mistake: burning garlic here turns bitter, so keep stirring."
- "Pour in tomato puree and fire-roasted crushed tomatoes. A smoky undertone layers here if using roasted tomatoes. Stir in tomato paste; it thickens the sauce and adds umami punch. Add crushed red pepper flakes, Italian seasoning blend, and sugar to balance acidity."
- "Lower heat to medium-low. Let the sauce bubble softly—not a rolling boil but consistent gentle bubbles breaking surface every few seconds. Simmer uncovered for about 22 minutes. Watch sauce thicken and deep red hue develop; stir occasionally but not constantly—heats uneven if neglected."
- "Baking soda time. Sprinkle evenly over sauce. It fizzes and foams, neutralizing harsh acidity. Stir until fizzing stops, a sign the acid tempered. This trick avoids sour tomato taste that can ruin prolonged cooking."
- "Add cooked seafood into sauce immediately. Cold or undercooked seafood in hot sauce cooks quickly but can toughen. Pre-cooked keeps tenderness intact; remember, fresh seafood cooks fast."
- "Simmer together for another 5 minutes just enough for flavors to mingle. Sauce thickened, seafood warmed through, blend of spice and subtle sweetness achieved. Overcooking seafood here means rubbery calamari or mealy scallops—been there."
- "Serve over your choice pasta. Long linguine or bucatini soak sauce best. Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley and a dusting of Pecorino or Parmesan shave for saltiness and color pop."
- "If using, add twist ingredient (roasted red pepper sauce) before simmering for flavor complexity."
- "If sauce seems too thick or starts sticking, splash warm water or seafood stock. Sauce should cling but not glue pasta."
- "Common issues: burnt garlic (start over rather than risk bitterness), bland spice (add flakes gradually next time), tough seafood (pre-cook briefly or add last minute)."
- "Keep tasting—seasoning can hide in tomato layers. Adjust salt and pepper at end, not too early."
- "Leftover sauce reinvigorates next day pasta or as marinara for bruschetta."
