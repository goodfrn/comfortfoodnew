instructions:
- "Set broken peppermint candies inside a ziplock between towels. Crush lightly with rolling pin. Don't powder it. Large shards make that rustic look I love, and give a nice crunch."
- "Spread a big sheet of waxed paper on a cookie tray or flat counter space. Keep it steady—keeps the bark from sticking and aids cleanup."
- "Microwave dark chocolate in 25-30 second blasts. Stir each round. Stop heating just shy of fully melted, bits of it still visible but soft. Then vigorous stirring melts the rest. Burning? Chocolate smells sharp, grainy. Bail out early next time."
- "Quickly spread the dark chocolate on waxed paper. Thin, about 10 by 22 inches. If it starts firming too fast, scrape thinner or warm hands slightly to smooth before it sets rock hard."
- "When mostly solid but still tacky (fingertip touch, slight indent), melt white chocolate same 30-second intervals. Stir often. Goes grainy if rushed. You want creamy, never scorched."
- "Drop peppermint oil in white melts, start with half teaspoon. Stir well, taste carefully. Potent stuff. Add another tiny drop if faint. Too much overwhelms the candy's softness."
- "Pour white chocolate spread over dark layer thinly. Work fast here—white melts can melt dark beneath, ruining the textured layers. If it blurs, refrigerate briefly to firm dark layer before white step next try."
- "Sprinkle crushed peppermint chunks generously over white chocolate, avoiding dusty powder at bottom of bag. That powder gets bitter and unappealing when mixed in. Bigger pieces lock in freshness, add interest in every bite."
- "Let rest undisturbed at cool room temp or fridge until fully firm and solid. Break roughly into shards. Store airtight or moisture ruins crisp texture."
