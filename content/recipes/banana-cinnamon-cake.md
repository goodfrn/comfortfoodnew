ingredients:
- "1 cup unsalted butter softened substitute coconut oil for dairy-free"
- "1 1/3 cups granulated sugar reduce by 2 tbsp to cut sweetness"
- "2 large eggs at room temperature"
- "3/4 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt for tang"
- "2 tsp vanilla extract real, not imitation"
- "2 2/3 cups all-purpose flour sifted for even rising"
- "1 tsp baking soda fresh, check expiration"
- "1/2 tsp fine sea salt"
- "3 ripe medium bananas mashed not pureed"
- "1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon fresh grind preferred"
- "1/4 cup almond milk or dairy milk for frosting"
- "8 oz cream cheese softened for frosting"
- "2 cups powdered sugar sifted to avoid lumps"
- "1/2 cup chopped walnuts toast lightly for crunch"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven to 345–355°F. Spray 9×13 glass dish; slick surface matters to prevent sticking."
- "In large bowl, cream softened butter plus sugar hand mixer or stand paddle. Don’t rush or you’ll fudge texture, fluffy means incorporate air—listen to change in sound; quiet and creamy."
- "Add eggs one by one beating well after each. Watch batter; it thickens and lightens color."
- "Mix vanilla and sour cream (or yogurt). Offbeat acidity balances cake grain."
- "In separate bowl, sift flour, baking soda, and salt. Sifting avoids pockets of uneven rise. Mix dry ingredients briefly then slowly add to wet bowl. Do not over mix or develop gluten; batter should be thick but foldable."
- "Mash bananas with cinnamon just enough to combine. Larger chunks give better bite. Fold into batter gently, pockets of banana show up when baked, best part."
- "Pour batter into prepared dish. Level surface with spatula. If batter too thick, ease with tablespoon almond milk but err on dry side for structure."
- "Bake 28–40 mins depending on altitude and oven quirks. Edges start to brown and pull from sides; center springs back lightly. Toothpick should come out with moist crumbs, not wet batter."
- "While cake cools, beat cream cheese until air whipped and shiny. Add almond milk and vanilla; mix smooth then stream in powdered sugar. Whip till fluffy but not thin runny."
- "Spread frosting evenly once cake cool. If too soft, chill frosting briefly. Top with toasted walnuts. Skip- crush pistachios or leave plain."
- "Let cake sit room temp 10 mins post frost. Warmer cake melts frosting, holding shape matters."

Add dry mix slowly, fold gently—no vigorous stirring or gluten develops. Thick but flexible batter is goal; overmix means tough cake. Mix bananas mashed but still chunky, keeps moist bites.
