## What You Need for Clams Casino

Half a pound of bacon, chopped small—that's your anchor. Three tablespoons of that bacon grease reserved; don't dump it. Red bell pepper, half a cup, chopped fine. Not green. Red brings sweetness. Onion too, half a cup, same treatment.

Two cloves of garlic minced. A teaspoon of Italian seasoning. One cup of cracker crumbs—Ritz, saltines, panko, whatever. Three-quarters cup Parmigiano Reggiano grated. A can of chopped clams, drained, but keep two tablespoons of the juice. Twenty-four clam shells or fresh littlenecks steamed and shucked.

Lemon wedges. Fresh parsley chopped. Cooking spray if you're doing the casserole.

That's it. Nothing weird.

## How to Make Clams Casino with Canned Clams

Oven goes to 350. Line a sheet pan with foil if you hate scrubbing.

Chop the bacon into small bits. Skillet on medium heat, let it render until crispy. That takes maybe five minutes. Scoop it out, but keep that grease in the pan. Turn heat down low.

Toss the red pepper into the bacon fat. Two or three minutes and it goes soft and sweet. Smell matters here—you'll know. Add the onion next, another two minutes, just until it's clear. Not brown. Then the garlic and Italian seasoning. One minute of stirring and the kitchen smells like actual food. Off heat now.

Bowl time. Dump the drained clams in. Add the cracker crumbs, the cheese, the bacon pieces, the pepper-onion situation from the pan. Fold it gently. Like you're handling something that breaks. Just combine it.

Arrange your clam shells on the tray, open side up. Spoon the mixture into each one evenly. Two raw bacon strips crisscrossed on top of each. This matters—raw bacon cooks down and gets crispy and salty on top.

Into the oven 15 minutes uncovered. Watch the bacon edges start to bubble. The fat glistens and starts to firm up. That's the signal.

Switch to broil for two to five minutes. Just watch it. Bacon pops, edges brown, you don't want charred. Pull it when the bacon is deep golden and blistered.

Garnish vigorously with parsley. Lemon wedges next to each. The acid cuts the richness.

## Getting Clams Casino Actually Crispy

The bacon on top is your crispiness. It can't be crowded or it steams instead of crisps. Space matters.

Bacon grease in the filling keeps it from getting dry. Most people underseasoned this filling—don't. The bacon adds salt, the cheese adds salt, so you're covered. Taste it before you spoon.

Broiler is fast. Two minutes of inattention and you burn it. Three minutes might be all you need. One minute might be too much. Watch. Don't walk away.

If you're doing fresh clams instead of canned, steam them first. Raw littlenecks won't open in the oven and you'll be fighting with them. Thirty seconds at 450 pops them just enough to shuck. Open carefully. Pry the clam loose but keep it sitting in the bottom shell. Same filling on top.

Clams overcooked turn rubbery fast. When the edges of the flesh start to curl slightly—done. They should still feel slightly plump, not tough.

## Casserole Version and Clams Casino Tips

No shells? Preheat 350. Grease a one-quart dish lightly. Cook the bacon same way, keep three tablespoons of fat.

Pepper in the fat for two or three minutes. Onion next, two minutes. Garlic and seasoning, one minute, off heat.

Bowl: clams drained, reserved clam juice, extra cracker crumbs to make one full cup, cheese, peppers, bacon, everything folded together gently.

Spoon it into the casserole. Level but don't smash it. Bake uncovered 20 minutes. Edges bubble and golden, you're done. Parsley on top, lemon wedges on the side.

Fresh clams from the grocery store need steaming first or you'll spend ten minutes wrestling with them. Dried herbs work in a pinch but fresh parsley makes a difference at the end. Green. Bright. Cuts the richness.

Crushed saltines work instead of cracker crumbs. Panko adds crunch but dries things out faster; if you use it, add a tablespoon more clam juice to the filling. Red pepper beats yellow beats green. Green tastes bitter. Don't do green.

Broiler burns bacon fast. Pull early if you're nervous. Overcooked clams are worse than undercooked bacon.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Clams Casino

**Can you make clams casino ahead of time?**
Assemble it the morning of, cover it, stick it in the fridge. Bake it same way. Maybe add two minutes because cold ingredients take longer. The bacon won't be quite as crispy pre-assembled—bake it fresh if possible.

**What if you don't have clam shells?**
Use the casserole version. Bake 20 minutes at 350 in a small dish. Same filling, same result, less fussy. Works better for feeding people anyway.

**Should you use fresh clams or canned?**
Canned is faster and easier. Fresh clams taste better but need steaming first and more effort. Both work. Canned clams casino at a casual dinner? Nobody cares.

**Why does the filling need clam juice?**
Moisture. Cracker crumbs soak it up and get gluey without it. Two tablespoons is enough. Keeps the filling tender instead of dense.

**Can you substitute the cheese?**
Parmigiano does something specific—salty, nutty, hard. Grana Padano works identically. Domestic Parm works too. Skip it and the filling tastes flat. Don't skip it.

**What's the difference between canned and fresh clams casino?**
Fresh clams stay in their shell the whole time. Canned clams get chopped and mixed into the filling. Fresh looks fancier. Canned tastes the same and takes less work.
