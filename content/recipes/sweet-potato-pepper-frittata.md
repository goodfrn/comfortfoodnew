## Why You'll Love This Vegetable Frittata

Makes a whole meal in one skillet. Breakfast, lunch, leftover situation—works every way. Gluten free without trying. Just eggs, veggies, cheese. No flour needed.

Sharp cheddar melts into the eggs and kind of disappears. Makes everything taste better without tasting like cheese. Technically breakfast food but eats fine room temperature the next day, maybe better that way. Avocado on top stays soft and creamy if you add it after, not before. The sweet potatoes get this particular kind of sweet-savory thing going on that regular potatoes don't pull off. Not a breakfast casserole, not a quiche—something in between. Feeds four to six people depending on what else you're serving. One pan. One oven. Done.

## What You Need for a Vegetable Frittata

Sweet potatoes. Two medium ones, peeled and cubed. Not yams. The cubes should be roughly the size of dice, maybe slightly bigger. Doesn't matter exact.

Yellow bell pepper. One medium. Seeded and diced small. Red works. Green tastes sharper. Stick with yellow if you want sweetness to match the potatoes.

Olive oil. A tablespoon. For tossing the veggies before they roast.

Eggs. Ten large ones. Crack them into a bowl. Don't get shell in there.

Sharp cheddar cheese. Three-quarters cup, shredded. Not mild. Not extra sharp where it gets bitter. Regular sharp cheddar. The kind from the block at the grocery store works better than pre-shredded—doesn't have the anti-caking stuff.

Fresh parsley. A third of a cup chopped, plus extra for the top. Cilantro works if you hate parsley. Same amount.

Ripe avocado. One. Diced when you're ready to serve, not before. Sitting in the frittata makes it turn that weird gray-green color.

Butter. For greasing the pan. Olive oil doesn't hold the way butter does. Use real butter.

Salt. Black pepper. The kind you grind fresh. Pre-ground goes dusty.

## How to Make a Vegetable Frittata

Preheat the oven to 205C. That's 400F if you need it. Middle rack. Butter the skillet—the 26 centimeter nonstick one. Get it all over the bottom and sides. You'll regret skipping this.

Toss the sweet potato cubes and diced bell pepper with a tablespoon of olive oil. Salt them. Pepper them. Spread on a lined baking tray in a single layer. Not piled up. They need to roast, not steam. Into the oven for 35 minutes. Halfway through—at about 17, 18 minutes—pull it out and turn the pieces over. Some edges should get brown and caramelized. Not charred. Just the color of dark honey where they've been touching the hot tray.

While the veggies roast, crack your ten eggs into a large bowl. Whisk them. Don't go crazy with the whisking—just until the yolk and white are one color. Add the three-quarters cup of shredded cheddar. Add the parsley. Salt. Fresh ground pepper. Mix it again, just enough to combine. Stop before it gets too beaten. Overbeaten eggs toughen up when they cook. You want them fluffy, not dense.

The vegetables should be soft when you poke them, not mushy. Take them out. Let them cool for a minute so they don't crack the eggs.

## How to Get a Vegetable Frittata Actually Golden

Pour the egg mixture into the buttered skillet. It should sizzle a little when it hits the pan. That's how you know the butter is hot enough. Scatter the roasted vegetables evenly over the top. Don't pile them in the center. Spread them out.

Slide the whole skillet into the oven. Twenty-two to twenty-five minutes. You're watching for three things: the top puffs up, it gets golden, and when you shake the pan, the center jiggles just slightly. That wobble means the inside is still soft. Give it another minute if it's too liquidy, but stop before it's completely firm—carryover heat keeps cooking it on the plate.

The edges might brown too fast. If they're going dark and the center's still wet, cover it loosely with foil for the last 5 minutes. Don't seal it tight. Just drape it. And don't open the oven door more than once. Every time you open it, you let heat out and the top can fall flat.

## Vegetable Frittata Tips and Common Mistakes

Watch the visual cues, not the clock. Golden color. That slight jiggle. Eyes matter more than minutes.

Make sure the skillet is ovenproof. Cast iron. Stainless steel. Nonstick that says oven-safe on it. If it's not ovenproof—handle or whatever—transfer the egg mixture to a baking dish after the veggies come out of the oven.

The avocado goes on after. Not in the frittata. On top when you serve it. Rough chop it so it doesn't turn into mush.

If your roasted vegetables release water during cooking, drain that off before you add them to the eggs. Extra moisture makes the frittata soggy. Pat the pieces dry with a paper towel if there's a lot of liquid pooling on the tray.

The frittata slides out better if you loosen the edges with a knife first. Run it around the perimeter, underneath if you can. Then slide it onto a plate.

Substitutions work fine. Zucchini instead of bell pepper. Mushrooms instead of either. Gruyère instead of cheddar if you want something sharper. Pecorino too. The parsley can be cilantro. Just keep the ratio roughly the same—you want flavor, not overwhelm.

Room temperature tastes good. Cold is fine. Reheats okay in a low oven covered with foil.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Vegetable Frittata

**Can I make this vegetable egg and vegetable casserole ahead of time?**
Yeah. Make it the night before. Cover it. Cold or room temp next morning. Slice it and eat it. Reheat in a 160C oven for 10 minutes if you want it warm.

**What if my sweet potato frittata is too wet in the middle?**
Means it needs a couple more minutes. Close the door. Wait. Check again in 90 seconds. The jiggle should be tiny, not a wave.

**Can I add more vegetables to this baked eggs and vegetables recipe?**
More vegetables means less egg, and the structure changes. You can add maybe another half cup of roasted veggies if you whisk an extra egg or two into the mixture. But at that point it's more casserole than frittata. Works either way. Just drier.

**Should I use sweet potato or regular potato for a potato and eggs frittata?**
Sweet potatoes. Regular potatoes take longer to soften. Same cutting size, 35 minutes isn't enough time. If you only have russets, cube them smaller.

**Why did my baked egg and veggie casserole come out rubbery?**
Overcooked. The center should jiggle. If you bake it until it's completely firm, it'll be tough. That wobble when you shake it is the sweet spot. It keeps cooking after you pull it out.

**What goes with this vegetable frittata recipe?**
Crusty bread. A green salad with something sharp in the dressing. That's it. Don't overcomplicate it.
