ingredients:
- "8 oz cream cheese softened"
- "2 cups shredded sharp white cheddar cheese"
- "¾ tsp fine sea salt"
- "⅛ tsp ground white pepper"
- "⅛ tsp cayenne pepper"
- "1 tsp Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 cup chopped walnuts"
- "2 tbsp minced fresh parsley"
- "Pretzel sticks optional for bite size balls"
instructions:
- "Beat softened cream cheese in mixer bowl with paddle until light and fluffing, about 3 minutes. Look for air bubbles and pale color as signs."
- "Add shredded cheddar, salt, white pepper, cayenne, Worcestershire sauce. Blend until evenly mixed but don't over beat. You want texture, not paste."
- "Cover bowl tightly; refrigerate at least 2 hours. Chilling firms the mix, flavors meld better and handling easier."
- "Remove chilled cheese; form into one large ball or split into roughly 30 small bites. Smaller ones need firmer chill to hold shape well."
- "Mix chopped walnuts and parsley in shallow dish. Roll cheese balls until coated evenly, press lightly. For finger food, insert pretzel sticks into each bite to serve like lollipops."
- "Keep refrigerated until serving. Cheese balls taste best cold but not too hard—letting sit 5 minutes at room temp before eating loosens texture."
