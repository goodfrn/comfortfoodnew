ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 medium yellow onion diced"
- "2 cans (14 ounces each) diced tomatoes with juices"
- "4 cups low sodium chicken broth"
- "1 packet taco seasoning mix"
- "1 can (4 ounces) diced green chilies drained"
- "8 ounces cream cheese softened"
- "4 cups shredded cooked chicken (rotisserie recommended)"
instructions:
- "Heat heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven on medium. Pour olive oil in. Toss in diced onions once oil glistens but isn’t smoking. Stir frequently. Onion edges turn soft and translucent, about 4-6 minutes — listen for gentle sizzle, not crackle or burn."
- "Dump in tomatoes with juices, chicken broth, taco seasoning packet, and diced green chilies. Stir just enough to combine. Increase heat to medium-high. Wait till mixture bubbles steadily but doesn’t boil aggressively. That bubbling signals flavors are marrying."
- "Drop softened cream cheese chunks into the pot. Reduce heat immediately to medium-low to avoid curdling. Stir slowly, repeatedly — watch the cream cheese meld into velvety cream. No lumps allowed here; patience pays off."
- "Mix in shredded chicken. It’s precooked so just warm through. Stir every few minutes while soup thickens slightly. About 6-7 minutes is enough. Check temperature and pull off heat once steaming and hot to touch, avoiding overcooking meat or toughening cheese."
- "Serve hot. Garnish ideas? Cilantro, sliced avocado, shredded cheese, crushed tortilla chips if you want crunch. Leftovers chill well but reheat gently, stirring often. If too thick next day, splash broth or water."

- "q: How to avoid curdled cream cheese? a: Lower heat to medium-low as soon add cheese. Stir slowly nonstop. Don’t rush melting. If cheese cold, soften first. Patience keeps texture velvety, lumps pop up quick if heat too high."

- "q: Why does soup get watery sometimes? a: Usually from low simmer temp or adding too much broth. Also if lid left on too long during simmer, traps steam. Simmer uncovered part of time helps thicken naturally."
- "q: Best way to store leftovers? a: Fridge in airtight container up to 3 days. Reheat on stove, stir often, add splash broth if thick. Freeze okay but texture changes slightly. Thaw in fridge overnight for best reheating."
