ingredients:
- "4 large bell peppers halved and seeded"
- "3 cups shredded cooked chicken breast (swap for turkey if needed)"
- "6 ounces softened cream cheese (use Greek yogurt for lighter)"
- "½ cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese plus ½ cup reserved"
- "¾ cup shredded mozzarella cheese"
- "¾ cup wing sauce (Buffalo-style, adjust for heat preference)"
- "1 tablespoon ranch seasoning mix (homemade or bottled)"
- "½ cup water"
- "2 green onions thinly sliced"
- "¼ cup blue cheese crumbles (substitute feta or omit if no blue cheese)"
instructions:
- "Get the oven set to 345 degrees F. Spray a 9x13 casserole pan lightly with non-stick spray. Arrange bell pepper halves cut side up snug but not overcrowded. Set aside."
- "Combine shredded chicken with softened cream cheese, ½ cup cheddar, mozzarella, wing sauce and ranch mix in a bowl. Use a rubber spatula to fold and mash until creamy but thick. Adjust cream cheese softness if mixture feels dry or crumbly."
- "Spoon this filling evenly into each pepper half, pressing filling to edges so no gaps remain. Don’t overfill or peppers might burst open while baking."
- "Pour ½ cup water into the baking dish bottom. This steams and softens the peppers instead of drying them out. Cover dish tightly with foil–seal well or steam escapes."
- "Bake for about 35 minutes or until peppers soften and filling is bubbling gently. You’ll hear a light sizzle and see steam escaping from edges when ready to move on."
- "Remove foil. Sprinkle reserved cheddar evenly on top of each pepper. Return to oven uncovered for 12 minutes or until cheese is melted fully and slightly golden spots appear."
- "Let rest 7 to 10 minutes once out. This sets the filling and cools peppers enough to handle. Scatter sliced green onions and blue cheese crumbles (if using) just before serving."
- "If you lack cream cheese, blend cottage cheese until smooth or substitute with ricotta. Wing sauce can be swapped with BBQ for a smoky twist but expect different flavor profiles. Ranch seasoning can be homemade from dried herbs if unavailable."
- "For kitchen shortcuts: prepping chicken day before saves time. Use a meat thermometer to ensure internal temp hits at least 165 F for safety. If filling seems too wet, add more shredded cheese or a teaspoon of flour to bind better."
- "Watch the cheese melt as a doneness cue rather than strict timer. It should bubble and brown lightly, not burn or shrink. Steaming water in pan should fully evaporate or reduce close to finish time to avoid soggy bottoms."
