ingredients:
- "1.5 lbs ground beef, browned and drained"
- "1 medium yellow onion, diced"
- "1 tbsp unsalted butter"
- "1 15oz can black beans, drained and rinsed"
- "1 14oz can diced tomatoes with green chilies"
- "1 6oz can tomato paste"
- "1 cup beef broth"
- "2 tsp chipotle chili powder"
- "1 tsp ground cumin"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika"
- "1/2 tsp garlic powder"
- "1/2 tsp onion powder"
- "1/4 tsp cayenne pepper, optional for heat"
- "Salt and pepper to taste"
instructions:
- "Brown ground beef over medium-high heat in wide skillet. Break meat into small bits. No pink visible. Drain off fat completely to avoid greasy chili ; too much residue dulls flavors and weighs down texture."
- "In same skillet, add butter and diced onion. Lower heat to medium. Cook, stirring often, until onions are soft and translucent ; about 5-7 minutes. Should smell sweet, no browning; caramelized edges change profile too much here."
- "Transfer cooked meat and softened onions into slow cooker."
- "Add black beans, diced tomatoes with chilies, tomato paste, broth, spices including chipotle chili powder, cumin, paprika, garlic and onion powders, cayenne if using. Salt and pepper last."
- "Stir everything to combine thoroughly; full blend avoids spice pockets or tomato clumps."
- "Set slow cooker on high for about 3 hours 45 minutes. Don’t obsess with time. Look for bubbling along edges, rich chili aroma filling kitchen, thickened base with visible slight oil separation but not greasy pooling."
- "Taste near end of cooking. Adjust salt or heat if needed. If too thin, leave uncovered last 15 minutes to reduce liquids."
- "Serve piled high with sour cream, shredded cheddar cheese, and chopped scallions. Creamy and sharp topnotes balance smoky, spicy chili."
- "Leftovers improve next day. Freeze well. Reheat slowly, stirring often, add splash broth if thickened too much."
