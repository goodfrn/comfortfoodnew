ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups Biscoff cookies or ginger snaps, crushed"
- "6 tbsp unsalted butter, melted"
- "4 large egg yolks"
- "3 tbsp lime zest"
- "1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk"
- "1/3 cup freshly squeezed lime juice"
- "Pinch kosher salt"
- "1 1/4 cups heavy cream"
- "1 1/2 tbsp powdered sugar"
- "1 tsp vanilla extract"
- "2 tsp sour cream"
instructions:
- "Heat oven to 355 F. Line 8x8 pan with parchment loosely hanging over edges for easy removal."
- "Crush cookies fine but not powdery. Best in food processor. No processor? Bag cookies and crush with rolling pin or skillet with force."
- "Drizzle melted butter over crumbs. Pulse or mix until mix looks sandy but sticks slightly when pressed."
- "Press firmly into pan’s bottom evenly. I use a flat-bottomed glass. Press hard enough so crumbs hold but don’t over compact or it gets rock hard."
- "Bake crust for 11 minutes. Tiny scent of caramel fills air. Watch edges just a bit darker. Remove, let cool while prepping filling."
- "Zest first, in long curls for garnish later. Juice limes freshly - bottled juice dulls zing and throws off balance."
- "Whisk yolks and zest on high with mixer whisk attachment until mixture thickens like light hollandaise, about 3-4 mins. Don’t rush or it won’t whip up."
- "Add condensed milk gradually. Whip in at high speed until filling gains body and volume, roughly 2-3 minutes. Texture is creamy, slightly glossy."
- "Mixer off. Stir in lime juice and a pinch salt with spatula. Fold gently but thoroughly. Scrape down sides - zest bits cling to whisk; don’t waste those."
- "Pour filling onto crust. Smooth surface with offset spatula or back of spoon. Should spread easily; if too loose, a quick extra mix needed."
- "Bake 12-17 minutes. Center should jiggle slightly, not fully set. Edges firm, center creamy. Avoid overbake; cracks mean dryness."
- "Cool bars to room temp before chilling. Cold helps filling set without cracking, develop clean lime flavor."
- "Chill minimum 3 hours or overnight if patient. Bars get firmer, easier to cut into neat squares."
- "Whip heavy cream at medium-high speed until soft peaks form, 1-3 minutes depending on mixer strength."
- "Add powdered sugar, vanilla, sour cream. Beat until medium peaks, smooth but holds shape. Sour cream adds tangy depth, stops whipped cream from overly sweet cloying."
- "Adjust sugar to taste; powdered sugar quantity flexible based on cream sweetness preference."
- "Spread whipped cream over chilled bars evenly. Return to fridge 30 minutes to firm topping. Zest sprinkle on top for aroma, zing, pop of color."
- "Remove bars with parchment edges, slice with sharp knife warmed in hot water for cleaner edges. Wipe blade between cuts."
- "Serve cold. Bars hold for 3 days refrigerated, but loses firmness if left out long."

faq:
- "q: Can I swap Biscoff for other cookies? a: Yes. Ginger snaps add barkier crunch, more spice. Graham crackers milder, less caramel notes. Adjust butter slightly if dry cookies. Crushing method stays same; processor or manual fine."
- "q: How do I know when custard filling is done? a: Jiggle test. Center should wiggle slightly, not fully firm. Edges start to set. Overbake and cracks form, dryness follows. Oven temp varies, so watch edges color and feel for subtle jiggle."
- "q: What if my whipped cream breaks or gets grainy? a: Usually whipped too fast or overwhipped. Add sour cream mid-whip to smooth, add tang and help stabilize. Powdered sugar type matters - coarse sugar won’t dissolve well. Chill bowl and beaters help keep cream cold, less risk of breaking."
- "q: How long to store and how? a: Fridge best, bars last 3 days max. Wrap tight to avoid drying or absorbing fridge odors. Can freeze but texture changes, best thawed gently overnight chilled. Avoid room temp storage to maintain firm topping and crisp crust."
