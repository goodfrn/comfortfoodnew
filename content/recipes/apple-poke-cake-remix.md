ingredients:
- "1 box yellow cake mix"
- "1 cup water, room temp"
- "⁄3 cup vegetable oil or melted butter"
- "3 large eggs"
- "2 cups chopped tart apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), tossed in 1 tbsp lemon juice"
- "2 tbsp granulated sugar"
- "1 package French Vanilla instant pudding mix"
- "2 cups cold milk"
- "1 tsp apple pie spice or ground cinnamon"
- "1 cup crushed vanilla wafer cookies (Nilla or similar)"
- "½ cup chopped walnuts or pecans"
- "Caramel sauce for drizzling (store-bought or homemade)"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 350F (remove racks if close to top to avoid burning edges). Spray a 9x13 pan well with cooking spray or grease with butter for extra flavor and sticking prevention."
- "In a large bowl, whisk cake mix, water, oil (or butter), and eggs just till combined—don’t overbeat will toughen crumb. Batter thick but pourable. It’s okay if a few lumps remain."
- "Mix apples with lemon juice plus sugar in a bowl. Shake excess sugar off by tossing through sieve or coarse colander—too much sugar sinks apples, making wet pockets."
- "Gently fold apple chunks into batter. Apples will bleed flavor but not soggy if folded carefully. Pour batter evenly into prepared pan, smooth top with spatula but don’t press down too hard."
- "Bake for 22-28 mins. Watch for golden edges. Cake surface springs back lightly. Insert toothpick into center; pull out with a few moist crumbs but no raw batter. Color shifts from pale yellow to deeper gold."
- "Right out of oven while still hot, poke holes deep and wide about ¾ inch apart with handle of wooden spoon or thick straw. Make plenty; more holes = better pudding soaking."
- "Cool cake on wire rack for about 15 mins. It should still be warm but not hot—hot pudding hits makes holes expand and leak."
- "Meanwhile, whisk pudding mix into milk briskly. Starts liquidy, then thickens after 2-3 minutes whisking. When it resists whisk and thickens further, stop. Let sit 2 minutes more to set."
- "Stir in apple pie spice or ground cinnamon into pudding. Adds warmth and ties flavors together better than plain vanilla."
- "Pour pudding gently over warm cake, focusing on holes. Pat pudding lightly into holes with a spoon tip or spatula. Use toothpick or skewer to prick through pudding to open blocked holes if you see puddles on top."
- "Tap pan lightly against countertop a few times to settle pudding deep inside."
- "Sprinkle crushed vanilla wafers and toasted nuts evenly on top. Walnuts add crunch, and wafers absorb some excess pudding to keep top from becoming mushy."
- "Cover tightly with aluminum foil or plastic. Chill minimum 1 hour, longer if possible. Chilling firms pudding inside holes, making slicing cleaner without collapse or pudding oozing out."
- "Serve cold or at room temp drizzled liberally with caramel sauce. Caramel contrast is key. Can use salted caramel for added zip."
- "If pressed for time, fudge chilling down to 40 minutes but avoid less. Cake chills while pudding sets—don’t skip."
- "Storing leftovers tightly covered in fridge for 3-4 days max. Taste improves after sitting overnight as flavors meld."
