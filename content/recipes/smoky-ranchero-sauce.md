ingredients:
- "1 ½ tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "½ cup diced yellow onion"
- "1 small chipotle pepper canned in adobo sauce, chopped (substitute 1 dried chipotle rehydrated or 1 tsp smoked paprika for milder heat)"
- "1 garlic clove minced"
- "1 ⅓ cups plain tomato sauce"
- "⅓ cup low-sodium chicken broth (can use vegetable broth or water in pinch)"
- "½ teaspoon fine kosher salt"
- "½ teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon dried oregano"
- "½ teaspoon chili powder"
- "2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (substitute lemon juice if needed)"
instructions:
- "Heat oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Watch closely so it shimmers but doesn't smoke; oil ready when thin ripples form on surface."
- "Add onions and cook, stirring often, until translucent and sweet-smelling, about 4 minutes. Listen for faint sizzle, smell the sweet onion aroma starting. Lower heat if browning."
- "Stir in chipotle pepper and garlic. Cook gently until garlic softens and you catch that roasted pepper scent—about 2 minutes. Avoid burning garlic; if it darkens too fast, reduce heat."
- "Pour in tomato sauce and broth, then add salt, smoked paprika, oregano, chili powder. Mix well to combine. Sauce should thin out slightly; bubbles should just break on surface, signaling low simmer."
- "Add lime juice last, off heat, to preserve brightness—don't cook it down. Blend using immersion blender in the pot or transfer carefully to a stand blender. Pulse until mostly smooth; some texture left for rustic feel."
- "Cool slightly, then set aside to let flavors meld. Sauce thickens mildly while resting. Refrigerate if holding over two hours, rewarm gently with splash of broth to loosen."

faq:
- "q: Can I use dried chipotle instead of canned? a: Yes, rehydrate first. Soak in hot water then chop. Smoked paprika is backup if none. Flavor less intense, adjust heat. Chipotle canned in adobo brings richer depth. Start small if swapping."
- "q: What if sauce too thick? a: Add broth or water carefully off heat. Don’t cook longer to thin or lose flavor. Stir gently, watch bubbles, simmer low. Thick sauce risks dull flavor when overheated. Adjust liquid; best texture is thin sauce holding shape, not runny."
- "q: How long to simmer sauce? a: Until you see gentle bubbles breaking on edges, not full boil. About 10-15 minutes, depends on pan and heat. Watch aroma shift—smoky pepper scent, softened onions. Stir occasionally. No timer rule, use senses. Too fast heat scorches spices and garlic, ruins taste."
- "q: Storage options? a: Fridge up to three days covered. Can freeze in airtight container several weeks. Defrost slow in fridge, rewarm with broth splash.
