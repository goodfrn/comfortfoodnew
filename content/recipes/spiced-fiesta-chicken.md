ingredients:
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1.25 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs cut into chunks"
- "3 cloves garlic minced"
- "1 medium onion diced"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
- "1 teaspoon onion powder"
- "1 teaspoon kosher salt"
- "freshly ground black pepper to taste"
- "1 red bell pepper diced"
- "1 yellow bell pepper diced"
- "1 cup cooked black beans (canned drained and rinsed works)"
- "1 cup corn kernels frozen or fresh"
- "14 ounces diced fire-roasted tomatoes"
- "3/4 cup long grain white rice rinsed"
- "1.75 cups chicken broth (or veggie broth substitute works)"
- "1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese (or sharp cheddar for a twist)"
instructions:
- "Heat olive oil in a wide nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. When oil shimmers, toss in chicken chunks. Let them sizzle undisturbed for about 4 minutes or until the exterior browns deeply. Constant stirring equals steaming, not searing."
- "Add minced garlic and diced onion. Sprinkle smoked paprika, onion powder, salt, and freshly cracked pepper over everything. Lower heat to medium. Stir occasionally for 3-4 minutes until onions go translucent and spices fill the air. Watch for the garlic not to burn; it turns bitter fast."
- "Stir in diced bell peppers, drained black beans, corn, diced fire-roasted tomatoes, rinsed rice, and broth. Give it a brief stir; you want everything evenly distributed but don’t overdo or rice breaks down."
- "Bring to a bubbling boil uncovered, high heat. Once bubbling nonstop, immediately cover with a tight lid and lower heat to lowest simmer setting. Let it sit gently for 17-22 minutes. No peeking. Leave that lid alone; escaping steam ruins rice texture. If you hear furious bubbling, turn heat even lower. Texture of rice should be tender but not mushy. Chicken internal temp hits 165 Fahrenheit; use thermometer here if in doubt."
- "Remove from heat. Taste for seasoning balance— might need salt or extra pepper. The moment chicken juices run clear and rice is fluffy, you’re there. If rice still chewy, add a splash more broth, cover tightly, and let sit off heat for 5 minutes."
- "Sprinkle Mexican blend cheese over entire skillet, cover again and let it rest. Cheese melts from residual heat, no extra flame needed. If impatient, a quick 1-2 minute broil at low rack in oven does the trick but watch closely or cheese scorches."
- "Serve warm with a barrage of garnish options: chopped cilantro, scallions, jalapeño slices, dollops of Greek yogurt or sour cream, bright pico de gallo, slices of avocado, lime wedges for acid pop, or crunchy tortilla chips. Each bite textured and tangy, bright with fresh versus cooked contrast."

faq:
- "q: How to avoid dry chicken? a: Use thighs over breasts, tend to shrink less. Browning key first, high heat then simmer low. Watch internal temp, 165 Fahrenheit target. If drying, add slight broth or cover to retain moisture. Slow cooking prevents toughness too."
- "q: Can I substitute ingredients? a: Yes, cheddar or pepper jack for cheese. Veg broth swaps chicken broth but reduce added salt. Bell peppers green or orange if red/yellow missing. Beans canned preferred rinsed, no gritty. Garlic amounts adjustable, burn risk if too much or high heat early."
- "q: What if rice too wet or dry? a: Add broth bit by bit if undercooked after simmer. Don’t stir while cooking or steam escapes. Maintain tight lid to trap moisture. Too dry? Lower heat, add splash broth, cover till fluffy. Texture clues more reliable than time alone."
- "q: How to store leftovers best? a: Cool quickly, fridge within 2 hours. Store airtight, add splash broth to keep moist. Microwave low power or stove gentle warming avoids drying out. Freezing works but cheese melts less gooey on thaw. Fresh garnish always better reheated separately."
