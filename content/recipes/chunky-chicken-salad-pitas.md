ingredients:
- "1 cup cooked chicken diced or shredded"
- "1/3 cup celery chopped fine"
- "1/3 cup shallots minced (substitute sweet onion, rinse if strong)"
- "1/2 cup red grapes halved"
- "1/3 cup toasted pecans roughly chopped (instead of walnuts)"
- "1/3 cup mayonnaise"
- "1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice"
- "1 teaspoon whole grain mustard (swap Dijon)"
- "1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon white pepper"
- "4 pita bread halves"
- "4 leaves crisp lettuce"
- "4 tomato slices"
instructions:
- "Chop chicken, celery, shallots. Rough chop pecans, toast in dry skillet until fragrant, about 2-3 minutes, watching so they don't burn. Toss grapes next to prep area."
- "Mix mayo, lemon juice, whole grain mustard, salt and white pepper vigorously in big bowl. Use whisk or fork to blend so dressing is tangy but not runny."
- "Fold chicken, celery, shallots, pecans, grapes into mayo mixture gently but completely. Taste. Adjust salt or lemon juice. Texture should be chunky with juicy bursts from grapes, nuts adding crunch."
- "Cut pitas in half. Tear lettuce, stuff one leaf and one tomato slice in each pocket. The crispy veggies keep the bread from sogging too fast."
- "Spoon chicken salad into pitas, packing but not overstuffing. Serve immediately or cover chicken salad separately and refrigerate max 24 hours. Toast pitas lightly if they feel too soft before stuffing."
- "If salad thickens after chilling, stir in small spoon of lemon juice or a splash of milk to loosen. If pitas get soggy, dry them in skillet or toaster quick before filling."
- "Listen for toasted nuts’ pop and crackle to avoid bitterness. Smell the lemon hitting mayo to know it’s bright enough but not sour. Fingertip test on salad - should cling to spoon but still move easily. Lettuce should be firm, no wilt or water spots."
