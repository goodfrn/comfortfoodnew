ingredients:
- "12 ounces fettuccine"
- "1 1/4 tablespoons Cajun seasoning divided"
- "1 1/4 pounds boneless skinless chicken breasts cut into strips"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil divided"
- "1 large green bell pepper sliced"
- "1 medium yellow onion sliced"
- "3 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 3/4 cups Alfredo sauce"
- "salt and black pepper to taste"
- "1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (optional)"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika (replaces some Cajun seasoning)"
instructions:
- "Get water boiling for pasta, salt liberally. Toss in fettuccine, watch for al dente edges curling but firm core. Drain firmly. Set aside but don’t let it dry out—moisture helps mix later."
- "Mix 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning with smoked paprika in a bowl. Toss chicken strips thoroughly so each piece grabs that spice coat. Don’t skimp or you'll lose punch."
- "Heat 1 tablespoon oil in skillet until shimmering but not smoking. Chicken goes in, sizzle it stirred occasionally; edges should brown and juices run clear, no pink. Remove chicken from skillet."
- "Add remaining oil if the pan looks dry. Throw in sliced bell pepper and onion. Stir often, they should soften but still hold snap—look for slight translucent edges and gentle give, about 7 minutes. Too mushy kills texture."
- "Toss in garlic last to prevent burning. Stir one minute until aroma hits your nose—big hint—don’t walk away."
- "Put chicken back. Pour in Alfredo sauce. Sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon more Cajun seasoning. Lower heat to simmer gently—bubbles should form slowly, not boil like a storm. Stir constantly to prevent scorching."
- "Season now with salt and black pepper carefully; Alfredo masks spiciness so taste before over-salting. Add pasta in, fold gently but thoroughly. Pasta will soak sauce slightly, steps matter here."
- "Serve straight from pan, garnish with parsley if you want the green hit. No waiting; sauce clings best while warm and silky."
- "If sauce too thick, splash a little pasta water. Too thin, simmer a bit longer to thicken—watch closely to avoid separation."
- "Use chicken thighs if you prefer juicier meat, but watch fat splatter. White meat’s leaner, quicker to dry if overcooked."
- "If Cajun is too hot for you, reduce to 1 teaspoon initially, add more at finish. Adjust to your heat tolerance—not all palettes survive the burn."
