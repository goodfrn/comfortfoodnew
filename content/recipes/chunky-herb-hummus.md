ingredients:
- "1 1/4 cups cooked chickpeas drained, some cooking liquid reserved"
- "3 tablespoons sunflower seed butter (instead of tahini)"
- "2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil"
- "3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice"
- "3 tablespoons cold water plus more as needed"
- "2 cloves garlic minced roughly"
- "1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika plus extra for garnish"
- "Salt to taste"
instructions:
- "==="
- "Place chickpeas in a food processor bowl. Pulse briefly to start crushing them. You want a rough mash with some grit left — don’t overpuree unless you want paste, not hummus texture."
- "Add sunflower seed butter, olive oil, lemon juice, cold water, garlic, smoked paprika, and salt. Blend until the mixture homogenizes but still has some body. Scrape down sides occasionally there may be clumps holding back flavor."
- "Taste. Need more water or olive oil? Add teaspoon by teaspoon. Texture should be thickish but spreadable, creamy pockets blending with whole chickpea bits."
- "Transfer to bowl, cover with plastic wrap. Chill minimum 45 minutes; an hour better if you can, to help flavors mingle but keep sauce bright."
- "Before serving, stir briskly. Smells sharper garlic with earthy paprika blow. Drizzle with extra olive oil, dust smoked paprika on top. Serve with pita wedges, crunchy veg sticks, or smear on crusty bread."
- "=== "
- "Note grainy roughness means under-processed or dry chickpeas. Add cooking liquid or cold water in small doses between blending to loosen. Avoid blending too long or hummus becomes gluey."
- "Sunflower seed butter offers nutty depth but less bitter than tahini; good swap if ot allergic or tahini is pricey."
- "If fresh garlic too harsh, roast beforehand or use garlic powder sparingly in the mix."
- "Smoked paprika adds smoky warmth unexpectedly savory. Feel free to vary heat with cayenne or substitute lemon for lime juice for tang shift."
- "Leftovers store fine for 3 days in airtight container in fridge. Stir again before serving as water separates."
