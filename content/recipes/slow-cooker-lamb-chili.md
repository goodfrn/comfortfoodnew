ingredients:
- "2 lbs ground lamb, grass-fed if possible"
- "4 large garlic cloves, minced"
- "2 medium yellow onions, chopped"
- "2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil"
- "2 cans (14.5 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes"
- "1 can (15 oz) dark red kidney beans, rinsed"
- "1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed"
- "1 cup beef broth, low sodium"
- "2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, diced plus 1 tbsp adobo sauce"
- "1 tbsp ground cumin"
- "1 tbsp smoked paprika"
- "2 tsp dried oregano"
- "1 tsp ancho chili powder"
- "Salt and black pepper, to taste"
- "Cheddar cheese, shredded, for topping"
- "Fresh jalapeños, sliced, for topping"
instructions:
- "Heat olive oil over medium-high in large skillet. Toss in chopped onions first. Stir, sweat till translucent and edges browning—sweet, nutty onion aroma fills air."
- "Add minced garlic after onions soften. Stir quick, garlic browns fast, 30 seconds max. Avoid bitter burned flavor."
- "Ground lamb next. Break apart chunks, let meat brown without crowding pan—no steaming here. Listen for sizzle, see edges caramelize. Pink patches vanish, fat renders. Drain excess grease if heavy but keep some fat for flavor punch."
- "Transfer browned lamb, onions, garlic mixture directly into slow cooker basin. No heating the crock pot yet (trust me)."
- "Pour cans of fire-roasted tomatoes, both beans, beef broth over meat mix. Add chipotle peppers with adobo sauce. The smoky heat replaces typical jalapeño in the pot, sharper depth. If no chipotle, sub 2 fresh serrano peppers chopped fine."
- "Sprinkle cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, ancho chili powder over everything. Season lightly with salt and fresh ground black pepper—you can adjust later."
- "Using a wooden spoon, mix well to combine all chunks with liquids. No stubborn clumps hiding."
- "Set crock pot on low. More patience here. Plan for 4 to 5 hours. Skip starting it for shorter when pressed, but minor flavor sacrifice. Stir at least once midway to break any settled bits, help mingling."
- "Listen for soft bubbling sound after first hour—sign the magic starts. The smell richens like campfire smoke and braised meat mingled."
- "Texture check after 4 hours: meat should shatter easily under fork, beans intact but tender. Sauce thickened slightly, not watery."
- "If still too thin, take lid off near end, switch heat to high for last 20 minutes. Keep checking so it doesn’t dry out."
- "Ready servings with shredded sharp cheddar cheese on top. Fresh sliced jalapeños raw or quick pickled add crunch and bite. Sour cream optional but overused elsewhere, skip here to let bold flavors sing."
- "Leftovers almost better next day when spices fuse solidly—makes great freezer stash."
- "Common snag: lamb too lean? Add splash olive oil during browning to prevent dryness. Meat too greasy? Drain early and rinse beans well to reduce sodium."
- "No slow cooker? Finish in oven-safe pot covered at 275°F for 3 hours, stir every 45 mins."
