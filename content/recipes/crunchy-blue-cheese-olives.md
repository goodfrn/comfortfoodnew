ingredients:
- "1 cup blue cheese, crumbled and stuffed inside green olives"
- "½ cup all-purpose flour"
- "1 tsp smoked paprika (split ½ tsp in flour mix and ½ tsp in sour cream sauce)"
- "½ tsp fine sea salt"
- "1 large egg, lightly beaten"
- "1 cup plain breadcrumbs (preferably fine or panko for crunch)"
- "Vegetable oil, for frying (about 3 cups or enough to fill pan 1-2 inches high)"
- "½ cup sour cream"
instructions:
- "=== Preparing Olives ==="
- "Drain green olives well. No soggy bits. Dab dry with paper towels—if wet, breading won’t stick and oil will spit like crazy."
- "=== Setting Up Coatings ==="
- "In a small bowl, toss together flour, ½ tsp smoked paprika, and fine sea salt. Keeps that smoky note subtle but present."
- "In a second bowl, lightly beat the egg till yolk and whites blend a bit—no whisking frenzy needed."
- "In a third bowl, have the plain breadcrumbs ready. Fine crumbs stick better but crush some panko if you want a chunkier crunch."
- "=== Breading Sequence ==="
- "Roll olives first in the flour mixture. Dust off excess, but don’t skip this step. Flour helps egg adhere better."
- "Next, dip olives fully in beaten egg. Let excess drip off—too much egg pools and ruins crust."
- "Then coat olives evenly in breadcrumbs. Press gently but no need to squish stuffing out (unless you see cheese bulging)."
- "=== Freezing Preparation ==="
- "Lay breaded olives in a freezer-friendly container. No touching—olive clumps trap moisture and mess up crispness."
- "Place parchment or wax paper between layers. Seal tightly and freeze for at least 70 minutes; I usually go 90 min to be sure."
- "=== Frying ==="
- "Fill a medium saucepan with oil about 1-2 inches deep. Heat on medium-high to around 350F (but I rely on smell and tiny bubbles forming around wooden spoon tip)."
- "Work in small batches—crowding kills crunch and drops oil temp. Lower olives in gently with a slotted spoon or sturdy spatula."
- "Hear that sizzle? That’s the moment. If it sputters too aggressively, oil is too hot—dial down. Too quiet means cold oil—olive gets greasy, no thanks."
- "Turn olives carefully after about 2 minutes. Brown all sides. Total frying around 3-4 minutes; cheese inside should melt quietly, but crust firm and golden."
- "Drain fried olives on paper towels to soak excess oil. For serving later, keep warm in oven at 200F on a rack so oil doesn’t sog."
- "=== Serving Sauce ==="
- "Mix sour cream with remaining ½ tsp smoked paprika. Stir well. Sharp smokiness cuts cheese richness."
- "Serve olives hot with the smoky sauce on the side—dunk or drizzle, your pick."
