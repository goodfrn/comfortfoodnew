ingredients:
- "2 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs"
- "1 large onion diced"
- "4 garlic cloves minced"
- "1 tablespoon fresh ginger grated"
- "1 can (14 oz) coconut milk full fat"
- "1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes"
- "3 tablespoons curry powder"
- "1 teaspoon turmeric"
- "1 teaspoon cumin"
- "1 teaspoon paprika"
- "1 tablespoon salt"
- "1 teaspoon black pepper"
- "1 tablespoon olive oil"
- "Fresh cilantro for garnish"
- "Juice of half a lime"
instructions:
- "Toss the diced onion, garlic, and ginger into the slow cooker. Doesn't need to be perfect. Rough chop is fine."
- "Put the chicken thighs on top. Whole pieces, don't cut them up yet. They hold together better during the cook and you shred or chop after."
- "Pour the coconut milk and diced tomatoes over everything. Add curry powder, turmeric, cumin, paprika, salt, pepper. Give it one good stir. Enough to mix the spices through but don't stress about it being even."
- "Lid on. Low for 4 hours or high for 2 and a half. Low is better. The chicken gets softer and the sauce thickens more."
- "Pull the chicken out. It'll be falling apart already. Shred it with two forks on a plate or just chop it rough. Either way works."
- "Put the chicken back in. Stir it through the sauce. Squeeze the lime juice in. Taste it. Might need a pinch more salt depending on your coconut milk."
- "Serve over rice. White or basmati. Cilantro on top if you like it. Naan on the side for dipping into the sauce. That's the best part honestly."

tips:
- "Chicken thighs over breasts every time. Breasts get dry and stringy in a slow cooker. Thighs stay soft and pull apart clean. Bone-in adds flavor but boneless saves you the hassle. For this easy chicken curry either works."
- "Full fat coconut milk. The can should be thick when you open it. If it pours out like water grab a different brand. This is what makes a chicken curry with coconut milk actually creamy. Light coconut milk makes a thin sad sauce."
- "Three tablespoons of curry powder. Trust it. Sounds like too much but it's not after four hours on low. Add the turmeric and cumin on top of that. The color should be deep golden. If it looks pale, more powder. That's how to make curry chicken that actually tastes like curry."
- "Low heat, four hours. Don't open the lid to check. Every time you do, you add 20 minutes. The slow cooker does its thing. When the time's up, the chicken should shred with two forks no effort. Sauce should be thick and creamy. If it's thin, take the lid off and set to high for 15 minutes. Reduces fast."
- "Lime juice at the end. Not during cooking. Fresh cilantro same thing. Serve over rice or with naan. Leftovers in the fridge for three days. Freeze up to two months. Reheat on stove with a splash of water. This chicken coconut curry slow cooker recipe actually gets better the next day."
faq:
- "q: Can I use chicken breast instead? a: You can but it won't be as good. Breast dries out in a slow cooker even on low. If you have to use it, cut the time to 3 hours and check early. Thighs are the right choice for this slow cooker curry."
- "q: How do I make it spicier? a: Red chili flakes or a chopped fresh chili in with the onions. Half a teaspoon of cayenne works too. Add it at the start so it cooks through. Taste before serving and adjust."
- "q: What if my sauce is too thin? a: Lid off, high heat, 15 minutes. It reduces quick. Or mix a teaspoon of cornstarch with cold water and stir it in. Thickens in a minute. Usually full fat coconut milk handles it on its own though."
- "q: Can I make this in a crock pot? a: Same thing. This chicken curry crock pot recipe works exactly the same way. Low for 4 hours. Every machine runs a bit different so check the chicken at 3 and a half hours if yours runs hot."
- "q: What rice goes best? a: Basmati. Always basmati with curry. Jasmine works too. Something light and fluffy that soaks up the sauce. Brown rice if you want but it changes the feel of the meal."
- "q: Can I add vegetables? a: Potatoes, bell peppers, spinach all work. Potatoes go in at the start, cut small so they cook through. Spinach and peppers at the end, last 20 minutes. Keeps them from turning to mush. That's how you make a solid chicken and veg curry in the slow cooker."
