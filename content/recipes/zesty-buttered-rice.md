ingredients:
- "1 1/2 cups jasmine rice"
- "2 tablespoons olive oil"
- "1 1/2 cups low sodium chicken broth"
- "3 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice"
- "1 teaspoon lemon zest"
- "1 1/2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves"
- "1/2 teaspoon salt"
- "1/4 teaspoon black pepper"
instructions:
- "Medium saucepan on medium-high heat. Add olive oil and swirl. Wait till it shimmers, not smokes. Toss in jasmine rice, stirring quickly. Toast just until texture shifts, tiny pops maybe, no color change. About 1-2 minutes; smell should be nutty, not burnt."
- "Pour in chicken broth, lemon juice, zest, thyme, salt, pepper. Stir to combine, break up any clumps. Increase heat—watch carefully till bubbling vigorously across surface, not just edges. That's your boil. This step wakens rice to absorb flavors; keep stirring occasionally to prevent sticking, but no frenzy."
- "Cover immediately with a tight lid, reduce heat to lowest simmer possible. Almost no movement in broth’s surface—gentle bubbles only. Close lid silently—no peaks popping up. No peeking before 18 minutes. This trapped steam is rice’s cooking magic."
- "Check liquid by gently lifting the lid once simmer ends. Rice should look plump, liquid fully soaked in, grains tender with slight bite. If wet spots linger, cover and wait 3 more minutes off heat, letting residual warmth finish job."
- "Fluff rice lightly with a fork, breaking any stubborn clumps but avoiding mashing grains together. Fresh thyme leaves sprinkled on top for aroma and fresh impetus before serving."
- "Serve warm; lemon brightness with aromatic herb freshness hits best fresh. Leftovers reheat well with splash broth or olive oil to loosen grains."

faq:
- "q: Why no browning rice? a: Browning kills jasmine rice’s floral aroma. You want toasted texture but no color change. Burnt smell means bitter taste later. Toast briefly till you smell nutty but no darkening. Otherwise aroma gets lost."
- "q: Can I use bottled lemon juice? a: Bottled lemon juice often lacks the sharp acidity and fresh zing. It dulls flavor and flattens brightness. Fresh squeezed lemon juice zings with playful bitterness that lifts rice flavor. Use fresh always if possible."
- "q: Rice sticks to pan. What now? a: Oil shimmer first step. Stir rice fast when adding. Toasting activates surface starch but no sticking. If you see stuck bits, lower heat next time, stir gently but regularly at start. Always rinse rice well till water runs clear to reduce starch."
- "q: How to store leftovers? a: Cool rice fast first. Store in airtight container. Reheat with splash broth or olive oil to loosen grains or risk clumps. Microwave or stovetop both fine. Avoid freezing—texture changes and tends to become mushy when thawed."
