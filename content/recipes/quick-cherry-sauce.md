ingredients:
- "4 cups pitted fresh cherries, halved"
- "1 cup tupelo honey instead of sugar"
- "2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice"
- "a pinch fine sea salt"
- "1 tablespoon cornstarch"
- "water or cherry brandy for thinning"
instructions:
- "=== Heat and dissolve ==="
- "Dump cherries, honey, lemon juice, salt, and cornstarch in a heavy-bottom pot. Medium heat, steady bubble, not a boil. Stir gently but persistently; start hearing a faint pop-pop. Honey needs time to melt into a syrupy bath. Usually 5 minutes, but sensory checks are king here."
- "=== Watch and judge ==="
- "Simmer and stir, the sauce thickening slowly. Cherry type changes everything—some need a full 17 minutes, others just 8. Look for the sauce to cling to the spoon, not slide right off. Sticky, glossy, and slightly elastic. The surface tightens, ripples slow. Smell deepens, that tart cherry aroma punches the air. Color deepens from bright red to a darker ruby."
- "=== Finish and fix ==="
- "Take off heat when thick enough. If it stiffens too much—had that once—add a few teaspoons of water or cherry brandy, stir vigorously to loosen. Never add liquid on the heat, wait until off stove to avoid splitting."
- "=== Final stir-in ==="
- "If liqueur is on deck, fold it in now. Brandy, Kirsch, or even Chambord. Adds warmth and a little kick. Skips artificial flavoring, trust the real thing."
- "=== Serve & reflect ==="
- "Cool slightly to thicken more. Use on pancakes, ice cream, roast pork—don’t limit yourself. Got extra? Toss some in yogurt or oatmeal. Let me know your trials in comments or ratings. Every batch is a little different; embrace it."
