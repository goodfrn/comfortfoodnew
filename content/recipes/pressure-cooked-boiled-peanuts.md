ingredients:
- "4 pounds raw green peanuts"
- "3 tablespoons kosher salt"
- "1.5 tablespoons apple cider vinegar"
- "10 cups water (or enough to cover peanuts)"
- "1 teaspoon smoked paprika"
instructions:
- "===Rinse and Prep==="
- "Wash peanuts thoroughly in cool running water; gritty dirt can ruin the texture. Drain well or blot dry if you want less foaming later. No skimping."
- "===Season and Soak==="
- "Place peanuts in the pot. Sprinkle salt evenly; don’t just dump it. Pour apple cider vinegar—not that plain white stuff—to add subtle tang and break down skins."
- "Add smoked paprika for smoky depth. Covers the bitterness some peanuts have post cooking."
- "Pour water gradually, just until peanuts are submerged. Too much water = bland mush. Watch the max fill line carefully; the pot foams and bubbles big with peanuts."
- "===Seal and Cook==="
- "Secure lid, seal valve tightly. Set pressure cooker to high pressure for 63 minutes.  Do not rush to quick release; natural pressure release helps skins relax, keeps peanuts plump instead of shriveled."
- "===Release and Assess==="
- "Once pressure drops naturally, open carefully—steam bursts out hot, smell rich, earthy, salty. Peek at peanuts for softness. Should be tender but not falling apart. Skin wrinkles and sometimes peels slightly when done."
- "If too firm, reseal and cook more in 5-minute bursts. Texture clues > strictly clock times."
- "===Serve and Store==="
- "Drain if you want less salty broth or serve with some juice for intensely flavored snacking. Store leftovers in cling wrap or airtight; refrigerated peanuts spit up flavors next day even better."
- "===Tips and Substitutions==="
- "If green peanuts unavailable, dry raw unshelled need way longer soaking, more water, and cooking, but pressure cooker helps cut down time drastically."
- "Reducing salt? Try adding soy sauce or miso paste in broth for umami depth without over salting."
- "Extra vinegar? Bubbles intensify and skins pop off—you want that or not is personal. Play with acid balance for your palate."
- "Smoke paprika optional but with a history of smoked nuts, worth the extra punch."
- "If pressure cooker unknown to you, test with water only first to avoid surprises—peanuts tend to froth and can clog pressure valves if done wrong."
