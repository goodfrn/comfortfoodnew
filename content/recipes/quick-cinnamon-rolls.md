ingredients:
- "1 cup Simple Mills almond flour pancake mix"
- "3/4 cup oat milk (or any plant milk)"
- "3 tbsp softened butter"
- "1/3 cup brown sugar"
- "2 tsp ground cinnamon"
- "String or dental floss for clean cutting"
- "Cream cheese frosting (optional): 1/2 cup cream cheese softened, 2 tbsp powdered sugar, 1 tsp vanilla extract"
instructions:
- "Preheat oven 340-355 degrees F—aim for 350 but ovens fluctuate. Listen for the quiet buzz settling, a hint your oven’s ready."
- "Pour pancake mix into a bowl. Slowly mix in oat milk—a 3/4 cup starting point. Dough’s going to look shaggy but don’t panic. Feel the stickiness; too wet add a sprinkle of almond flour. Too dry, splash a little more milk. Knead gently on a floured surface. Use heel of hand, press down and stretch away, incorporate the dough to a homogenous ball. It should spring back if poked but not cling to fingers."
- "Press the dough into a long rectangle, roughly 9x12 inches. Not obsessively perfect, imperfections add charm. Spread butter thick; this is your richness layer. Then sprinkle cinnamon and brown sugar mix liberally across, use fingers—don’t scrape, spread with love."
- "Roll carefully but firm. A tight roll is your friend; loose rolls spill sugar during baking and get dry edges. Grab some unwaxed floss and slide it under the log, cross over itself, pull tight to slice clean pieces without squashing. Should yield around 8-9 rolls. Don’t panic if one’s misshapen, rustic wins here."
- "Coat your baking dish with non-stick spray or butter. Place rolls spaced but close enough to touch when risen. Oven time, 13-17 minutes. Watch for a golden dome. Slight crackle on top means the sugar is caramelizing, smells warm, toasty cinnamon filling the air. Don’t overbake or rolls dry out hard; softness matters."
- "Remove and let cool without skirts or napkins. Twenty minutes minimum. The butter and sugar settle; cutting too soon just a sticky mess. Meanwhile, if frosting, whip cream cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla till fluffy. Spread after cooling. Optional but gives a slight tang to the candy sweetness."
- "Serve warm, not hot. Texture is tender with just a slight chew. Keep leftovers wrapped; best day two reheated gently or cold for contrast."
