ingredients:
- "4 medium flour tortillas"
- "2 tablespoons vegetable oil"
- "2 tablespoons basil pesto"
- "6 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced"
- "3 tablespoons chopped sun-dried tomatoes packed in oil"
instructions:
- "Heat half tablespoon vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat; wait till it shimmers but not smoking."
- "Spread 1 tablespoon pesto on two tortillas; on the other two, layer mozzarella slices, then sun-dried tomatoes evenly."
- "Place cheese-topped tortilla, cheese side up, into the skillet; immediately top with a pesto-coated tortilla, pesto side down."
- "Listen for a gentle sizzle. Cook until bottom tortilla edges turn golden and cheese feels soft—not fully runny, around 2-3 minutes."
- "Flip carefully with a spatula to brown the other side; this takes less time, about 1-2 minutes. Cheese should bind but still slightly gooey."
- "Transfer to board and slice into fourths; watch cheese stretch but not leak excessively."
- "Add remaining oil to skillet, repeat process with leftover tortillas. Keep oil quantity minimal to prevent soggy crust."
- "If mozzarella feels too wet, pat dry with paper towel before layering. Use roasted red peppers in place of sun-dried tomatoes for mellow sweetness."
- "Avoid overheating oil; scorched oil ruins pesto flavor. If cheese escapes, plug the gaps immediately with a spatula to keep quesadilla intact."
- "Rest quesadillas briefly on cutting board—melting will finish internally while cooling slightly."
