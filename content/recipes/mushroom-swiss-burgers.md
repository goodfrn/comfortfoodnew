ingredients:
- "2 frozen beef burgers"
- "2 tablespoons unsalted butter"
- "1 cup cremini mushrooms sliced"
- "1 small shallot minced"
- "3/4 cup water"
- "1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce"
- "1 teaspoon maple syrup"
- "1 teaspoon Dijon mustard"
- "1/2 teaspoon dried thyme"
- "Salt and black pepper to taste"
- "4 slices Swiss cheese"
- "2 burger buns"
instructions:
- "Skillet on medium or grill to 350F. Burger goes in frozen. Don't thaw."
- "Leave it. No poking. No pressing. Three to seven minutes. Juices start pooling on top — that's your flip signal."
- "Flip. Four to seven minutes other side. Edges brown, feels firm but not hard. Pull it off. Let it rest on a plate."
- "Same pan. Butter in. Wait till it foams. Mushrooms and shallots in. Medium high. Don't touch much. Five six minutes till edges go golden and pan looks almost dry."
- "Water, Worcestershire, maple syrup, Dijon, thyme in. Stir. Salt and pepper. Cook till liquid coats the mushrooms thick and clingy. Not soupy. Set mushrooms aside."
- "Burgers back in the pan. Two slices Swiss on each. Lid on. Two three minutes. Watch it. Cheese goes shiny and gooey — done."
- "Bun. Burger. Mushrooms on top. Eat right away."
introduction: "This mushroom swiss burger came from a bad night with a dry patty and sad onions. Swapped the onions for shallots. Sharper, earthier, way better with the mushrooms. Brown sugar for maple syrup too — more depth, less flat sweet. That combo on a mushroom and swiss burger just works different. The frozen patty thing sounds wrong but it's actually better. Juice stays in. You get a real crust if you leave it alone and don't poke it. That crust is everything on a mushroom swiss cheeseburger — it's what holds up under the mushrooms and cheese. Cremini mushrooms not button. More flavor, caramelizes better. Cook them down till the pan is almost dry and the edges go golden. That's when the mushroom swiss is actually good. Cheese under the lid melts slow and gooey. Two three minutes, don't walk away. This swiss and mushroom burger recipe is done in under twenty minutes. Resting the patty after cooking is the one step most people skip. Don't. Dry burger is almost always a resting problem."

tips:
- "Don't thaw the patty. Frozen straight in the hot pan. Sounds wrong. Isn't. Juice stays locked in and you get a real crust. That crust is what makes this mushroom swiss burger recipe worth making."
- "Leave the patty alone. No poking, no pressing, no checking. Juices pool on top — that's when you flip. Press it and all that juice ends up in the pan not in your mushroom swiss hamburger."
- "Mushrooms need a dry pan to caramelize. If there's too much liquid they just steam and go grey. Medium high heat, stir twice max. Wait for golden edges and almost dry pan. That's when the mushroom and swiss flavor actually hits."
- "Maple syrup not brown sugar. It caramelizes different — more aroma, less flat. Combined with Dijon and Worcestershire it makes the sauce on this burger mushroom recipe taste like it took way longer than it did."
